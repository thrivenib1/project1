5 Management Responsibility

5.1 Management Commitment

The Management of Integra is committed to ensure the following:

  • Customer requirements are understood and met to enhance customer satisfaction.
  • Statutory and regulatory requirements are adhered.
  • The Quality Policy is understood, implemented and maintained at all levels of the organization.
  • Quality Policy will be disseminated through displaying it at key locations in the organization, awareness training, team briefing, audits and reviews.
  • Resource requirements are identified, adequate resources including assignment of trained personnel for management are provided, work and verification activities including self-quality assessment (internal quality audit) are performed.
  • Conducting management review.
  • Review of the quality objectives for adequacy and effectiveness at project and functional levels.

5.2 Customer Focus

Integra is a customer focused organization. All customer feedback, issues, customer satisfaction survey are discussed in the management review meeting. Necessary actions are generated to increase customer satisfaction.

5.2.1 General

The Management of Integra will ensure the following with the aim of achieving customer satisfaction:

  • Identify customer needs and expectations
  • Convert customer's needs and expectations into requirements
  • Review customer requirements
  • Amendments to contractual requirements are reviewed for implications
  • Meet the customers' needs, expectations, and requirements
  • Collect feedback from customers through customer satisfaction survey and take necessary corrective / preventive actions

5.3 Quality Policy

Integra is a provider of software products and services to customers in various parts of the world.

Integra's Quality Policy as stated by the Managing Director is:


   "Integra is firmly committed to business excellence in Software Development and Services by continually improving its quality system and meeting expectations of its internal and external customers."

The quality policy is endorsed by the Managing Director. The quality policy is reviewed during the Management Review meeting.

Integra’s management and employees are committed to assuring that this policy is implemented, understood and maintained at all levels of the organization.

All new employees are trained on QMS after joining the organization as part of induction training. The Quality Policy and Quality Objectives of the organization are available for reference by all employees through PCC (Intranet). Whenever there is any change in the policy or the objectives they are communicated to the employees through mail.

The Quality Policy and the Quality Objectives will be displayed in prominent locations of the premises.

5.4 Planning

5.4.1 Quality Objectives

Integra has established Quality Policy as defined in section 5.3. In line with the Quality Policy, Quality Objectives are defined at organizational level.

The Organizational Quality Objectives for the year 2017-18 are:

ü Limit Schedule Variance to 10% in software development & services.

ü Limit Effort Variance to 15% in software development & services.

ü Convert at least 10% of enquiries into orders.

ü Limit Escaped Defect Ratio to 5%.

ü Limit Project Cost Variance to 20%.

In line with the Quality objectives of the organization, operation and function level objectives are defined. These are published in PCC and updated regularly.

The Managing Director approves organizational objectives. The function level objectives are approved by the MR. The quality objectives are defied with the aim to meet requirements of the products, services and processes and most importantly the customer requirements.

5.4.2 Quality Management System Planning

The quality planning is carried out for the products / services by defining and documenting how the requirements of product quality are met. The Quality Management System Structure provides a framework for this.

Planning is done with the following considerations:

  • Achieve the quality objectives
  • Consider the processes that must be used to achieve objectives
  • Consider the resources that will be needed to achieve objectives
  • Integrity of the QMS is maintained when changes to QMS are planned and implemented

5.5.3 Management Representative

The Managing Director appoints the Management Representative for Quality Management System implementation. Mr. Bhaskar Jyoti Phukan has been appointed as Management Representative of IMSSPL and IMSPL with effect from 2nd November 2015.

The Management Representative is responsible for:

  • Ensuring that the processed needed for the Quality Management System is documented, implemented and maintained in accordance with ISO 9001:2008 standards.
  • Reporting to the MD, on the Quality System, for reviews as a basis for improvement of the QMS.
  • Ensuring continual process improvement.
  • Ensuring promotion of awareness for meeting customer requirements throughout the organization
  • Organizing and initiating third party audits for certification and surveillance
  • Ensuring training related to the Quality Management System for all employees
  • Planning, organizing and conducting internal quality audits
  • Ensuring that adequate documentation support is provided as per requirements
  • Ensuring the closure of NCs found in the audits.

    • The Management Representative has the authority to:

  • Approve Internal Auditors
  • Approve the Quality Management System and procedures.
  • Approve process and project champions
  • Conduct surprise audits

Internal communication is currently ensured through systems and procedural interface requirements. This is explained in the Information and Work Flow (FP17) procedure.

5.6 Management Review

5.6.1 General

The MR will call for management review meeting once six months or whenever necessary. The MD will chair the meeting and the participants will be MR, BU Heads, Marketing Head, Sales Head, HR Manager, Head Talent Transformation, and other invited members, as needed.

  • The objective of the review will be to evaluate:
  • The performance of the Quality System
  • The suitability of the Quality System
  • The adequacy of the Quality System
  • The effectiveness of the Quality System
  • Suitability of the Quality Policy and Objectives
  • Opportunities for improvement and the need for changes to the QMS including the quality policy and quality objectives

5.6.2 Review Input

Input for Management Review will include performance and improvement opportunities related to the following:

  • Follow-up actions from earlier management reviews.
  • Audit results.
  • Feedback from customers.
  • Product conformance data.
  • Process performance information.
  • Status of corrective and preventive actions.
  • Changes that might affect the QMS.
  • Feedback from employees.
  • Recommendation for improvement.

5.6.3 Review Output

Output for Management Review will include decisions and actions related to the following:

  • Actions to improve the Quality System and processes
  • Actions to improve the products related to customer requirements
  • Actions to address resource needs (including human resource, hardware, software and infrastructure)
