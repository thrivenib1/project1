7.0 Introdution

7.1.1 Purpose
This process defines the method used within Integra for performing Verification (VER). The purpose of the VER process area is to:
Ensure that the selected work products meet their specified requirements

7.1.2 Policy
The VER ensures that the verification of product and product components (services and their components) against all selected requirements and identification of corrective action.

7.1.3 Scope
This process is applicable to all work products.

7.1.4 Entry criteria

The basic criterion for initiating the VER process is:

  • Completion of any of the work products before being released to the intended users (internal or external)

7.3.2 Tasks
The steps of the VER process are described below:

7.3.2.1 Select Work Products for Verification (SP 1.1)

  • The PM will identify the work products for verification based on their contribution to meeting project objectives and requirements

The work products can be:

  • Document of understanding (DOU)
  • Technical Proposal.
  • User requirement specifications (URS).
  • Software requirement specifications (SRS).
  • Effort estimates.
  • High level design document.
  • Low level design document.
  • Source code.
  • Project Management Plan.
  • Configuration Management Plan.
  • Quality Assurance Plan.
  • Risk Management Plan.
  • Software test plan.
  • Software test specification.
  • Software test reports

PM will also identify the methods for the verification of the each of the products identified.
The methods of verification can be:

  • Offline review
  • Seminar (Online review)
  • Code walk through
  • Prototyping
  • Simulation
  • Modelling

7.3.2.2 Establish the Verification Environment (SP 1.2)

PM will identify and establish the verification environment to verify the work product
The verification environment can be:

  • Any specific tools required verification.
  • Simulators.
  • Interfaces with external systems

7.3.2.3 Establish Verification Procedures and Criteria (SP 1.3)

The PM will establish the verification procedures and criteria to ensure the products meet their requirements.
The criteria can be:

  • Product / Product Component requirements.
  • Standards.
  • Organizational Policies.
  • Test parameters.
  • Test data.
  • Proposals and agreements

7.3.2.4 Prepare for Peer reviews (SP 2.1)

  • PM will identify the key reviewers for the work products, prepare and update any material that will be used in the peer review such as check lists, templates, and guidelines.
  • PM will prepare the review schedule
  • PM will identify the entry and exit criteria for the work products
  • PM will define the criteria requiring another peer review
  • Peer review training material
  • Select the work products for peer review
  • Identify the goals for the peer review

7.3.2.5 Conduct peer reviews (SP 2.2)

Conduct peer review to identify and eliminate the defects in the early stages.
The peer review team will conduct the review with the following goals:

  • Identify and document defects and other issues in the work product.
  • Record the results of the peer review including the action items using FRVER01.
  • Collect peer review data.
  • Identify action items and communicate the issues to the relevant stake holders.
  • Conduct the additional peer review if there is a need.
  • Ensure the exit criteria for the peer review are satisfied

7.3.2.6 Analyze peer review data (SP 2.3)

Analyze the data which is the outcome of the peer review

Record the data related to the peer review in the form of IRR (FRVER01)
Analyze the peer review data for the following:

  • Number of defects versus number expected.
  • Types of defects detected.
  • Causes of defects.
  • Defect resolution impact

Protect the peer review data

7.3.2.7 Perform Verification (SP 3.1)

Perform verification to identify and remove the defects in the early stages.

  • Perform verification of the products against their requirements.
  • Record the verification results using (FRVER01).
    Identify action items based on the results of the verification

7.3.2.8 Analyze Verification results (SP 3.2)

Analyze Verification results to ensure the product meets the requirements.

  • Compare the actual results with the expected results.
  • Provide information on how the defects can be resolved and initiate the corrective action
