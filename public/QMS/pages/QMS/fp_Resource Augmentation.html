ISO Clause: 6.2 and 7.5 FP02 Version: 3.90 Date: 19th Oct, 2017

2.1 Introdution

2.1.1 Purpose
The software services in the IT industry are also provided in the form of consulting services which include resource augmentation (Time and Material, with or without delivery responsibility and preferably onsite at customer premises). The outsourced resource augmentation services have become a definite trend being followed by almost all medium to large size companies and the number of resource augmentation opportunities is growing at a steady pace. The resource augmentation services enable customers to not only focus and leverage on their core strength like product/ IP building, but also save on overheads such as recruitment, ramp-up/ down, training cost etc which is taken care by the partner company providing resources. Typically, resource augmentation engagements are in the areas of software requirement study, design, development, integration, testing, implementation/deployment, lifecycle maintenance, porting, migration, technical publication, localisation, project management and other related areas. Generally, the customers pass on such outsourcing opportunities to the partner companies and get these outsourced assignments done by taking software engineer(s) either onsite at their premises or offsite at partner companyâ€™s premises. All efforts are made to provide the services as per the agreed terms with the customer.

The purpose of this document is to define the processes for providing resource augmentation services to customers. The processes are meant to be followed by resource augmentation sales team (RAST) for various activities performed by them at Integra Micro Software Services (P) Ltd., Bangalore (IMSSPL).

2.1.2 Scope
These processes are applicable to the resource augmentation sales being provided by IMSSPL. This comprises of the activities, where software engineers from IMSSPL are deployed on customer projects which could be executed either at customer or IMSSPL premises. IMSSPL provides resource augmentation to companies, which are looking to better leverage technology towards their business goals. The goal of resource augmentation services is to provide software engineers to customers in various segments like VAS, mobile devices, mobility, database and system support services, Internet of things(IoT), consumer electronics, industrial automation and enterprise solutions including BPM with focus on migration and porting, lifecycle maintenance, technical writing, development and verification & validation etc. The services offered by IMSSPL through resource augmentation also include:

  • Requirement study
  • Project management

The generic skill set being supported by IMSSPL will be defined in the Business Unit Process.

2.3 RAB Sales Activities

2.3.1 Enquiry Generation

  • The RASH/ RASM/ RASC/ BU Heads and any other source gather information (verbal and/or written) from both existing and prospective customers, who may need resource augmentation services, pro-actively to increase business, exploring all possible contacts.
  • Wherever necessary, arrangement is made for the customer representative(s) to visit IMSSPLâ€™s facilities as part of rapport building exercise and for showcasing companyâ€™s capabilities.
  • RASH organizes presentations on IMSSPLâ€™s areas of expertise showcasing its relevant projects, in consultation with BU heads, and also organizes for demonstration of software developed by IMSSPL that might interest the customers/ prospects.
  • The RASH provides the projection of number of engineers to be made available for resource augmentation business based on business target worked out in the beginning of the financial year, on a quarterly/ six monthly/ yearly basis. Moreover, BU heads would explore all possible options of taking engineers from external world (direct recruitment and through partners) and/or parent company for specific requirements (mainly back-to-back), if a requirement is not serviceable with the available pool of engineers.
  • Enquiries are received through weekly/fortnightly/monthly tracker from customers and on daily basis through email and over telephone. In few cases, open and future enquiries are discussed in person with the customers during meetings.
  • The RASM and/ or RASC logs the enquiry into the EMS for all enquiries received and updates the same on a day-to-day and/or weekly basis to reflect the latest status. The same is also maintained in the resource augmentation enquiry tracker.
  • The RASC maintains a customer master database that includes information like address, contact information, and domain, etc of customers and software consulting engineers deployed at customer premises.

2.3.2New Accounts

  • New accounts initiated by the sales team are passed on to RAST for resource augmentation related sales after 1 year. In such scenarios, the initial formalities like NDA, monthly billing rates, MSA, etc would be handled by the respective sales team member. However, RAST also explores the market and makes efforts to open new accounts and submits proposals. MSA and SoW are maintained by the commercial division. However, the RAST would maintain scanned copy of the same for its reference.
  • The new account enquiry details are passed on to COO to decide on which BU to take it further.

2.3.4 Enquiry Qualification

  • The RAST discusses with the customers and/or prospects to understand the exact requirements, wherever required. The discussion could be either telephonic or in person and may also involve BU heads and COO, if required depending on requirements. The discussions are meant to understand the technical details, experience level, mandatory and desired skills, any specific domain, location of work, duration and expectations of customers. The first level of filtering is done by RASH based on focus areas, domains, skills etc. Thereafter, the enquiry if qualified by RASH will be forwarded to the COO for assigning to a specific BU.
  • The respective Business unit head decides on dropping enquiries based on focus areas, bench status, and other business-related issues.
  • The status is updated in the weekly enquiry tracker which is shared with all stakeholders.

2.3.5 Enquiry Processing

  • The RAST forwards the resumes to the concerned officials of the customer for consideration. In case of back-to-back (B2B), the same is informed to the customer clearly with the approximate joining time involved.
  • Based on the customerâ€™s feedback, the short-listed engineers are informed by the RAST about the next stage of selection process.

2.3.6 Selection

  • The RASC interfaces with the customer for scheduling interviews for short-listed engineers who are also given required briefing and other relevant information by BU head.
  • The proposed software engineers, after the interview, prepare interview transcripts (FR-RAB8) to the extent possible and gives it to the RAST for maintaining the same for future reference.
  • The RAST does post-interview follow-up with the customer for detailed feedback on engineers interviewed, especially who were not found suitable. And the feedback received is also shared with the BU heads and respective engineers.
  • In case a proposed engineer is not short-listed, the reasons for the same are identified in consultation with the customers, wherever possible and shared with the BU heads for further analysis and corrective action. Engineers not found suitable by the customer continue their assignments with the BU Heads.

2.4 Deployment

2.4.1 Return Requests

  • At times, return requests are received from software engineers who are deployed on customer projects, who are looking for a change from their current assignment. Based on their requests, the matter is discussed first with BU and then with customer by RAST and processed based on decision taken by the BU head.
  • If it is decided that the engineer can be brought back and utilised for other projects/roles, then the same is communicated to the customer and relieving date is worked out. A suitable replacement is provided, if asked for by the customer with an overlap of two weeks for smooth transition of knowledge.
  • Once the transition is over, these engineers are moved to respective BU for redeployment purposes.

2.4.2 Revenue Loss
Revenue loss can happen in the following ways. However, the efforts are made to deploy more number of engineers to mitigate the risk of business loss due to the following. Moreover, all possible options are explored by the BU heads in consultation with RAST to minimize attrition to the extent possible.

  • Attrition: A deployed engineer resigns and IMSSPL is unable to provide a replacement for the same during the given time frame or if the customer decides not to take a replacement due to other business reasons. In such cases, anything above the industry standard of 20 % will be considered as a business loss.
  • Team Downsizing: The customer decides to send the engineers back for various reasons like business, restructuring, vendor consolidation, project closure/fore closure, customer getting acquired, etc by giving notice as per agreed terms.
  • Performance and Conduct: A deployed engineer is also sent back in case the customer finds his/her performance not up to the mark or if found to have any behavioural issues which do not conform to their business ethics or standards.
  • Leave: Engineers also return at times due to medical reasons like accident, ailments requiring long/hospitalised treatments or availing of maternity leave. In all these cases replacements are provided, wherever asked for, with overlap of two weeks at no extra cost to the customer during knowledge transition period.

2.4.3 Customer Complaints

In case of any customer complaint, the issue is logged using issue and escalation log FRPMC04 and the same is addressed by corresponding stakeholders and tracked until closure by the RAST.

2.5 Status Reporting

2.5.1 Status Report for the Management

  • The dash board is prepared by the MIS division based on weekly reports on resource augmentation deployment and enquiry status and shared with the management along with other important information related to target, revenue, etc along with other stakeholders on weekly/fortnightly/monthly/quarterly basis.
  • The review of business is also done with the top management as and when required.

2.5.2 Customer Satisfaction Survey (CSS)

  • The RAST shares the list of current customers with SQA for enabling the same.
  • The customer feedback, if collected is further analysed by SQA and the BU heads. Areas for improvements are identified and action items are worked out in consultation with the management and thereafter, the same is shared with the customer.

2.5.3 Resource Augmentation Sales Reports

RAST maintains the following two important reports related to resource augmentation sales:

  • Resource augmentation deployment status
  • Resource augmentation enquiry status

The RAST maintains reports related to resource augmentation customers and deployed software engineers. The resource augmentation master consists of the following information:

  • Deployment status
  • Deployed software engineerâ€™s status
  • Projected revenue status report
  • Attrition summary
  • Billing and invoicing information
  • Leave details
  • Award and appreciation details, if any
  • Onsite travel details, if any
  • Training details, if any
  • Details of customers
  • Customer complaint log, if any
  • Purchase order status

RAST maintains the entire data and reports year/month wise in a tree structure as defined in FRSAL5 with multiple sub folders that is designed to capture relevant information in respective folders. However, the tree structure is fine-tuned as per business needs and to simplify data capturing and extraction whenever required.

2.5.4 Backup Management

  • The complete tree structure of resource augmentation sales data /folders is backed up on external drive every month in the second week of following month.
  • RAB Sales team member will maintain the folder tree structure for maintaining transactional files and records will be maintained with the sales head in a secondary backup device.
  • This process is adapted considering the amount of transactions being handled by RAST in the form of mails from all stakeholders including customers every day.
  • RA sales data is transaction based and hence, large numbers of files are created/ modified on daily basis. Since the Doc-Mgr (open source document management tool) and the SVN do not have option of uploading multiple files in one go, it was decided to back up the data on external drive to save time.
  • As a part of disaster recovery, quarterly backup of data is given on DVD to the system admin which is kept under safe custody in other office of IMSSPL.
