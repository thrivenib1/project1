Project Monitoring and Control
ISO Clause: 7.3.1 PR03 Version: 3.00 Date: 17th May 2010

3.0 Introdution

3.1.1 Purpose
The purpose of Project Monitoring and Control is to track project progress, take corrective and preventive actions when the performance deviates from the target, integrate relevant stakeholders, form and sustain an integrated team for the development of work products

3.1.2 Policy
The organization is committed to using the best practices of Project management for monitoring and controlling projects work products to satisfy Customer requirements.

3.1.3 Scope
The monitoring and controlling of the project.

3.1.4 Entry criteria
The basic criteria for initiating the PMC process are:

  • Contract Sign-off, Project kick-off.
  • Established project plan (e.g. PMP, CoMP, CMP, QMP, RMP) with Milestones

3.3.2 Tasks
The steps of the PMC process are described below:

3.3.2.1 Monitor project planning parameters

  • Monitor the actual values of the project planning parameters against the project plan which includes cost, effort, schedule, resource.
  • PM will monitor the project, conduct reviews and update project plan.
  • PM will monitor the skill/knowledge levels as per plan.

3.3.2.2 Monitor commitment

  • Monitor/Review commitment of internal and external stake holder as listed in the project charter.
  • Review external and internal commitments every week in the internal and external stakeholder meeting. Review commitments that have not been satisfied and commitment having high risk of not being satisfied.
  • Document the results of the commitment review in the weekly report.

3.3.2.3 Monitor Project Risks

  • Revise the risks document when new risk is identified, when risks mitigates, when risks are retired and when project circumstances change significantly.
  • Project Manager will follow the risk management process/PA
  • Project team members will report potential risks
  • Review the risks during weekly team meetings.
  • Review risk management status with stakeholders during formal progress review meeting held monthly or on the planned event date or planned milestone date.

3.3.2.4 Monitor Data Management

  • Changes to approved documents will be initiated based on the approved change requests. The changes to the document will be reviewed and approved before execution.
  • Review and update data management activities as defined in the project plan.
  • Identify and document significant issues and the impact.
  • Document the results of data management activity review in Configuration audit report.

3.3.2.5 Monitor Stakeholder Involvement

  • Review the involvement of the stakeholders as required in specific project activities. PM documents the issues, impacts and results of the stakeholder involvement progress review in issue list.
  • Review the work products with the stakeholder to confirm if it is meeting the requirements of the stakeholder. PM document the recommendations and actions to resolve problems in the product or deliverable requirements and design.

3.3.2.6 Conduct progress review

  • Project Manager and Delivery Head will periodically review the project progress, performance and issues.
  • Monitor the actual values of the project planning parameters against the project plan.
  • Every week, the Project Manager will monitor tasks. Document the results of the review meeting in the weekly status report. And track all action items to closure.
  • Weekly status report will be sent to the Delivery Head.

3.3.2.7 Conduct Milestone review

  • Review the accomplishments and results of the project at selected project milestones.
  • On reaching milestone, event, or date for review, the Project Manager identifies information that needs to be prepared and/or presented. Document the results, action-items and decisions of the review meeting in MoM and track all action items to closure.

3.3.2.8 Analyse Issues

  • Project Manager will collect and analyze the issues and determine the corrective actions necessary to address the issues. Issue resolution meeting is conducted every forth night or as defined in PMP.
  • Project manager will resolve coordination issues with relevant stakeholders. Take corrective action on identified issues. Manage corrective actions to closure and document in the Issue log.
  • The PM will identify preventive actions and document them.

3.3.2.9 Take and manage corrective actions

  • PM will review and agree upon the corrective and preventive actions to be taken with stakeholders.
  • Communicate status of issues and resolve issues with the relevant stakeholders.
  • Monitor and track corrective actions to completion.
  • Update the issue log to capture the completion of corrective action and effectiveness of corrective actions. Track issues to closure.
