Requirements Development
ISO Clause: 7.3.2 PR05 Version: 3.00 Date: 17th May 2010

5.0 Introdution

5.1.1 Purpose
Produce and analyze customer, product and product-component requirements.

5.1.2 Policy
To provide a simple and seamless method of understanding and capturing the project’s requirements, thereby maintain uniformity across all stakeholders’ expectations with the usage of appropriate expertise from humans and software tools.

5.1.3 Scope
The scope of requirements development process includes the following activities:

  • Elicitation, analysis, validation, and communication of customer needs, expectations, and constraints to obtain customer requirements that constitute an understanding of what will satisfy stakeholders.
  • Collection and coordination of stakeholder needs.
  • Development of the lifecycle requirements of the product.
  • Establishment of the customer requirements.
  • Establishment of initial product and product component requirements consistent with customer requirements.

5.1.4 Entry criteria

  • This process area addresses all possible areas of gathering requirements and documenting them. It could happen at the beginning of a project once a contract is signed or during the project life cycle.
  • A Signed contract or Purchase order from the customer can form a good starting point.
  • In the absence of the above, customer provided specification document in the form of a User Requirement Specification (URS) or Statement of Work should be available.
  • Gap Analysis Report (GAR) of the URS, if any will be useful.
  • During the project a Change Request (CR) could affect the requirements and will have to follow the processes laid down in this PA.

5.3.2 Tasks
The steps of the Requirements Development process are described below:

5.3.2.1 Elicit Needs (SP 1.1)

  • Identify and list all possible inputs for this process to begin. They could be SOW, PMP, SSR, or even a CR.

To capture the information, have discussions with the development team and also with the customer (if necessary) to understand the expectations, clarify the specifications and arrive at testing requirements.

5.3.2.2 Develop the customer requirements (SP 1.2)

  • Prepare the Document of Understanding (DOU) based on the information captured as mentioned above. Use the template TPREQ01 to develop this document.
  • Carry out a peer review of the DOU and update for accepted changes.
  • Follow the Verification process (PR07) while carrying out the reviews and recording the comments.

5.3.2.3 Establish product and product component requirements (SP 2.1), Allocate product component requirements (SP 2.2)

  • Proceed to the preparation of the SRS using the inputs gathered so far.
  • Classify the requirements as functional and non-functional requirements.
  • Capture this information under the relevant sections of the SRS document. Use template TPRQD02 for the same.

5.3.2.4 Identify interface requirements (SP 2.3)

  • Categorize the various types of interfaces of the product as software, hardware, external, user interfaces and communication.

5.3.2.5 Establish operation concepts and scenarios (SP 3.1), establish a definition of required functionality (SP 3.2)

  • Arrive at the functional architecture of the software.
  • Represent the requirements diagrammatically using various techniques such as Use case diagrams , Activity Diagrams,
  • Create Use Case Diagrams for the requirements, interfaces and outputs. Include them in the SRS document.

5.3.2.6 Analyze requirements (SP 3.3)

  • Review the requirements to check if the requirements gathered from various sources have been mentioned in the SRS document. This should ideally be carried out by another Project Manager within the organization.
  • Some basis for review could be
  • Qualification of the requirement.
  • Clarity of the requirements.
  • Categorization of critical and non-critical components of the requirements.
  • Sequencing of activities based on their criticality.
  • Remove data duplication.
  • Provide corrections and ensure the changes are affected in the SRS document.

5.3.2.7 Validate requirements (SP 3.5)

  • In order to confirm that there are no requirements slipping through the cracks, all requirements will have to be validated. This could be performed by demonstrating prototypes or simulation of the software under development.

5.3.2.8 Analyze requirements to achieve balance (SP 3.4)

  • Make assessment of the requirements mapping to every stakeholder. Use various methods such as simulations and prototyping to perform this activity.
