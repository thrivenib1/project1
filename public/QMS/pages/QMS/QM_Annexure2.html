Glossary
QM-A02 Version: 3.00 Date: 17th May 2010
Expression Description
Acceptance Formal signing off of the delivered product by the customer after doing acceptance testing. This is formally recorded in a sign-off letter duly signed by the relevant authority.
Acceptance Criteria The set of test conditions used as a part of acceptance testing that the delivered product satisfies in order to be deemed as satisfying the product requirements.
Acceptance Testing Testing of the delivered product performed by the customer to ensure conformance to product requirements.
Approved Recorded decision that the product or part of the product verified has satisfied the quality requirements.
Assessment An exercise which is conducted in Interview mode using questionnaires to evaluate the process compliance in a project / organization.
Audit Systematic and independent examinations that are planned and executed using written procedures and checklists to determine if an organization is meeting the requirements and organization’s quality objectives.
Audit ability The ease with which conformance to standards can be checked
Auditee The organization, which is being audited.
Auditor / Assessor A person who has the qualification to perform quality audits.
Author The creator of a product or a product item.
Authority A right given or delegated to a person to approve a product or a product item.
Baseline A reference point at which the status of a product or a product items is established and recorded so that its growth or decline can be tracked.
Build A building block of a product that incorporates a subset of the capabilities of the entire product of which it is a part.
Quality Management System The organizational structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes and resources for implementing quality management necessary to achieve the quality objectives stated in the quality policy.
Certificate of Conformity A document signed by a qualified party confirming that, at the time of assessment, the product or service met the stated requirements.
Certification A recognized qualification granted to an organization by a registrar testifying to the organization’s capability to manage their quality system.
Certification Body An impartial body, Government or non-governmental, possessing the necessary competence and reliability to operate a certification system and in which the interests of all parties concerned with the functioning of the system are represented.
Clause A major section in the ISO 9001 standard.
Client Synonymous with customer.
Compatibility Ability of entities to be used together under specific conditions to fulfil relevant requirements.
Completeness The degree to which complete implementation of a required function has to be achieved.
Compliance An indication or judgement that the product or service meets the requirements of the relevant specification..
Conciseness The compactness of program in terms of lines of code.
Configuration A product’s constituent components along with the specific versions of the components.
Configuration Control An element of configuration management, consisting of the evaluation, coordination, approval or disapproval, and implementation of changes to configuration items after formal establishment of their configuration identification.
Configuration Item A product entity, which is hardware, software, and data or documents which, has the ability to evolve or decline in status during the course of product development.
Configuration Management A discipline applying technical and administrative direction and surveillance to identify and document the functional and physical characteristics of a configuration item, control changes to those characteristics, record and report change processing and implementation status, and verify compliance with specified requirements [IEEE-STD-610].
Configuration Management Plan A document setting out the organization and procedures for the configuration management of a specific product or project.
Conformity Fulfillment of specified requirements.
Consistency The use of uniform design and documentation techniques throughout the software development project.
Continuous Improvement A discipline to purposefully improve processes, procedures, and practices used in developing and delivering products such that productivity, quality, cycle time, and costs are incrementally and continuously evolved to meet changing business and customer needs.
Contract Agreed requirements between Integra and the customer transmitted by any means [ISO 9001].
Contract Management Systematic activities carried out by the supplier before signing the contract to ensure that requirements for quality are adequately defined, free from ambiguity, documented and can be realized by the supplier.
Correctness The extent to which a program satisfies its specification and fulfills the customer’s mission objectives.
Customer The individual or organization who is serviced by Integra by way of delivering a product and who pays Integra on satisfactory rendering of that service.
Customer Complaint Any dissatisfaction expressed orally or in writing by the client on the product or service offered.
Customer Requirements The specification of the functional, operational and performance characteristics of a product as desired by the customer.
Defect Non-fulfillment of an intended usage requirement or reasonable expectation, including one concerned with safety. This is referred to as nonconformance in a product.
Deliverable Product item, which could be software, data, documents to be delivered to the client as per contract.
Dependability Collective term used to describe the availability performance and its influencing factors: reliability performance, maintainability performance and maintenance-support performance.
Design The process of applying various techniques and principles for the purpose of defining a device, a process, or a system in sufficient detail as to permit its physical realization.
Design Review Documented, comprehensive and systematic examination of a design to evaluate its capability to fulfill the requirements for quality, identify problems, if any, and propose the development of solutions.
Development The process of converting the design into a usable application, which consists of hardware, software, data and documentation components.
Efficiency The amount of computing resources and code required by a program to perform its function..
Error Non-fulfillment of an intended usage requirement or reasonable expectation, including one concerned with safety.
Execution Efficiency The runtime performance of a program.
Expandability The degree to which architectural, data, or procedural design can be extended.
First Party Audit An audit where the supplier has its own organization staff performing the audit.
Flexibility The effort required for modifying an operational program.
Functional Manager A manager who manages a team and its work in a specific functional area.
Functionality Is assessed by evaluating the feature set and capabilities of the program, the generality of the functions that are delivered, and the security of the overall system.
Hardware Independence The degree to which the software is de-coupled from the hardware on which it operates.
Inspection Activity such as measuring, examining, testing or gauging one or more characteristics of an entity and comparing the results with specified requirements in order to establish whether conformity is achieved for each characteristic.
Instrumentation The degree to which program monitors its own operation and identifies errors that do occur.
Integrity The extent to which the access of software or data by unauthorized person can be controlled.
Interchangeability Ability of an entity to be used in place of another, without modification, to fulfill the same requirements.
Interoperability The effort required to couple one system with another.
ISO 9001 Model for quality assurance in design, development, production, installation and servicing.
Lifecycle The integrated set of activities involving analysis, design, coding, implementation, testing and maintenance of software.
Maintainability The effort required in locating and fixing an error in a program.
Major Non-conformance A failure of the quality management system or the requirements defined in a clause or sub-clause in ISO 9001 not being performed as defined in the supplier’s quality management system.
Management Review Formal evaluation by top management of the status and adequacy of the quality system in relation to quality policy and objectives.
Manager An individual responsible for satisfactory completion of an assigned or delegated function.
Metrics Statistical data collected about productivity and quality of software.
Minor Non-conformance A weakness in the performance of the quality management system other than major non-conformance usually can be resolved quickly by the audited organization.
Modularity Functional independence of program documents.
Non-conformity Non-fulfillment of a specified requirement.
Operability The ease of operation of a program.
Performance Is measured by processing speed, response time, resource consumption, throughput and efficiency.
Phase Defined segment of work.
Portability The effort required in transferring the program from one hardware and /or software system environment to another.
Preliminary Audit An audit prior to a third-party audit used by an organization to determine readiness for registration.
Problem A discovered defect in the product or process.
Procedure Specified way to perform an activity.
Process Set of inter-related resources and activities, which transform inputs into output.
Product Result of activities or processes.
Product Audit The examination of the product to determine whether or not it meets the requirements of the established product specification.
Product specification A formal document recording the customer’s expectation of the function and performance of the software application.
Project Any software development or consulting service provided to the customer.
Project Delivery Aggregate of activities involved in servicing a customer right from the inception stage of submission of tender or proposal up to the implementation and sign off of the product.
Project Member An individual reporting to the project leader directly or indirectly contributing to the developed product.
Proposal A response to a tender submitted by Integra-India to the customer detailing the technical and commercial considerations for the project.
Purchaser The individual or organization that contracts to the supplier for a product.
Quality Conformance to explicitly stated functional and performance requirements; Fitness to use; the totality of features and characteristics of a product or service that bears on its ability to satisfy a given need.
Quality Assurance All planned and systematic activities implemented within the quality system and demonstrated as needed, to provide adequate confidence that an entity will fulfil requirements for quality.
Quality Audit Systematic and independent examination to determine whether quality activities are related results comply with planned arrangements whether these arrangements are implemented effectively and are suitable to achieve objectives.
Quality Auditor Person who is qualified to perform quality audits.
Quality Control Operational techniques and activities that are used to fulfill requirements of quality.
Quality Inspection The process of measuring, examining, testing, gauging or otherwise comparing the item with the applicable requirements.
Quality Manual Document stating the quality policy and describing the quality system of an organization.
Quality Plan Document setting out the specific quality practices, resources and sequence of activities relevant to a particular product, project or contract.
Quality Policy Overall intentions and direction of the organization with regard to quality as formally expressed by top management.
Quality System The organizational structure, responsibilities, procedures, processes and resources needed to implement quality management.
Record Document that furnishes objective evidence of activities performed or results achieved.
Registrar A national body that can have issued a registration of certification stating that a third party audit has demonstrated the satisfaction of the ISO 9001 requirements by the designated organization audited.
Registration A recognized qualification granted to an organization by a registrar testifying to the organization’s capability to manage their quality system.
Reliability The extent to which a program can be expected to perform its intended function with required precision.
Requirements Specification A formal document recording the customer’s expectation of the function and performance of the software application.
Reusability The extent to which a program can be reused in other applications – related to the packaging and scope of the functions that the program performs.
Rework Action taken on a nonconforming product.
Risk Analysis Analysis of alternatives and identification /resolution of risk.
Scope The coverage taken during the audit.
Second Party Audit An audit requested by someone other than the supplier on the supplier.
Security The availability of mechanisms that control or protect programs.
Self-documentation The degree to which the source codes provide meaningful documentation.
Service What the purchaser or customer receives, uses and perceives.
Sign Off A recorded statement from the purchaser of a product or service to the supplier stating the acceptance of delivered product or service in its documented status.
Simplicity The degree to which a program can be understood without difficulty.
Software Intellectual creation comprising the programs, procedures, rules, and any associated documentation pertaining to the data processing system.
Software Item An identifiable part of the software product at an intermediate step or at the final step of development.
Software Product Complete set of computer programs, procedures, and associated documentation and data for delivery to a user.
Software System Independence The degree to which the program is independent of nonstandard programming language features, operating system characteristics, and other environmental constraints.
SQA Software Quality Assurance.
Subcontractor An individual or organization that provides a product or service to a supplier.
Supplier An organization that supplies a software product to a customer.
Supportability Combines the ability to extend the program, adaptability and serviceability in addition to testability and compatibility.
Surveillance Audit Continuous monitoring and verification of a registered organization to ensure it continues to meet ISO 9001 requirements.
System Audit The comparison of the system operated by a company with the documented procedures.
Test An examination to verify and /or validate that a software solution has been produced correctly and meets requirements.
Test Cases Document listing the input test conditions for which the concerned software item should be tested.
Test Plan Document listing the features to be tested for the concerned software item.
Testability The effort required in testing a program to ensure that it performs its intended function.
Testing Performance of defined tests.
Third Party Audit An audit to determine if an organization is qualified to be registered using ISO9001 requirements as the basis of comparison.
Traceability The ability to trace a design representation or actual program component back to requirements.
Training The degree to which software assists in enabling new users to apply the system.
Usability The effort required in learning, operating, preparing the input and interpreting the output of a program.
Validate To prove that the right product is built.
Validation Confirmation by examination and provision of objective evidence that the particular requirements for a specific intended use are fulfilled.
Verification Confirmation by examination and provision of objective evidence that specified requirements have been fulfilled.
Verify To prove that a product is built.
Work Instruction A document containing details of how to do something. The description should be so simple that any two people with the same qualification would achieve the same nominal results.
Work Product An interim or partial solution of the final product that is delivered to the customer, for example design, code prior to integration.
