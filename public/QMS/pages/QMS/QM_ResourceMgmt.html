6 Resource Management

6.1 Provision of Resources

6.1.1 Policy

Integra determines and provides resources needed.

· To establish and maintain the Quality Management System and continually improve its effectiveness.

· To enhance customer satisfaction by meeting customer requirements.

6.2 Human Resources

6.2.1 General

The Managing Director will be responsible for identifying and providing adequate resources for management, performance of work and verification activities including internal quality audits.

· The assignment of the persons to various roles will be done by the top management/ Group Heads.

· A single individual may be assigned to more than one role.

Integra selects and assigns personnel to various roles are competent on the basis of applicable education, training, skills and experience. The human resources recruitment, allocation and management are practiced as explained in the operational procedures Recruitment procedure (FP04) and Personnel Management (FP03) and Infrastructure and HR Request and Release procedure (PR11) ..

6.2.2 Competence, Awareness and Training

Integra will ensure that training needs are identified and training is provided for all the personnel performing activities affecting quality. Personnel performing specific assigned tasks will be qualified on the basis of appropriate education, training and/or experience, as required. The Training department will obtain feedback on the training imparted and analyze the data for improving the training effectiveness. Refer to operational Talent Management Procedure FP05 for more details.

Integra has established and maintains procedures to:

· Determine competency and training needs.

· Provide training to address identified needs.

· Evaluate the effectiveness of training at defined intervals.

· maintain appropriate records of education, training, skills and experience

Integra has established and maintains procedures to make employees at each relevant function and level aware of:

· The importance of conformance with the quality policy, and with the requirements of the quality management system.

· The significant impact of their work activities on quality, actual or potential.

· The benefits of improved performance.

· Their roles and responsibilities in achieving conformance with the quality policy and procedures and with the requirements of the quality management system.

· The potential consequences of departure from specified procedures.

6.3 Infrastructure

Integra defines, provides and maintains the infrastructure needed to ensure conformance of a product through the planning process. Consideration of these factors is identified during the product and process planning stage. Hardware and Software planning is done as per operational procedure FP16 Infrastructure planning

These include:

· Hardware and software.

· Suitable maintenance.

· Support services.

6.4 Work Environment

Integra has defined and implemented the human and physical factors of the work environment needed to achieve conformity to product and service requirements. Consideration of these factors is identified during the product and process planning stage. Workspace is planned as per operational procedure FP06, General Administration. Safety and security of infrastructure and of employees is ensured using operational procedure FP07, Security Management.

These include:

· Workspace and associated facilities.

· Ambient working conditions.

· Work ethics.

· Work methods.

· Health and safety conditions.
