8 Measurement, analysis and improvement

8.1 General

Integra has defined, planned and implemented measurement, monitoring, analysis and improvement processes to ensure:

· The products conform to requirements

· Conformity to the Quality Management System,

· Continual improvement and effectiveness of the QMS

The Quality Objective will be measured every quarter and discussed in the management review meeting.

· The measurement will cover all projects and functional areas.

· The effectiveness of measures implemented is periodically evaluated.

· Integra identifies and uses appropriate statistical tools for analysis of data.

· The results of data analysis and improvement activities are the input for the management review process.

8.2 Monitoring and measurement

8.2.1 Customer Satisfaction

8.2.1.1 Policy

Integra monitors information on customer satisfaction and dissatisfaction. The methods and measures for obtaining customer satisfaction information and the nature and the frequency of reviews are defined in the section below.

Actions will be taken based on the findings of Customer Satisfaction Survey.

8.2.1.2 Procedure

· The Manager Quality will conduct customer satisfaction survey.

· The survey will be done at least once in six months or at the end of the project delivery or more frequently on a need basis as decided by the management.

· The Manager - Quality will send the Customer Satisfaction Survey Form to the concerned customer representative and obtain the feedback.

· Customer Satisfactory Survey data is analyzed and presented for Management Review.

· The MR will share the feedback with relevant stakeholders.

· The MR is responsible for ensuring that the collected customer satisfaction data is appropriately tracked and maintained for all the customers.

· Customer satisfaction data serves as a means to assess the overall performance and continual improvement of the quality management system at Integra.

8.2.2 Internal Audit

8.2.2.1 Policy

· Determine whether the QMS has been effectively implemented and maintained.

· Identify potential opportunities for improvement.

· Verify conformance to ISO 9001:2008 standard.

8.2.2.2 Procedure

Integra's audit process, including the schedule, is based on the status and importance of the activities, areas or items to be audited, and the results of previous audits. The areas that will be covered through internal quality audit include (a) projects, (b) functions viz. Quality Management, Training, System Administration, Personnel Management, Recruitment, Business Development, Resource Augmentation Business, Technical Documentation and Purchase, (c) MR and (d) Top Management.

The internal quality audits and assessments will be carried out by personnel independent of those having direct responsibility for the activity being carried out.

The results of the audits and assessments will be recorded and brought to the attention of the personnel having responsibility in the area audited. The audit results will be used to identify areas for improvement and good practices. The projects / support functions will be graded as A, B, C or D according to the audit rating.

The management responsible for the area audited will take timely corrective and preventive actions to eliminate detected problems and their causes. Follow-up audit activities will verify and record the implementation and effectiveness of corrective and preventive actions taken.

· The Internal Audit will be carried out as per the Internal Audit (MP04) procedure.

· Records and the results of an audit will be maintained as per the Control of Records PR- procedure.

· The Management Representative will be responsible for planning and organizing internal quality audits.

· The Auditor will prepare non-conformance report, if any non-conformance is found in the area of audit.

· The Auditee will be responsible for closing the Non-conformance Report.

· The Manager-Quality will prepare the Audit Report Summary at the end of the audit.

· The Manager-Quality will track all open Non-conformance Reports for closure.

· The Internal Audit findings will be discussed in the Review Meetings as per the Management Review (FP15) procedure.

8.2.3 Monitoring and Measurement of Processes

8.2.3.1 Policy

Integra will apply suitable methods for measurement and monitoring of processes necessary to meet customer requirements and to demonstrate the continuing ability of processes to satisfy its intended purpose.

Processes with quantified objectives will be identified at various levels of the organization.

Measurement results will be used to identify correction and corrective actions to improve those processes.

8.2.3.2 Procedure

Identification, measurement and monitoring of processes are carried out as per the Measurement and Analysis (FP11) procedure. Process parameters will be analyzed using statistical techniques to enable identifying corrective and preventive action.

The status of identified processes will be discussed in the management review meeting. The responsibility to identify and apply suitable methods for monitoring and measurement of processes is assumed by functional managers, and is performed according to Measurement, Analysis and Improvement process.

· Monitoring and, where applicable, measurement activities are performed on the quality management system processes necessary to meet customer requirements and track quality objectives and to additional processes where the potential benefit is identified.

· Monitoring and measurement of processes demonstrate the ability of the processes to achieve planned results. When planned results are not achieved, action is taken to correct the immediate problem and, where appropriate, corrective action is initiated to eliminate the root cause of the problem and ensure conformity of product.

8.2.4 Monitoring and Measurement of Product

8.2.4.1 Policy

Integra will establish a procedure for measurement and monitoring of the characteristics of the product to verify that requirements for the product are met.

Evidence of implementation of required measurement and monitoring, and conformance with the acceptance criteria used are recorded. Records indicate the authority responsible for release of the product to the customer.

Product or service is not dispatched to the customer until all the specified activities have been satisfactorily completed and the related documentation is available and authorized. The only exception is when the product is released with full traceability through end use, thus allowing for positive recall.

8.2.4.2 Procedure

8.2.4.2.1 General

Integra applies suitable methods for documenting and reviewing test plan for different types of testing such as Unit, Integration, System and Acceptance Tests. Testing will be carried out as per test plan covering test objectives, test coverage, independence of tester, tools, environment, test conditions and test data, reviews and approvals, security and safety, refer to Verification procedure (PR07).

8.2.4.2.2 Receiving Inspection and Testing

The hardware and software products including the data supplied by the customer and third party will be verified upon receipt to see whether they meet the requirements.

8.2.4.2.3 In-process Inspection and Testing

The inspection and testing are carried out as per the Quality Plan.

8.2.4.2.4 Final Inspection and Testing

Any difference between the development and production environment and the risks associated with such differences will be identified and justified during Project Definition phase. The system test activities will be recognized as relating to Final Inspection and Testing.

8.2.4.2.5 Inspection and Test Records

Inspection and test records are maintained to provide evidence that the product has been inspected and/or tested. Reviews are carried out to verify that the product has passed the inspection and/or test. No product will be dispatched until all the activities specified in the Quality Plan have been satisfactorily completed and associated data and documentation are available and authenticated.

8.2.4.2.6 Metrics

The PM will identify the metrics required by the contract/organisational standards in the Quality Assurance Plan (QAP).

The Inspection and review activities will be carried out as per the Verification procedure (PR07) and Validation procedure (PR08) procedures.

The included software product will be verified and subjected to the Configuration Management (PR04) procedure.

A release readiness review meeting will be held before delivery to the customer.

8.3 Control of Nonconforming Product

8.3.1 Policy

Integra ensures that a product that does not conform to the requirements is controlled to prevent unintended use or delivery.

Integra provides for identification of, recording and reviewing the nature and extent of the nonconforming products.

The system level procedure defines arrangements made for ensuring that nonconforming product is controlled.

8.3.2 Procedure

8.3.2.1 General

A record of defects found during testing will be maintained. The defects encountered during the unit testing will be recorded. The non-conforming software item will be transferred from the controlled library to the dynamic library or into a separate environment. The fixing and re-testing will be done in this separate environment.

8.3.2.2 Review and Disposition of non-compliant product

The defects will be analyzed to determine non-conforming items. Such items will be modified and re-tested based on a documented scope for re-testing. Disposition or resolution of the defect could be done in one of the following:

· Fix defects to meet the requirement

· Reject due to amendment of requirements, concession or incorrect reporting of defect

The software items will be stored in different libraries based on their status as per the Configuration Management procedure. The location of these libraries will be defined in the SCM Plan. The defects will be recorded and resolved as per this procedure. The Delivery Head will review the control of non-conforming products and approve the release of the product to the customer after the product satisfies the requirements. A release review is conducted prior to release of the product to the customer and the release review is attended by the Delivery Head and the Quality Manager.

8.4 Analysis of Data

8.4.1 Policy

Integra will establish a procedure for the analysis of applicable data as a means of determining the effectiveness of the Quality Management System and for identifying where improvements can be made.

Integra collects data generated by measuring and monitoring activities and other relevant sources.

Integra analyzes applicable data to provide information on:

· The suitability, effectiveness and adequacy of the Quality Management System

· Process operation trends

· Customer satisfaction and dissatisfaction

· Conformance to customer requirements

· Characteristics of processes and products

8.4.2 Procedure

Product data and information will be collected as explained in the Project Closure (PR15) procedure.

The PM will collect metrics data on a monthly and phase-end basis as per the Progress Monitoring and Control (PR03) and Project Closure (PR15) procedures. The PM will analyze the data.

Metrics Report and Monthly Status Reports will be used to evaluate performance and baseline report will be generated periodically as per the Measurement and Analysis (FP11) procedure.

The results of analysis will be discussed in the review meetings as per Management Review (FP15).

8.5 Improvement

8.5.1 Continual Improvement

8.5.1.1 Policy

Integra continually improves the Quality Management System.

Integra has established a system level procedure that describes the use of the quality policy, objectives, internal audit results, analysis of data, corrective and preventive action and management review to facilitate continual improvement.

8.5.1.2 Procedure

The following procedures for the analysis of applicable data are established as a means of determining the effectiveness of the quality management system and for identifying the areas where improvements can be made.

Feedback from customers is collected to take necessary corrective / preventive actions as per the Quality Management (FP13) procedure.

Identification, measurement and monitoring of processes are carried out as per the Measurement and Analysis (FP11) procedure.

Analysis of the project / product data and information will be done on a monthly and phase-end basis as per the Project Monitoring and Control (PR03) and Project Closure (PR15) procedures.

Metric reports and Monthly Status reports will be used to evaluate performance and report will be generated periodically as per the Measurement and Analysis (FP11) procedure .

The results of analysis will be discussed and actions identified in the review meetings as per the Management Review (FP15) procedure .

8.5.2 Corrective Action

Integra will continually improve the effectiveness of the quality management system through the use of the quality policy, quality objectives, audit results, analysis of data and management review. Corrective action will be taken to eliminate the root causes of non-conformities in order to prevent recurrence.

8.5.2.1 Policy

Integra will establish processes for eliminating the root causes of nonconformity in order to prevent recurrence.

The procedure for the corrective action process includes, but is not limited to:

· Identification of non-conformities (including customer complaints)

· Determination of root causes of non-conformities

· Evaluation of the need for actions to ensure that non-conformities do not reoccur

· Implementation of any actions determined necessary to ensure that non-conformities do not recur

· Recording the results of actions taken

· Follow-up to ensure that the corrective action taken is effective and recorded

8.5.2.2 Procedure

· The customer complaints will be handled as per the Customer Complaints Management (PR14) procedure .

· The product non-conformities will be subjected to the Configuration Management (PR04) procedure .

· The MR will analyze the process non-conformities and implement changes as per the QMS Document Management (FP14) procedure .

· The relevant information or action taken including the QMS changes will be submitted for Management Review.

8.5.2 Preventive Action

8.5.3.1 Policy

Integra has established a process for eliminating the causes of potential non-conformities to prevent occurrence. Quality Management System records and results from the analysis of data are used as inputs for preventive actions, as applicable.

The system-level preventive action procedure addresses:

· Identification of potential non-conformities

· Determination of the causes of identified potential non-conformities and recording the results

· Determination of preventive action needed to eliminate the causes of potential non-conformities

· Implementation of preventive action

· Review to ensure that the preventive action taken is effective and recorded

8.5.3.1 Procedure

The relevant information or action taken including the QMS changes will be submitted for Management Review.

The SQA/SEPG will analyze the data gathered through audits, metrics, customer complaints, customer satisfaction survey, project closure reports and select the most vital problems requiring preventive action.
