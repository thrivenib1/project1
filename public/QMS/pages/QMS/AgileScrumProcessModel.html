Introduction

Agile Scrum is an iterative, incremental framework for projects and product development. It structures development in cycles of work called Sprints. These iterations are no more than one month each, and take place one after the other. The Sprints are time boxed they end on a specific date whether the work has been completed or not, and are never extended.


2. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

In Scrum, there are three roles: Product Owner, Scrum Master and Team


a) Product Owner

The Product Owner identifies product / application features, translates these into a prioritized list, continually re-prioritizes and refines the list based on interaction with stakeholders (management, delivery head, customer, sales team, etc). Ideally the project manager can take up this role.

b) Scrum Master

The Scrum Master makes sure the team understands and follows the practices of Scrum. The scrum master is responsible for resolving any impediments or blocks faced by the team. The team lead can ideally take up this role.

c) Team

The Team builds the product / application that the Product Owner indicates. The Team in Scrum is ?cross-functional? ? it includes all the expertise necessary to deliver the potentially shippable product each Sprint.

3. THE PROCESS

3.1. Starting the Project

➣ Create PIN for the project using FRPPL01

➣ Conduct project kickoff and record using TPPPL06

➣ Prepare the Software Project Management Plan (SPMP) by using the PMP template (TPPPL03), CMP using the template TPCON01, QAP using the template (TPPPL04) and RMP using the template (TPRSM01).

➣ Prepare product backlog, which is a list of features / functionalities of the product / application using the following template (TPPBL01)

Note: Only a single Product Backlog exists. The Product Backlog may evolve with new customer features, engineering improvements, known defects, etc.

3.2. Starting the Sprint

At the beginning of each Sprint, the Sprint Planning Meeting is conducted

➣ Review the high-priority items in the Product Backlog

➣ Select items to be executed in the upcoming sprint

➣ Discuss the goals and context for the selected items.

➣ Break down each selected item into individual tasks, which are recorded in a document called the Sprint Backlog using the following template or any tool to track the progress and derive burn down charts TPSBL02

3.3. Daily Scrum

Once the Sprint has started, the Team engages in Daily Scrum. This is a short (15 minutes or less) meeting that happens every workday at an appointed time and is mandatory for all team members.

➣ One by one, each member of the Team reports three things to the other members of the Team: (1) What they were able to get done since the last meeting; (2) what they are planning to finish by the next meeting; and (3) any blocks or impediments that are in their way.

➣ The Scrum Master notes down any blocks or impediments.

➣ The Team members update their estimate of the amount of time remaining to complete their current task in the Sprint Backlog.

Note:

1. There is no discussion during the Daily Scrum, only reporting answers to the three questions; if discussion is required it takes place immediately after the Daily Scrum.

2. On update of sprint backlog, the sprint burn down chart gets automatically updated. This graph shows, each day, a new estimate of how much work (measured in person hours) remains until the Teamâ€™s tasks are finished

3.4. Ending the Sprint

The Sprint is never extended ? it ends on the assigned date regardless of whether the Team has completed the work it committed to.

➣ Create Sprint Closure Report using the following template TPSCL03

➣ List down the deliverables with the status

➣ Enter the scope and effort (planned & actual)

➣ Calculate and fill scope adherence ((No. of sprint items completed / No. of sprint items planned) * 100)

➣ Calculate and fill effort variance (calculated as defined in QMS)

3.5. Sprint Review

After the Sprint ends, there is the Sprint Review, where the Team and the Product Owner review the Sprint

➣ Demonstrate the product or application built during the sprint to all the stakeholders.

➣ Collect the feedback, if any, on further improvements, requirements, etc.

➣ Update the product backlog based on the feedback.

3.6. Sprint Retrospective

The Sprint Retrospective, which follows the Review, involves inspection and adaption regarding the process.

➣ Create a sprint retrospective sheet using the following template TPSRP04

➣ Based on feedback from team, fill the first two columns i.e., factors that worked well during the sprint and factors that were problematic during the sprint.

➣ Analyse the underlying causes and list down action to be taken for improvement.

➣ Review and update status of previous sprintâ€™s action items.

4. Appendix: Terminology

a) Burn Down

The trend of work remaining across time in a Sprint. The source of the raw data is the Sprint Backlog, with work remaining tracked on the vertical axis and the time periods (days of a Sprint) tracked on the horizontal axis.

b) Estimated Work Remaining

The numbers of hours that a Team member estimates remain to be worked on any task. This estimate is updated at the end of every day when the Sprint Backlog task is worked on.

c) Sprint

Iteration, or one repeating cycle of similar work, that produces increment of product or system.

d) Product Backlog

A prioritized list of requirements with estimated times to turn them into completed product functionality. The list emerges, changing as business conditions or technology changes.

e) Sprint Backlog

A list of tasks that defines a Teamâ€™s work for a Sprint. Each task identifies those responsible for doing the work and the estimated amount of work remaining on the task on any given day during the Sprint.

f) Stakeholder

Someone with an interest in the outcome of a project, either because they have funded it, will use it, or will be affected by it.

g) Team

A group of people that is responsible for managing themselves to develop an increment of product every Sprint.

h) Time box

A period of time that cannot be exceeded and within which an event occurs.
