Requirements Management
ISO Clause: 7.2 PR01 Version: 3.00 Date: 17th May 2010

1.0 Introdution

1.1.1 Purpose
This process defines the method used within Integra for providing Requirements Management (REQM).

The prime purpose of the REQM processes is as follows:

  • Manage all requirements received and generated by the project including technical, non-technical and process related requirements.
  • To identify inconsistencies between those requirements and the project’s plans and work products.
  • To formally manage any change requirements.

1.1.2 Policy
The REQM process is to be referred to and followed for obtaining and managing requirements throughout the project lifecycle of any type of project/service provided within the organisation.

1.1.3 Scope
Within the duration of the project, REQM is used after the complete initial requirements are defined and mutually agreed upon by the authorised requirements provider and requirements receiver. Thereafter REQM defines how the evolving and additional new requirements are managed and consistency is retained till the end of the project.

This process is applicable to all projects/services within the organization.

1.1.4 Entry criteria

The basic criteria for initiating the REQM processes are:

  • Customer approved RFP/URS

1.3.2 Tasks
The steps of the REQM Process are described below:

1.3.2.1 Obtain an understanding of Requirements

  • Post activities by the Sales department in soliciting work from customers in a specific domain, the customer if interested and if the need exists will provide either an RFP or a detailed URS for specific project work.
  • If the customer provides a detailed URS, then the identified Architect / Project Manager will analyse the URS and prepare a system study report (SSR) and DOU (Document of Understanding) using TPREQ01 based on a detailed study besides past experience if any in that domain.
  • The SSR /DOU will be reviewed by the DH. Refer to guidelines for requirements elicitation GLREQ01.
  • Identify requirements not stated by the customer but necessary for the specified or intended use of the product
  • Identify requirements needed to fulfil the statutory and regulatory compliance of the product
  • The reviewed SSR and DOU will be sent to the customer for approval and sign-off by the sales representative.

Alternatively:

  • If the customer provides an URS after project kick-off, the Architect / Project Manager will analyse the URS and update the SSR and technical proposal and prepare change request if applicable.
  • The SSR and CR will be reviewed by the DH.
  • The reviewed CR will be sent to the customer for approval and sign-off by the sales representative.

1.3.2.2 Obtain Commitment to Requirements:

  • The internally reviewed and approved SSR / DOU or the reviewed URS will be sent to the customer for approval and sign-off by the sales representative.
  • Negotiations on schedule /effort (if required) for implementation of the refined understanding of requirements is carried out. Mutually agreed updates in schedule/effort, affected work products, product components will be subsequently updated in the project plan. This is done by the Sales representative and the System Architect.
  • Changes/Updates to earlier envisaged requirements if any, should be negotiated and mutually agreed upon.
  • Any Changes/updates in schedule / effort and ensuing change in cost needs to be approved by the customer prior to implementation. A formal written acceptance of the same is required. This is co-ordinated by the Sales representative.

1.3.2.3 Manage Requirements Changes:

  • Post sign off of URS, if any changes to requirements are received, the procedure to be followed is documented under “Change Management” in the Configuration Management procedure.

1.3.2.4 Maintain Bidirectional traceability:

  • Both forward and backward traceability of requirements is essential for effective requirements management. To ensure this the following tasks need to be carried out.
  • The PM and Architect will prepare a RTM using TPREQ02 to ensure the bi-directional traceability between URS, SRS, Design, Code and test artefacts.
  • When a change request is received and accepted, the RTM in all the above artefacts is updated as required.

1.3.2.5 Identify Inconsistencies Between Project Work and Requirements:

  • The project plan, current status of activities and work products are regularly reviewed and monitored on a weekly basis.
  • These reviews would highlight inconsistencies if any between whatever has been implemented and the constantly evolving requirements.
  • Explicit or implicit inconsistencies if any are documented including the source of inconsistency or conditions under which it occurs. These changes are formally reviewed.
  • Necessary corrective actions are implemented and the project plans and work products are updated accordingly.
