6.1 Introdution

6.1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to define the Facility and Administration activities.

6.1.2 Policy
This process will be applicable for general good housekeeping of the infrastructure and allocation of the same to the employees of the organization.

6.1.3 Scope

This procedure will be applicable to the general administration of facilities and purchases in Integra.

6.3 Task Details

6.3.1 Plan Facilities

The Head- Engineering, will plan for the office space and interiors based on the Business Plan for the next calendar year. The thumb rule for estimating office space is 60 square feet per employee. The office space is usually planned about 6 - 12 months in advance.

6.3.2 Allocation of work Space to new joiners

The General Admin in-charge will ensure the allocation of workspace to newly joined employees. (Intimation for the same is received from the personnel dept. through an e-mail)

6.3.3 Access Control System

The General Admin In-Charge will monitor and maintain the access control system of the organization. Access to the work area of the organization is controlled with the help of access control system. For security reasons different secured zones are created inside the office premises. Access to these areas is controlled by the access control system.

6.3.4 General Work Area: (Zone one)

Access is granted to all employees of the organization to this area. Access control system installed at the main entrance of the office premises monitors the access of all employees. This system also serves the purpose of attendance of all employees. A gust must be accompanied at all times by the concerned employee, with whom the gust has business transactions.

6.3.5 Server Room and Patch Panel Room: (Zone two)

Access to this area is further restricted to only the Director, Executive Director (Head Engineering) and the System Administration team.

The General Admin In-Charge will create the access permissions for different employees to these restricted zones and the list of all access holders to these areas will be maintained.

  • Access to Zone one will be created on the advice of the HR.
  • Access to Zone two is created on the advice of the Executive Director (Head Engineering)

6.3.6 Access Report:

The General Admin In-Charge will prepare a monthly access report (Zone wise) and submit the same to the Executive Director (Head Engineering) in the first week of every month for the preceding month.

6.3.7 Removal of access:

The General Admin In-Charge will remove access permission of an employee to any of the zones based on the advice of the DH/PM/APM/HR with immediate effect. The General Admin In-Charge will keep records of all such incidences.

6.3.8 Access Control Database

The System Administrator will maintain the access control database for a calendar year i.e. from January to December. Backup of the access control database will be taken and will be archived as per Backup Recovery and Archival Procedure PR-10 .

6.3.9 Identify and Evaluate Suppliers

The General Admin In-Charge/Purchase Coordinator will identify suppliers for office supplies and services including interiors. Suppliers typically include:

  • Housekeeping Agencies
  • Facility Maintenance Agencies
  • Security Agencies
  • Office Supply Stationers
  • Interior Designers and Architects
  • Hardware and Software Product Suppliers

The Head- Engineering will identify suppliers for hardware and software products and services. The General Admin In-Charge will evaluate these suppliers using the Supplier Assessment Form (FR-65A) and will be approved by Head- Engineering.

The suppliers will be selected based on the following criteria:

  • Capability of the supplier to supply or service on-time
  • Past performance
  • Reputation
  • Ability to provide continuous support
  • Competitive pricing and quality product

6.3.10 Maintain List of Approved Suppliers

For hardware and software products and services, the General Admin In-Charge will prepare an approved list of suppliers as per Approved List of Suppliers (FR-65B) and submit the same to Head - Engineering for review and approval.

The issues with the suppliers will be logged as per Supplier Issue Log (FR-65C). Based on the type of issues and occurrences, the General Admin In-Charge will decide to blacklist a supplier. The General Admin In-Charge will remove such suppliers from the approved list of suppliers, if their performance is not satisfactory.

6.3.11 Purchase of Office Supplies/Services

The General Admin In-Charge will purchase office supplies and services as per the Purchasing procedure.

6.3.12 Coordinating import and re-export of equipment

The Admin Team will coordinate with government agencies like Customs and STPI to ensure that equipment that is needed for any project is cleared and is available for use without any delays. Monitoring of Incoming and Outgoing Goods

The Admin Team will log all outgoing goods using Gate Pass (FR-65D).

The Admin Team will inspect the incoming goods based on the purchase order by using Inward material checklist (CH-28). Any defective goods will be returned to the supplier.

The System Administrator will inspect any incoming hardware / software. Any defective items will be returned to the supplier through the Administrative Officer.

6.3.13 Monitoring of Incoming and Outgoing Goods

The Admin Team will log all the incoming and outgoing goods using Gate Pass (FR-65D).

The General Admin In-Charge will inspect the incoming goods based on the purchase order by using Inward material checklist (CH-28). Any defective goods will be returned to the supplier.

The System Administrator will inspect any incoming hardware / software by using Inward Material Checklist (CH-28). Any defective items will be returned to the supplier through the Administrative Officer.

6.3.14 Archival Maintenance

The General Admin In-Charge will maintain the project archival as per the Backup, Recovery and Archival Maintenance (PR10) procedure.

The General Admin In-Charge will maintain other records as per QMS Document Control procedure PR-53.

6.3.15 Process Administration Request

The Admin Team will process the following type of job requests:

Job Type (Generic)

  • Additional visiting cards
  • Employee ID card replacement
  • Restoration of electrical /telephone breakdown
  • Workspace allocation
  • Furniture
  • Booking of meeting rooms (through PCC)
  • Booking LCD (through PCC)

Job Type (Special)

  • Telephone Equipment
  • External photo-copying
  • Voice and Data SIM

The General Admin In-Charge will allocate workspace to employees based on the following criteria with the approval of HR for workstation and functional area and cabin from CTO & above

  • Cabins/Semi-height Cubicles/Workstation Cubicles will be allocated based on the above approving authority
  • Project/functional area requirements

6.3.16 Front Office Support

The Front-Office Support will include the following activities:

  • Receiving and forwarding telephone calls/fax
  • Issue of office supplies to employees
  • Monitoring of employee attendance details
  • Receiving customers, visitors and suppliers
  • Monitoring of lunch details
  • Monitoring of BSNL/Airtel Bills
  • Monitoring of Postal and Courier documents

6.3.17 Security and Vigilance

The General Admin In-Charge will ensure that all the development centers will have round-the-clock manned security personnel.

6.3.18 Prepare Admin Status Report

The General Admin In-Charge will prepare Admin. Monthly Status Report (FRGAM02) at the end of every month and submit it to Head-Engineering for approval. The Head-Engineering will forward the status report to the Director for discussion in the Operational Review Meeting as stated in the Management Review (FP15) procedure.

6.3.19 MLD and MLR

The General Admin In-Charge will maintain MLD and MLR for the function.

6.3.20 Retention and Disposition of Records

  • The owner of the record will maintain the records for a period of one year or as required by the contract if applicable.
  • In January every year, each Function Head will identify all records pertaining to the function which are more than a year old. All such records will be packed and suitably labeled and handed over to the General Admin In-Charge for storage.
  • In January every year, the General Admin In-Charge will identify all records, which are more than three years old and will destroy them as applicable.
  • The General Admin In-Charge will maintain the list of the stored item using Archival Register FR-10A.
