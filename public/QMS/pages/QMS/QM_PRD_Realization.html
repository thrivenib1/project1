7 Product Realization

7.1 Planning of Product Realization

7.1.1 Policy

Processes, their sequence and interaction are determined, planned and implemented to realize product quality and to meet customer requirements. Product realization planning will be consistent with the requirements of Quality Management System and the results will be documented.

Planning covers the following:

· The processes required to meet customer requirements (scope),

· Quality objectives of the production

· Time management,

· Identifying and managing risks,

· Change management for work products, scope, time and cost

· The realization processes and resources needed to achieve the desired results

· Measurement and monitoring of the processes and the work product

· The verification and validation activities, criteria for acceptability and

· The records needed to establish evidence that the realization processes and the work product meets requirements

7.1.2 Procedure

The documented procedures described in this manual may need to be selected, adapted and/or supplemented based on the nature of the project or customer needs. Project management plans will be prepared after the project initiation. The project management plan, comprising of Project Management Plan (PMP), Quality Assurance Plan (QAP), Configuration Management Plan (CMP), Communication Management Plan (CoMP) and Risk Management Plan (RMP) will be prepared for every project. Work aids for preparing these documents are available in the templates and checklists sections of the QMS.

These plans provide a means for tailoring application of the Quality Management System to a specific project. The plans address the following:

· The Life Cycle Model to be used

· Input and Output criteria for each phase Quality requirements expressed in measurable terms as appropriate

· The Verification and Validation activities to be carried out

· Any customer processes to be followed as part of the contractual requirement

7.2 Customer-Related Processes

7.2.1 Determination of Requirements Related to the Product

Integra ensures the following with the aim of successful realization of a product.

· Identification of customer needs and expectations for delivery and post delivery activities

· Identify requirements not stated by the customer but necessary for the intended use of the product

· Identify the statutory and regulatory requirements applicable for the product

· Identify other requirements considered necessary by Integra.

7.2.2 Review of Requirements Related to the Product

Integra will review the product requirements prior to submission of proposal to supply the product or service. The contracts undertaken by Integra will be reviewed as per the Business Development procedure (FP01) to ensure that: Business Development procedure (FP01).

· The requirements are adequately defined.

· Any differences between customer requirement and contract are resolved.

· Integra has the capability to meet the contractual obligation.

· Requirements development procedure, (PR05), is used to develop the requirements relating to the product.

· Requirements Management procedure, (PR01), is used to manage the requirements relating to the product.

· Review records will be maintained.

· The customer generally gives the user requirements; however, if the customer does not give documented requirements, Integra will document the requirements and obtain approval from the customer.

7.2.3 Customer Communication

Integra ensures effective communication with the customer with the aim of successful realization of the product. A representative of the customer will be identified as the Customer Project Coordinator (CPC). Project Manager will interact with the CPC for all communications. The sales representative for the project will also be involved in all communications with the customer. Customer communication will focus:

· Product or service information.

· Product or service realization process monitoring and controlling status reports, if required as per the contractual agreement.

· Enquiries, contracts and amendments.

· Collecting feedback and customer complaint.

· Communication plan will elaborate the communication process.

· Records of communication relating to technical, schedule or cost issues will be maintained by the PM.

· The PM will also maintain records of escalations in the project, if any

7.2.3.1 Contract Amendment

Amendment to the contract will be reviewed for feasibility, impact on schedule and cost. Relevant information will be transferred to the concerned functions within Integra and to the customer. The procedure for contract amendment is defined in Business Development Procedure (FP01).

7.2.3.2 Customer feedback and complaint

· All complaints from the customer will be addressed by using Handling Customer Complaints procedure (PR14)

· Feedback from customers is collected to take necessary corrective / preventive actions as per Customer Satisfaction Survey section of Quality Management (FP13) procedure.

7.3 Design and Development

Integra has established documented procedures to control, verify and validate Design and Development of products and services. The PM in consultation with the Architect and the EPG will decide the process model to be followed for the project from the available process models. If a suitable process model is not available for the PM can suggest and customize a process model and forward to the EPG for approval. Detailed activities to be followed during the SDLC of the project are explained in the process model.

7.3.1 Design and Development Planning

Project proposal is prepared as per Business Development Procedure (FP01). Estimation for the project is prepared using Estimation Guidelines GLPPL01 and Estimation Worksheet TPPPL02 or Object Point Estimation Worksheet TPPPL02A.

The PM will tailor the SDLC processes to suit the project requirements and mention the deviations in the PMP. Also, this will incorporate any customer processes to be followed as part of the contractual requirement. The PMP will form the complete reference guide with respect to the schedule and processes for the project, except for interfaces with support functions.

The plan will be reviewed and approved prior to use and the schedule and the milestones relating to the project will be communicated to the CPC. The plan will be updated as the project evolves.

7.3.2 Design and Development Inputs

The customer provided documented requirements. Integra will review the requirements. Incomplete, ambiguous or conflicting requirements will be resolved with the customer. However, where mutually agreed, Integra will develop the requirement specification in close co-operation with the CPC. The requirement specification will specify:

The quality characteristics such as, functionality, efficiency, reliability, usability, maintainability, and portability

· Applicable statutory and regulatory requirements as per contractual agreement.

· Any other requirements essential for design and development of the product.

A software requirement specification (SRS) is prepared and a traceability of requirements between the SRS and customer requirements is established using TPREQ. The SRS template is available in the work aid section of the QMS. The SRS will be reviewed for adequacy and approved prior to use and review record will be maintained.

7.3.3 Design and Development Outputs

The outputs of the Design and Development activities will be in accordance with the chosen process model. The outputs may include Software Design Documents HLD and LLD, Source code, and User manual. The software design is documented using template TPDES01 and TPDES02 developed using guidelines GLDES01.

· Traceability will be established with the requirements document.

· Identify and provide adequate information for the purchasing, production and service provisions.

· Document acceptance criteria of the product.

· Document the user guide of the product

7.3.4 Design and Development Review

The outputs of design and development will be reviewed to ensure that the specified requirements are met. The reviews include design reviews and code reviews. Group review of SRS and SDD will be carried out as per Procedure for Verification PR07.Review records will be maintained.

7.3.5 Design and Development Verification

The design outputs will be verified by means such as reviews, prototypes, simulation or tests using verification procedure PR07.

Acceptance Criteria: The acceptance criteria for the work products generated during each phase of the projects are described in the "Output" of respective phases of Process Models defined in the Process manual section of the QMS. Please refer to section QMS-02.

7.3.6 Design and Development Validation

The code will be tested prior to acceptance and subsequent use. Validation procedure PR08 will be used to validate the product. The validation will be performed in earlier stages prior to product completion. Integra will ensure that the design validation conforms to the defined user requirements.

Depending upon the complexity of the design, prototypes will be made for the validation of the design prior to coding.

7.3.7 Control of Design and Development Changes

Integra will ensure that all design changes are identified, documented, reviewed and approved by authorized personnel before their implementation. Any changes to the approved design will be done as described in Configuration Management (PR04) procedure. Review records will be maintained.

7.4 Purchasing

7.4.1 Purchasing Process

Integra will establish and maintain documented procedure to ensure that the purchased product or support service conforms to the specified requirements.

The purchased product can be software and/or hardware items intended for inclusion in the final product or service or a tool used to assist the development of the required product.

Integra will evaluate and select suppliers on the basis of their ability to meet the requirements as per FP08, Purchaseprocedure.

Whenever Integra decides to subcontract any work item it will follow FP08, Purchase procedure.

7.4.2 Purchasing Information

Purchasing documents will be maintained and reviewed for adequacy of technical specifications. The purchase will be made from approved suppliers.

7.4.3 Verification of Purchased Products

The purchased products, hardware and software, will be verified, before acceptance, to confirm whether they meet the requirements.

The General Administration and System Administration team will be responsible for verifying the purchased products. The System Administration team will maintain hardware and software as per the System Administration and Management procedure (FP10).

In case, the purchased product is intended to be integrated with the product/project to be delivered to the customer, the PM will verify the product for conformance with requirement before accepting and the review record will be maintained.

7.5 Production and Service Provision

7.5.1 Control of Production and Service Provision

Integra plans and controls software development and services through:

· The availability of specifications that define the requirements and characteristics of the product that are to be achieved.

· The availability of clearly understandable standards and/or guidelines for those activities where they are necessary for the achievement of conformity of product.

· The use and maintenance of suitable hardware and software.

· The use of a properly managed network.

· The provision of suitable working environment.

· The implementation of suitable monitoring and verification activities.

· Suitable methods for release and delivery of product.

· Post delivery activities for the product and services.

· Ensuring that the PCs are free of viruses.

· Access control using passwords for all users and super user passwords for the system administrator.

· Ensuring control over replication wherever applicable.

· Suitable plans, instructions and methods for release and installation.

· Maintain inventory of hardware and software.

7.5.1.1 Control of Software Development

The PM will select a suitable life-cycle model form the above process models. The process model can be customized as per requirement of the project. The software development method is specified in the respective process model. There is certain variation in the development process and it is specific to the process model selected for particular project. Therefore the PM should select a model appropriate for the project. It is important however to note that the basic Software engineering practice is valid for all the process models, however there is variation in extent of documentations in these process models. The PM should suggest if further improvement in the process model is required and such requests should be sent the SQA department for improvement of the QMS.

7.5.1.2 Control of Software Servicing

When specified in the contract, post-acceptance support will be provided for a specified period.

A separate contract will be prepared for maintenance activities. A Maintenance Plan will be prepared covering the scope of maintenance, initial status of project and resources needed. Customer approval will be taken on the plan.

The following procedure will be followed:

The change requested during post-acceptance period will be subjected to Configuration Management (PR04)

The maintenance activities can be typically classified into the following:

· Problem resolving or bug fixing.

· Interface modification due to changes/limitations of the operating environment.

· Functional expansion or performance improvement.

7.5.2 Validation of Processes for Production and Service Provision

Integra at present does not deal with any product where processing deficiencies may become apparent only after the product is in use.

If any products, where the resulting output cannot be readily or economically verified by subsequent monitoring, verification or testing, are to be handled, Integra will validate the process to demonstrate the effectiveness and acceptability.

Evidence of validated processes will be recorded and maintained.

7.5.3 Identification and Traceability

Integra has established procedure for identifying / tracking software items during all the phases of the project from initiation through delivery and acceptance.

At the organizational level, a Software Configuration Control Board (SCCB) has been formed, keeping in mind, to manage the configuration of reusable components. The SCCB is a board having the authority for managing the organization's software baselines. SCCB authorizes the establishment of software baselines and the identification of configuration items / units. SCCB consists of Engineering Head, DH, PMs, Architects & Configuration Controller (CC). The SCCB will manage the re-usable software components of the organization. The Engineering Head heads the SCCB.

Software Configuration Management (SCM) of a project is managed by the PM and a member of the project team playing the role of CC. The System Administration team will create project repository and provide necessary access permissions to the team members to specific locations in the project repository. Thereafter the CC will manage the SCM activities of the project.

The identification and traceability will be achieved through a Configuration Management System that will provide the capability to:

· Identify uniquely the versions of each software item.

· Identify the versions of each software item (baseline), which together constitute a specific version of a complete product.

· Control simultaneous updating of given software item by two or more people working independently.

· Identify and track all actions and changes resulting from Request for Change

Configuration Management procedure (PR04) will be used to record, manage and report status on software items and of change request and of implementation of approved changes.

7.5.3.1 Identification and Traceability Procedures

Identification schemes will be established as per the Configuration Management (PR04) procedure. The libraries where the software items will be located will also be identified.

The SCM Plan will include:

· Document and source items and their naming conventions

· Version numbering scheme.

· Event at which the items are brought under configuration control.

· Baselines.

· Check-in and Check-out mechanisms.

· Tools used for configuration management.

The changes to configuration items brought under configuration control will be as per the Configuration Management procedure. The baseline record and configuration will be carried out as per the same procedure.

7.5.4 Customer Property

Documented procedures are established for the control of verification, storage and maintenance of customer supplied products. The customer-supplied items may include

· Third party tools

· Hardware

· Software items including source files and documents

· Commercial software supplied by the customer as per permitted site licenses

Any customer supplied product that is lost, damaged or is otherwise unsuitable for use will be recorded and reported to the customer.

7.5.4.1 Procedure for handling customer property

· All the customer-supplied items will be controlled as per the Control of Customer Supplied Material (PR12) procedure.

· All the software items will be subjected to the Configuration Management (PR04) procedure.

· All the customer documents will be subjected to the Project Document Management (PR13) procedure.

· All the customer-supplied material will be reviewed and verified, and record maintained.

7.5.5 Preservation of product

Integra will establish and maintain procedures for handling, storage, packaging, preservation and delivery of products.

7.5.5.1 Handling

All media containing software items will be handled in a way that prevents damage or deterioration.

7.5.5.2 Storage

A mechanism will be established for

· Storing software items

· Controlling access to software items

· Maintaining baselines

7.5.5.3 Packaging

The media containing software items will be checked for virus before delivery to the customer. During packaging, the software may be encrypted/ compressed.

7.5.5.4 Preservation

A system will be established for regular backups and storage of media in protected environment that prevents damage or deterioration. The backup mechanism will also enable disaster recovery. For Integra product development, off-site backup will be ensured.

7.5.5.5 Handling and Preservation Procedures

The software items will be stored in software libraries as per the Configuration Management procedure. The actual locations of these libraries will be defined in the SCM Plan.

The PM will package and deliver the software items as per the contractual agreement. The common methods for delivery are FTP, E-mail, copy or courier. The backups will be maintained as per the Backup and Recovery procedure.

7.6 Control of Measuring and Monitoring Devices

Integra will identify and review the tools and techniques used for conducting tests to verify conformance of the software product to the specified requirements

7.6.1 Control Procedure

The PM will be responsible for identifying appropriate testing tools that are to be used for project execution. The testing tools will be documented in the Project Plan.

The DH and PM will review and approve the testing tools.

The tools could be purchased, supplied by the customer, obtained from the public domain or developed internally.

The testing tools will be verified and subjected to Configuration Management procedure.

The Infrastructure Team will be responsible for installing and maintaining the hardware and software as per the PR11 Infrastructure and HR Request and Release procedure.
