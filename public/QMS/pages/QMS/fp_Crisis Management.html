9.1 Introduction

This document is intended to serve as a reference and training manual for all personnel involved in crisis management of the organization. Parts of this document may be circulated among all employees in order to keep everyone aware of the procedures to be followed. This crisis management manual shall be applicable to all facilities and branches of the organization.

Examples of crisis situations include, but are not limited to:

  • Fire in the premise.
  • Electrical mishap
  • Riot situation.
  • Accident leading to serious injury and loss of property.
  • Bomb threat.

9.4 In case of fire alarm

Fire alarm systems have been installed in the office premises and fire extinguishers are placed in select locations of the building. The General Admin personnel are trained in safety measures to follow in case of any accident. The General Admin In-Charge will verify proper functioning of all the safety instruments installed in the premises.

The following steps are to be followed in case of fire alarm:

  • All the Admin team members will reach the Admin Section on hearing the fire alarm siren.
  • Security personnel will identify the zone and raise an alarm informing the Admin In-Charge and any admin personnel in the admin area.
  • Admin In-Charge will enable the emergency exit in the access system to open all doors.
  • Electrical In-Charge (Technical Executive) will reach the switchgear room and immediately switch off the power supply pertaining to the identified zone.
  • The housekeeping boys along with the housekeeping Supervisor will ensure that all the doors are open and fire marshal in each section should alert the employees and force them to move outside the building.
  • Security personnel after identifying the zone will alert other guards and open the back exit leaving one guard at the post.
  • Housekeeping boys will reach the fire zone, accompanied by the fire marshal, with the extinguishers / sand buckets and douse the fire.
  • The Admin In-Charge will ensure that the fire extinguishers are refilled on a regular basis and fire safety steps will be displayed at prominent location of the building.
  • The Admin In-Charge will ensure that the employees are trained on safety precautions.

9.6 Bomb Threats

Bomb threats are delivered in a variety of ways. The majority of threats are called in to the target. Occasionally these calls are through a third party. Sometimes a threat is communicated in writing or by a recording. Two logical explanations for reporting a bomb threat are:

  1. The caller has definite knowledge or believes that an explosive or bomb has been or will be placed and he/she wants to minimize personal injury or property damage. The caller may be the person who placed the device or someone who has become aware of such information.
  2. The caller wants to create an atmosphere of anxiety and panic which will, in turn, result in a disruption of the normal activities at the premise where the device is purportedly placed.

Whatever the reason for the report, there will certainly be a reaction to it. Through proper planning, the wide variety of potentially uncontrollable reactions can be greatly reduced.

  • Bombs being delivered by car or left in a car are a grave reality. Parking should be restricted. If restricted parking is not feasible, properly identified employee vehicles should be parked closest to the facility and visitor vehicles parked at a distance.
  • Security and maintenance personnel should be alert for people who act in a suspicious manner, as well as objects, items, or parcels which look out of place or suspicious.

Contact Person: Rishi G K or Sridhar K

Responding to bomb threat

The bomb threat caller is the best source of information about the bomb. When a bomb threat is called in:

  • Keep the caller on the line as long as possible. Ask him/her to repeat the message. Record every word spoken by the person.
  • If the caller does not indicate the location of the bomb or the time of possible detonation, ask him/her for this information.
  • Inform the caller that the building is occupied and the detonation of a bomb could result in death or serious injury to many innocent people.
  • Pay particular attention to background noises, such as motors running, music playing, and any other noise, which may give a clue as to the location of the caller.
  • Listen closely to the voice (male, female), voice quality (calm, excited), accents, and speech impediments. Immediately after the caller hangs up, report the threat to the chairman of crisis management team.
  • Report the information immediately to the police department, fire department, and other appropriate agencies.
  • Remain available, as law enforcement personnel will want to interview you.
