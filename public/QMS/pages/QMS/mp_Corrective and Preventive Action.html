Corrective and Preventive Action
ISO Clause: 8.5.2 & 8.5.3 MP05 Version: 3.00 Date : 17th May 2010

5.1 Introdution

5.1.1 Purpose

This procedure is defined to implement an effective corrective and preventive action mechanism in the organisation.

5.1.2 Policy

This process will be applicable to all non-conformities and potential non-conformities in all projects and functions.

5.1.3 Definitions

Corrective Action: Action taken to eliminate the cause(s) of identified non-conformity or other undesirable situations

Preventive action: Action taken to eliminate the potential cause(s) of non-conformity or other potentially undesirable situations .

5.2 Stakeholders

5.2.1 Responsibilities
Sl.No. Role Responsibility Authority
1. PM /Function Head

Identify corrective and preventive actions using root cause analysis.

Implement corrective and preventive actions
2. QM/MR Verify effectiveness of implementation of corrective and preventive actions Approve corrective and preventive actions

5.3 Task Details

5.3.1 Corrective Actions

It may be necessary to take corrective actions due to audit findings or customer complaints. For customer complains practices, also refer to PR14.

Audit finding/ Customer complaint: Where non-conformances or non-compliances are identified through the audit process, the responsible project or functional area representative, the PM / Functional manager is responsible for:

1. Identifying the root cause(s) of non-conformities or non-compliances and document them

2. Take appropriate correction to eliminate the error

3. Identifying appropriate corrective actions (including modifying or creating procedures and work practices) necessary to eliminate the causes of the non-conformity.

4. Planning and implementing corrective actions.

5. Verifying the close-out and effectiveness of corrective actions using measurement techniques

The Quality Manager or MR will verify effectiveness of implementation of corrective actions.

5.3.2 Preventive Actions

1. Analysis of root causes of non-conformities provided input to the preventive action.

2. Determine the necessary actions required to be taken in-order to remove potential non-conformity

3. Review risks associated with the preventive action.

4. Measure effectiveness of the potential action taken for improvement (including modifying or creating procedures and work practices)

5. Implement effective action across organisation.

The Quality Manager or MR will verify effectiveness of implementation of preventive actions.

Records of corrective and preventive actions will be maintained as per the control of records procedure.
