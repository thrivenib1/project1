16.0 Introduction

16.1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this process is to define the steps required to plan and conduct a formal evaluation and decision making. A formal evaluation process may be used at any point in the life-cycle of a project[1] or execution of any other work whenever a formal decision is needed, for example COTS evaluation and selection. The activities involved in this process are:

  • Establishing the criteria for evaluating alternatives
  • Identifying alternative solutions
  • Selecting methods for evaluating alternatives
  • Evaluating the alternative solutions using the established criteria and methods
  • Selecting recommended solutions from the alternatives based on the evaluation criteria

16.1.2 Policy

  • Decision Analysis and Resolution (DAR) will be used for taking decision whenever the cost impact of the decision is more than rupees 5,00,000
  • DAR will be used in design process as a part of review meetings to select a technical solution.

16.1.3 Scope
DAR process is applicable to all projects and support functions across the organization and it is integrated with all other processes.

16.1.4 Entry Criteria

Criteria for determining when to use formal evaluation include the following:

a) When there is a clear definition of an issue requiring a decision

b) When there is a reasonable assumption that more than 1 valid option exists

16.2.2.4 Guidelines for Decision Analysis (DAR/SP 1.1)
Every decision is not significant enough to require a formal evaluation process. The choice between the trivial and the truly important is unclear without explicit guidance. Whether a decision is significant or not, is dependent on the project and circumstances and is determined by established guidelines. This section explains under what conditions a formal evaluation is to be undertaken.

Guidelines for methodologies, convening the committees, planning and conducting DARs:

  • DAR process will be used when the decision has greater risk exposure defined in the Risk Management process.
  • This process will be applied when a decision impacts the project goals (Schedule, Effort, Quality)
  • Making design-implementation decisions when technical performance failure can cause a major/critical failure
  • Follow the configuration management procedures for ensuring change control.
  • DAR will be applied when such decisions has the potential to significantly reduce:
    • Design risk (say use of new technology)
    • Response time
    • Effort
    • Schedule

16.2.2.5 Evaluation Criteria (DAR/SP 1.2)

Refer to DAR Report

  • Develop the evaluation criteria to be used when evaluating decision alternatives.
  • Map criteria to requirements, operational scenarios, and/or business cases.
  • Coordinate with stakeholders to ensure their needs, objectives and priorities are met.
  • Document the rationale for the selection or rejection evaluation criteria.
  • Define the range and scale of values for the evaluation criteria. Scales of relative importance should be established for criteria with non-numeric values or formula can be established to convert the non-numeric value to a numeric.
  • Establish a ranking for each criterion relative to each other. Use the defined range and scale to reflect the needs, objectives, and priorities of the stakeholders.
  • Assess the evaluation criteria, range and scale of values, and rankings with the stakeholders that will be impacted by the decision-making results.
  • Incorporate changes resulting from the assessment.

16.2.2.6 Identify Alternative Solutions (DAR/SP 1.3)

  • Conduct literature searches, interview stakeholders, conduct brainstorming and working group sessions, and combine key attributes of existing alternatives to generate the additional alternatives.
  • Document identified alternatives, indicate which will be considered and include rationale for those included and rejected.

16.2.2.7 Evaluation Methods (DAR/SP 1.4)

  • The DAR evaluation report provides the evaluation method.
  • The DAR leader can tailor the template or develop one as appropriate for the specific DAR.

16.2.2.8 Evaluate Alternatives (DAR/SP 1.5)

  • Evaluate the alternatives against the evaluation criteria. Use iterative analysis cycles as necessary.
  • Identify any unknowns, guesses, or assumptions made about the solution meeting evaluation criteria and document observations that support or disprove those assumptions.
  • Identify new alternatives if all current alternatives prove unsatisfactory and conduct evaluations of those alternatives.
  • Update evaluation plan, as needed.
  • Document evaluation results, including any unsatisfactory alternatives. Include rationale for additions of new alternatives and any changes to criteria.

16.2.2.9 Select an Alternative (DAR/SP 1.6)

  • Select the most favourable alternative.
  • Identify risks associated with selecting the solution.
  • Document the selected alternative, associated risks, and rationale for selection.
  • Review evaluation results and selected alternative with Stakeholders.
  • Review evaluation results and selected alternative with Stakeholders.
  • Collect identified DAR data.

16.2.3.3 Tailoring guidelines

The procedural steps included here are the core requirements for the conducting DAR at Integra. The PM of the project or the support function head, who is also the DAR lead, would tailor the DAR template depending on the need for the particular analysis.

16.2.3.4 Outputs

The outputs of the DAR process are;

  • Evaluation plan and
  • DAR report

16.2.3.5 Exit criteria

DAR report

16.2.3.6 Process measures

Actual effort spent (cost and schedule) on the decision and selection process.

16.2.3.7 Verification

The DAR report will be verified by the PPQA of the project, service or function and then by the senior management. The verification will be carried out as soon as the DAR analysis is completed.
