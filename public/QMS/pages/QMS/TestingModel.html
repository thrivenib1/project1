Testing Model
ISO Clause: 7.1 PM02 Version: 3.00 Date: 17th May 2010

Introduction

The Testing Process Model is used when scope of the work is limited to only testing the software. The main phases in this model are:

1. Requirement Phase,

2. Analysis Phase,

3. Testing Phase

4. Closure Phase

A brief discussion and detail activity involved in each of these phases are given below:

1. Requirement Phase

On signing the contract the CMG will create PCC entry for the project. The Marketing In-Charge of the project or the PM of the project will call for project kick-off meeting. (Refer to FP01 Sales Procedure Business Development Procedure.) The PM will present the schedule, effort, risks and the requirements from other support groups in the kick-off meeting. On formation of the project team, work on requirement phase begins. The PM will create a Project Folder for the project as per Control of Documents MP01 Document (MP01). Use Project Kick-off Checklist, CHPPL04.

1.1 Requirements Study

On signing the contract, the customer provides the Requirements for testing.

. PM will study the scope of the project and understand the customer requirements

. PM will review the customer-supplied material and identify the process to control the same as per the procedure for Control of customer-supplied material, (PR12).

. The PM will maintain the MOM of the Kick-off meeting using PCC / FRMMR02

. PM will identify the training needs for the TM and co-ordinate with the Training Co-ordinator for the training.

. PM will raise a Training request by using FROTR08.

Input Output of the Requirement Phase

1.2 Project Management Plan

1.2.1 Preparation of SPMP

The PM will prepare the SPMP, using the SPMP Template TPTST02 .The PM can tailor the SPMP to meet the project requirement with approval from the SQA. The PM will update the schedule and the effort section in SPMP for different cycles of the project. For multiple projects, the PM will prepare a Sub-project plan using template (TPTST03) and customise the document as per requirement for the project. While executing sub-projects the procedure for Sub-project PR-18 will be referred.

For device testing, the PM will prepare a sub-project for each device using TP-03, if more than one device needs to be tested in the scope of the project. Subsequently, if the same device is tested for different builds, the TP-03 will be updated with the schedule and effort for different cycles.

1.2.2 Review & Approval of SPMP

The DH will review and approve the SPMP and sub-project plan (if applicable) to ensure that the commitments stated in the plan are pragmatic and can be met. DH will record the review findings using PCC / IRR (FRVER01) and the record will be kept in the Project folder as per Document Control procedure.

1.2.3 Amendment to SPMP

The SPMP will be updated under the following conditions:

· Re-estimation of the project occurs

· Major scope changes to the contract occur

· Changes occur due to Formal / Informal Reviews and Audits (Internal/External)

· Changes occur due to Requirement Change Request

The revisions made in the SPMP will be reflected in the Revision History and will require approval from the same authorities that reviewed and approved the plan initially.

1.3 Resource planning

· The PM will request for human resources as per Infrastructure and Human Resource Request and Release process.

· PM ensures that required testing tools are identified and procured.

· All customer supplied materials will be controlled as per the Control of Customer Supplier Material (PR12)

1.4 Baseline

At the end of this phase, PM will baseline the SPMP and any customer supplied material (if applicable) as per the Configuration Management (PR04).

1.5 Progress Monitoring

The Progress of the project will be tracked as per Project monitoring and Control (PR03).

2 Analysis Phase

2.1 Analysis

2.1.1 Requirements from customer

If customer supplies a document specifying the test requirements, the Architect/PM/TL will review the same using the IRR(FRVER01) and the record will be kept in the Project folder as per Document Control procedure. If there is any discrepancy, the Review record, FRVER01 will be sent to the customer. After getting concurrence from the customer, PM will resolve the issue and the test requirement document will be baseline.

2.1.2 Requirements Specification developed by the Integra

In case the customer does not provide the test requirements Integra will prepare the test specification as follows:

1. PM will conduct a meeting with the Architect, TL to study the customer’s inputs (like code, User manual, etc). The minutes of meeting will be kept using FRMMR02.

2. TL will prepare the Software Test Specification (STS) by using Test Specification template (TPVAL02), during this activity all necessary clarifications are obtained from CPC.

3. The STS will be reviewed by PM and review findings will be recorded using IRR (FRVER01) , and the record will be kept in the Project Quality folder

4. After review, updated STS will be sent to the customer for approval.

5. Version numbering of STS will be done as per procedure for Document Control MP01

6. If the customer requests any change it will be incorporated in the STS and then it will be baselined as per Configuration Management (PR04). The record (e-mail, any other written communication) for Customer approval will be kept in Project folder.

7. Configuration Management procedure, PR-04 will be applicable throughout the lifecycle of the STS.

2.1.3 Acquisition of testing tools

PM ensures that required testing tools are acquired before the start of the testing. After acquiring these tools they are validated for their adequacy before use by PM/TL.

Input Output of Analysis Phase

2.2 Re-estimation

After completion of STS, PM will re-estimate the effort and the schedule of the project. Re-estimation will be also carried out whenever there is any change request, either from the customer side or by the project team itself. The SPMP will be modified to include all the affects arising out of re-estimation. The same person who approved the SPMP earlier approves it once again and the revision history will be updated in the document.

3. Testing Phase

➣ The testing team will execute the test cases using the STS as per Validation Procedure (PR08).

➣ The test report will be recorded using (FRVER02) / BCC. If the customer specifies a particular format /tool, this will be mentioned in the SPMP and the same will be used for recoding the test report.

➣ TMs will submit the test report to the TL.

➣ TL reviews the Test report (FRVER02) and will maintain the review records by using Inspection and review record (FRVER01)

➣ At the end of testing of every module (i.e. before sending the report to the customer), TL will ensure that False pass testing* is carried out and record will be maintained in the same format as above.

➣ TL will discuss with the team members about the test results and finalises the test report (FRVER02)

(*False pass testing – Test cases will be selected at random (5% or as specified in the SPMP) from the passed test cases and TL allocates them to other members of the team for testing and the results are cross checked.)

Input Output of the Testing Phase

PM will ensure that regular backup of the product work items is taken using the Backup, Recovery and Archival procedure (PR10).

4. Closure Phase

4.1 Packaging and delivery

➣ The PM will send the test report to the customer as per the contractual agreement.

➣ TL will generate DN, RN (available in PCC) and send it to the PM along with the package of test data and test results.

➣ The SQA team and DH will verify that all deliverables have passed through the required tests and verification processes.

➣ The Deliverables will be packed as per the contract agreement and sent to the customer.

➣ The PM will advise the commercial dept for raising the invoice as per the agreement.

Input Output of the Closure Phase

4.2 Customer Signoff

Customer Signoff is obtained after delivering the Test Report to the customer through either e-mail or written confirmation. These documents will be kept in the Project folder

4.3 Archiving

The Test Specification and Test report is archived or disposed as per the contract agreement. Refer to Backup, Restore and Archival Maintenance Procedure (PR10)

4.4 Project Closure

Infrastructure will be released as per Infrastructure and HR Request and Release (PR11)

Customer supplied material will be returned as per the Control of Customer Supplied Material (PR12)

The PM will collate Project metrics (FP11) and call for Project Closure meeting as per Project Closure Procedure PR15.

4.5 Customer Complaints

All customer complaints will be handled as per procedure for handling customer complaints (PR14) and will be tracked till closure.
