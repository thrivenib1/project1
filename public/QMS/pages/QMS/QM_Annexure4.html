SEPG Charter
QM-A04 Version: 3.80 Date: 1st August, 2016

1 Purpose

The QMS of the organisation is modified at regular interval as an effort towards achieving the organizational objectives of Quality Policy, through continuous improvement of the defined processes and practices. The QMS needs to be revised from time to time to keep pace with the changing business environment and to meet the requirement of business process of the organisation, to suite the prevailing scenario. In order to manage the change request of the QMS, a group called – SEPG (Software Engineering Process Group) is formed. This manual is developed to help the SEPG operation to achieve its objectives.

This Charter describes the objectives and scope of the Group’s activities, description of duties for which the SEPG is responsible, procedural requirements on the appointment of group members, requirements for the conduct of Group members and meetings, and other requirements. All SEPG activities must be conducted pursuant to this Charter.

2. Scope

The activities defined in this manual are aimed at achieving customer satisfaction by consistently providing conforming products and meeting customer requirements through application of the documented system and continual improvement of the QMS.

3. Objectives

The objective of SEPG:

(1) to analyze the current status of the established process relating to software engineering;

(2) to identify the improvement needs;

(3) to recommend changes to be made in the processes;

(4) to provide the management with alternate best process or best practices;

(5) to work closely with SQA to enhance awareness, understanding, appreciation, and practice of the established processes across all functions;

(6) to support, promote, and coordinate in the process of continual improvement;

(7) to develop coordinated plans for the implementation and management of these areas.

4. Members

The members of the SEPG are appointed by the SQA Head / MR, with the approval of the Management, from among the employees of the organization, with good experience and domain knowledge of Software Engineering.

A person from the SQA team would be appointed to the group as a non-voting member, to ensure relevant information exchange and consistent management, as appropriate.

· The total number of the SEPG members will be 10. Term of a member will expire after three years to encourage new members to come to the Group.

· One of these members will be selected as the Chairperson of the Group

· The Chairperson will function for a period of one year. It is encouraged that every member becomes Chairperson by turn.

· One third of the members will move out in the third year making way for new members to come to the Group every year then on.

· 60% required for process approval and Chairperson mandatory for such decisions.

5. Management Responsibility

The Management is committed to the full support, utilization, and enhancement of the SEPG. In order for the SEPG to achieve their full potential, the Management shall:

· provide sufficient support to allow the SEPG to operate efficiently and effectively;

· provide guidance to help SEPG operate efficiently and with consistency across the system.

6. Roles and Responsibilities

· The SEPG, in accordance with this Charter, shall advice the MR, regarding the change requirements of the QMS.

· SEPG shall act as an advisory body to the MR.

· SEPG may serve as a forum for consultation and deliberation among its members and as a source of consensus advice to the MR. Such consensus advice shall fairly represent the collective and individual views of the SEPG members.

· The Chairperson acts as the point of contact for the SEPG and the SQA.

· Any matter that a member wishes to raise to the attention of the SEPG shall be brought to the attention of either the MR or the SEPG Chairperson so that it might be placed on the agenda as a discussion topic. The MR and the SEPG Chairperson shall discuss topics for the agenda and agree that the topic is an appropriate issue before it may be placed on the agenda.

7. Roles and Responsibilities

· Chair: The Chairperson sets agendas for all SEPG meetings with the concurrence of the MR, presides over all meetings of the SEPG, signs all correspondence and documents authorized by the SEPG, and generally represents the SEPGs views.

· In absence of the Chairperson, the members elect a member to chair a schedule meeting and he/she performs the role of the Chairperson for that meeting.

· The SEPG meeting would be held atleast once a month.

· If any of the normal meeting day happens to be a holiday then the meeting would be held on the next working day at the said time and venue.

· Apart from these two fixed days the MR has the authority to call for a SEPG meeting.

· Attendance of all the members of the SEPG is a requirement for these meetings. If any member is not able to attend the same then prior information has to be given to the MR and the Chairperson of the SEPG.

8. Roles and Responsibilities

8.1 Working Groups and subcommittees

· The SEPG Chairperson and the MR may establish such subcommittees as necessary to fulfill its duties. Subcommittees shall be composed solely of members of the SEPG and shall be recognized as official sub-units of the SEPG. Subcommittees are subject to all requirements of this Charter.

8.2 Working Groups

· The SEPG may establish working groups with approval from the MR, for specific purposes or topics that need focused attention that cannot be accomplished by a subcommittee. Working groups may be composed of members of the SEPG and/or persons outside the SEPG (any employees of the organization). Working groups shall be chaired by a member of the SEPG and shall function under the purview of the SEPG. Working groups established by the SEPG to address specific issues shall be disband once the final advice on the particular matter is submitted to the SEPG.

9. Activities

1. The main activities of the SEPG are defined in the objective of this charter.

2. Any employee of the Organisation can generate a change request in the QMS.

3. A change request can also be generated either by SQA or by the SEPG itself.

4. All change requests must be recorded and properly documented.

5. A QMS change request could come in the form of a written document or e-mail, in the format given in the QMS (FR-53A).

6. On receiving the change request, the Chairperson of the SEPG and the MR will decide on the next date for the SEPG meeting and a formal meeting of the SEPG is convened.

7. The SEPG deliberates on the feasibility of the request and appropriate step is taken.

8. All SEPG meetings are recorded and maintained in accordance with the QMS procedure FR-55A and FR-55B.

9. The recommendation of the SEPG in regards to the change in QMS is forwarded to the MR, in a proper document format, for his/her review and approval of the CMG.

10.The MR has the right to send the recommendation back to the SEPG for further deliberation.

11.The SEPG shall provide advice directly to the MR via a written recommendation or a motion passed by the SEPG and reflected in the minutes.

12.Any information or advice resulting from discussions in subcommittees or working groups that is requested by the SEPG shall be presented to and considered by the full SEPG and incorporated into the SEPG’s recommendation to the MR.

10. Conclusion

1. The SEPG shall operate in pursuant to the terms of this Charter.

2. This Charter shall remain in effect from the date of signature.

3. Revisions to the Charter may be made as determined necessary by the SQA with input from the SEPG.
