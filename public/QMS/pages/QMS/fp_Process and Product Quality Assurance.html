12.1 Introduction

12.1.1 Purpose

This process defines the method used within Integra for providing Process and Product Quality Assurance (PPQA). The purpose of the PPQA process area is to:

  • Objectively evaluate performed processes, work products, and services against the applicable process descriptions, standards, and procedures.
  • Identify and document non-compliance issues.
  • Provide feedback to project staff and managers on the results of quality assurance activities.
  • Ensure non-compliance issues are addressed

12.1.2 Policy

The PQA ensures that the organizational set of standard processes is followed and organization goals are met in every project/ services and function. To ensure compliance the PQA will conduct audits at regular intervals.

Every project should be audited during its development lifecycle.

12.1.3 Scope

This process is applicable to all projects and product development, service delivery and support functions across the organization.

12.1.4 Entry Criteria

The basic criteria for initiating the PPQA process are:

  • Project kick-off
  • Established plan for the service delivery.

12.3.2 Tasks

The steps of the PPQA process are described below:

12.3.2.1 Objectively evaluate products and services

PQA, of the corresponding projects and support functions, will objectively evaluate work products.

12.3.2.2 Identify and document non-compliance

The PQA will prepare the audit findings and document them using:

  • Non-conformance report (FROPF01)
  • Auditor Notes (FROPF03)
  • In case of any disagreement between the PQA and the auditee, the MR will take the final decision and it will be binding on both.

12.3.2.3 Resolution of non-compliances

  • PQA will communicate non-conformances to the relevant stakeholders
  • The auditee will take necessary corrective actions and report the action taken to PQA
  • When non- conformances are not resolved locally; it will be escalated to senior management
  • All open non-conformances will be brought to the notice of the reporting manager of the particular project/service/function.
  • The MR will analyze the non-conformance data to identify all non-conformances, which results in process improvement.
  • The MR will track all non-conformances till closure.
  • The MR will prepare the consolidated audit report and present it to the senior management, auditee and PQA.

12.3.2.4 Establish records

  • The MR will review the audit records and approve for base lining the data as explained in the CMP for the PPQA function.
  • The MR will keep the following records in Process Asset Library after every PPQA evaluation cycle is completed.
  1. Organisational Process Data
  2. Risk Repository
  3. Organisational Metrics
  4. Productivity Data
  5. DAR Repository
