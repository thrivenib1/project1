Project/Product Documentation Management

Project/Product Documentation Management
ISO Clause: 7.1 PR013 Version: 3.00 Date: 17th May 2010

13.0 Introdution

13.1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define procedures for technical documentation.

13.1.2 Policy
Product document management process is applicable in the development of the all documentation relating to the products and services.

13.1.3 Scope
This procedure will be applicable to the technical writing function.

13.2 Stakeholders

13.2.1 Responsibilities

Sl. No. Role Responsibilities Authority
1. Project Manager Prepare schedule for technical writing and Review product documents Approve product documents
2. Head - Documentation Prepare schedule for the team
3. Technical writing Team Develop the documents
4. Head - Documentation Review the document Approve product documents
5. Head - Documentation Deliver the documents
6. Head - Documentation MLD and MLR
7. Head - Documentation Metrics collection and analysis

13.3 Task Details

13.3.1 Receive Customer Supplied Material
The PM enters the Planned Start Date and Planned End Date for the technical writing in the WBS of the project. The top-level tasks are assigned to the Head- Documentation. When the tasks are defined in the WBS, the Head-Documentation is informed by email.

13.3.1.1 Prepare a Schedule for the team

The Head-Documentation prepares a monthly schedule for the Technical Writer(s). This should include tasks outside the project’s user documentation.

Subtasks are defined to the team members in the WBS of the project/product.

If the WBS is not created by the PM for the technical writing activities, the project documentation tasks are included only in the monthly schedule prepared for the team.

The monthly schedule is mailed to the team members.

13.3.1.2 Change in Schedule
If the original schedule given by the PM changes due to any reason, the PM will modify the WBS, or notify the Head-Documentation team about this change by mail.
PM will notify similarly, the changes in the requirements to the Head-Documentation. Change in the estimated effort is also given by the PM.

13.3.2 Develop the Documents

  1. The technical writing team develops the required documentation.
  2. The progress is tracked in the WBS of the project/product or in the timesheets of PCC. For documents other than the project user documentation, progress is monitored through timesheets of PCC.
  3. The work items will be put in a specified location in the /product workspace. The PM specifies the location. This is usually done using CVS.

13.3.3 Review the Documentation
The project documents undergo the following review cycle before they are delivered to the Project Manager:

  • Self-review by the team member (No records are maintained for this but all errors are removed.)
  • Peer-review by any other team member and review records is entered in PCC/FRVER01.
  • Final review is done by Head-Documentation and records are maintained in PCC/FRVER01.
  • Fixing and verification is done after each review.

The PM is responsible for the review of the documents delivered by the technical writing team.

The review feedback is given to the Head-Documentation. The Head-Documentation ensures that the feedback is incorporated.

The changes to be made in the work items are informed by the PM to the Head-Documentation.

13.3.4 Deliver the Documents
Work items released to the project are baseline, and the PM is responsible for the configuration management. The PM can assign the responsibility to the Head-Documentation for coordinating with the CTL for check-in and check-out of Doc group’s work items.

13.3.5 MLD and MLR
The Documentation Head will maintain MLD (FRPDM01) and MLR (FRPDM02) for the function.

13.3.6 Metric Collection and Analysis
The Metrics pertaining to the function will be collected as per Metric Plan and analyzed (monthly) for corrective / preventive action.

13.3.7 General Activities

13.3.7.1 Track the Progress
The work of the technical writing team is progressed through PCC. Members of the technical writing team fill the time sheets of PCC for all their activities.
If the project documentation tasks are defined in the project WBS of PCC, the tasks are also tracked through WBS. In this case, those tasks are not entered in the timesheets.
Particularly for project/product documentation, both efforts in time and defect count are monitored.
For other documentation, effort in time is monitored.

13.3.7.2 Prepare Status Report
Timesheets entered by team members are reviewed by the Head-Documentation every week. Time spent by the Technical writing group on all activities other than user documentation for the projects will be tracked through Timesheets.
The Head-Documentation will prepare a monthly report and send to the Head Engineering.

13.3.7.3 Backup

A daily backup of all the work items of all the team members is put in the specified location on the Backup server by the Head-Documentation.
