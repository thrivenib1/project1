Sales Process
ISO Clause: 7.2 FP01 Version: 3.90 Date: 19th Oct, 2017

1.0 Introdution

1.1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to define the sales process of Integra Micro Software Services Pvt. Ltd. (Referred as IMSSPL). Scope for business with the customer can arise either by the customer or a sales partner asking for a Request for Information (RFI) from IMSSPL or by the Sales team of IMSSPL prospecting the potential customer asking for Request for Proposal (RFP) or any other member of IMSSPL. In either case the Sales team tracks the information and records it in internal tool Enquiry Management System (EMS). The updates on ongoing enquiries and new enquiries, if any, from the tracker maintained by sales team, are included in MIS Dashboard by the MIS Dashboard team, and the detailed report is shared with the top management and other concerned stakeholders like Business Unit (BU), Business Development (BD), HRD, SQA, Finance, and any other stakeholders on a fortnightly/monthly/quarterly basis.

1.1.2 Policy
The Sales process will be followed for soliciting requirements from the existing customers and prospects for providing software engineering services, fulfilling requirements and also for the purpose of maintaining relationship with the customers.

1.3 Task Details of Sales

1.3.1 Prepare Sales Plan
The sales plan FR-SAL3 is discussed and finalized with the IMSSPL Top Management (COO/MD), Business Unit Heads, Business Development Team, HRD, etc. in the beginning of financial year. The plan will detail the following:

  • Order and Revenue targets for the year (1st April YYYY to 31st March YYYY), preferably quarter wise and sales owner (Business unit heads) wise
  • Technology and domain focus areas
  • Geographical focus
  • Annual sales Plan (for the next financial year) review meeting is held with the Management in the beginning of every financial year, preferably in April and thereafter, on a quarterly basis.
  • The Sales Head will maintain summary of the review meeting.
  • The progress will be reviewed by the COO with all the key stakeholders in the Business Review Meeting (BRM) every quarter and Sales Review Meeting (SRM) on monthly/quarterly basis.
  • Sales plan can be changed on need basis in consultation with COO, BD and BU Head, depending on market scenarios, talent availability, and other business reasons. The changed plan will be shared all the other stakeholders.
  • Target for the financial year will comprise of both resource augmentation and projects, preferably under the following categories
    1. NCNW - New customer new work
    2. NCRW - New customer repeat work
    3. ECRW - Existing customer repeat work
    4. ECNW - Existing customer new work
  • The Sales Head with inputs from BD team, Business Unit Heads and COO will finalize sales Plan at the start of the financial year covering the following:
    1. Services
    2. Revenue
    3. Partnerships
    4. Critical success parameters
    5. Resources and support required
    6. Any other relevant information specific to business

1.3.2 Plan Sales Activities

  • Sales head will assign the targets to the sales teams in the beginning of the financial year, it will be reviewed and monitored monthly and the status will be shared with the Management.
  • The Sales team will carry out activities in consultation with Sales Head as per the sales Plan.
  • The activities planned for the quarter will be reviewed by the Sales Head.

1.3.3 Identify Prospective Customers

The Sales team will collect information on prospective customers by exploring personal contacts, through professional networks, conferences, and market research. The sales team will ensure that Sales Data like Customer database and prospective customer list with details such as address, contact information of key executives, customers area of specialization etc. are maintained in an excel sheet team and/or domain wise in a common repository with the Sales Head.

1.3.4 Generate Enquiries

Enquiries may come from:

  1. Prospective customer:

    The Sales team will record all the enquiries made by prospective customers. Enquiries received by BU, BD, COO and any employee will be forwarded to the Sales Team for further processing and tracked in EMS. If the enquiry from an existing customer is for resources, then it is processed as per FP-02. If it is a resource augmentation enquiry from a new customer, then the respective sales team member will work on agreements like NDA/NHA, Commercials, MSA, etc. in consultation with the COO, legal cell and Sales Head.

    Every enquiry would be considered as an opportunity and will be first evaluated by the sales team for feasibility. Further, based on identified focus areas, Risks, Technical competence, decision to drop an enquiry is taken in consultation with the business development, business unit and COO.

    For all qualified opportunities (fixed price project and ODC) a proposal is submitted and tracked till closure by the concerned sales team member. All opportunities, which are not qualified, would be dropped after logging the same and record maintained and presented to all stakeholders during the SRM/BRM/weekly meeting.

    The process of tracking enquiry will be as mentioned below:

    • The Sales team will enter each enquiry into Enquiry Management System (EMS)
    • All project enquiries received from existing and Prospective customers will be tracked using EMS by the sales team.
    • After discussing, a decision is taken whether to submit a proposal or not. Even the prequalified enquiries will be logged and tracked till closure.
    • The sales team member is responsible for ensuring timely submission of proposal against any RFP / RFC, and further follow-up in coordination with the business unit and business development team
    • The concerned Sales team member works toward getting an NDA signed before sharing of confidential information from either side, to the extent possible, wherever applicable.
    • The COO is the authorized signatories for NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) and MSA. However, sales head is also authorized to sign the same in the absence of COO.
    • An SOW is signed, if applicable and the authorized signatory for the same is concerned BU Head.

    The NDA should be signed at least before a statement of work is submitted to customer. In case of any deviation, the Sales team member takes prior approval from the BU head and/or COO. However, in cases of emergency, this process can be waived off in the business interests in order to reduce the turnaround time to the customer. However, the sales team member needs to keep a track and see that the NDA is signed subsequently.

  2. Existing customer:
    • All enquiries received from existing customers are recorded in EMS which is discussed during review meetings. The Business Development, Business Unit Head and Sales Head are responsible for ensuring timely submission of proposal against any RFP/RFQ and further follow-up.
    • The status of important enquiries is discussed and tracked on a weekly basis, with all stakeholders.
    • Enquiries are categorized as Existing Customer Repeat Work (ECRW), Existing Customer New Work (ECNW), New Customer New Work (NCNW) and New Customer Repeat Work (NCRW) and updated in the project enquiry tracker. This tracker is included in MIS Dashboard which is shared with the top management and other concerned stakeholders.

1.3.5 Propose Technical Solution

  • The Business Unit team proposes a technical solution for the enquiry.
  • Sales team shares the solution/technical proposal with the customer/ prospect.

1.3.6 Effort Estimate and Proposal

  • For any new enquiry, BU team studies the request, and estimate the effort, commercials and schedules and forward the same to the Sales team member.
  • The proposals could be in the form of an email, PPT, Excel and Word (PDF) formats depending on the time on hand to submit the proposal, customer preference, nature of work.
  • The sales team keeps record of the proposals for future reference in a common repository. The BU and BD take a call to submit two separate (technical and commercial) proposals or a single techno commercial proposal also.
  • The Sales team allocates the EMS number to the proposal. However, this is not applicable to tender submissions, budgetary, indicative, estimated proposals etc. and is only followed in the case of a formal complete proposal.
  • BD maintains the review records of the proposal.

1.3.7 Review and Approve Proposal
Proposal is finally reviewed and approved as follows depending upon the value:

  • Sales Head in consultation with BU Head, and the COO, if required approves proposals as per FRSAL4.
  • The approvals are obtained either through an email, call or a meeting scheduled for the same depending on the situation, to ensure timely submission of proposals, in case the concerned approving authority is not available at hand to review and approve the proposal.
  • The Sales team sends the approved proposal(s) to the customer and maintain the final copies in a common repository with the Sales Head.

1.3.8 Submit Proposal

The Sales team is responsible for ensuring timely submission of proposal against any RFP / RFQ.

1.3.9 Obtain Order / Contract

The Sales team member follows-up on the status of the proposal with the customer and tries converting the proposal into a contract/PO.

1.3.10 Review Contract

Documents like NDA/ SoW/ MSA etc. received from a new customer/prospect are reviewed from legal perspective by Sales team member, head, legal and COO. Input on specific clauses on need basis is taken from other stakeholders like HRD, Finance and Top Management.

The contract is reviewed as per FR-SAL2 to ensure that it is adhering to required guidelines and is as per the Technical and Commercial proposal submitted. Changes if any are intimated to customer and resolved. The final signed hard copies of the document are handed over to the commercial in charge for repository purposes and a scanned copy of the same is maintained with the sales team as well for records. POs if issued by the customer are checked for correctness with respect to the name of the organization, amount, validity, applicable taxes and any other specific terms and conditions as per agreed proposal. Issues if any on the same are resolved with the customer and the order is accepted.

1.3.11 Accept Order

The Sales team member and BU Head ensure the following after accepting an order:

For new customers, sending of IMSSPL's details like bank account for wire transfer where applicable, STP details for payment in foreign currency, procedure for duty free import of items into the bonded warehouse etc. as per agreed payment terms.

The confirmation to start the work could be in the form of proper purchase order, email, SoW. The acceptance of the purchase order is sent to customer after reviewing the required details. After obtaining one of the above documents from the customer, project initiation meeting is called and the project team is formed.

1.3.12 Amend Contract

Any changes to the scope, schedule and cost of the project is recorded by the PM. The amendments are submitted to the BU Head or the Sales Head for review and approval. Sales Team Member sends intimation to the customer informing the change of scope, schedule or cost of the project and obtain approval where applicable.

1.3.13 Conduct Project Initiation meeting

  • When Sales team wins an order, Sales team member calls for Project Initiation meeting after accepting the order.
  • The copy of PIN is shared by sales team member with SQA and BU Head for reference and monitoring.
  • The objectives of the initiation meeting are:
    1. To provide a briefing about the project
    2. To know the resources allocated for the project
  • The Sales team member invites the following members for the Project Initiation meeting:
    1. Business unit head
    2. Project Manager
    3. Sales head
    4. SQA
    5. COO, on need basis
    6. Any other member as needed

1.3.14 Project Interaction

  • The Sales team member and BU Head of the project take part in the Project Progress Reviews of the project scheduled by the SQA.
  • The Sales team member acts as the customer’s representative in such reviews.
  • The Sales team member and BU Head facilitate and interact with the customer in case of any deviation from the contractual agreement.
  • The Sales team member takes pro-active action for resolving delivery related conflicts, which might develop during the execution of the project.

1.3.15 Raising Invoice

  • The Commercial in-charge raises invoice to the Customer on the advice of the Sales team member /BU Head and a copy is sent to the Finance department. The status update is provided to the respective PM.
  • The Sales team member ensures that payments are collected from the customer as per agreed credit terms.
  • The Sales team member updates the payment status in the debtor statement sent by Finance Head monthly.

1.3.16 Project Closure (Commercial)

The BU Head/PM informs Sales head on completion of project. Sales team member works toward raising final and other pending invoices and collecting payments and ensuring project closure.

1.3.17 Maintain Sales Data
The Sales team maintains the following databases

  1. Target List: This list contains details about the business establishments and organizations that are potential customers to IMSSPL. Details such as company name, geography, key contact information, etc. are included in the database. This list is maintained sales team member wise.
  2. Enquiry List: Whenever an enquiry or a request for proposal comes from a prospective customer or an existing customer, an enquiry number is allocated in EMS.When an enquiry is converted to an order, status is updated in the EMS. The EMS is updated appropriately even if an enquiry is dropped.
  3. Contract List: A list of all contract signed during the financial year will be maintained by the Sales team.

1.3.18 Enquiry Management System (EMS)

The EMS is an internal tool used for logging and tracking all enquiries received. It is updated whenever a milestone is achieved for the enquiry like proposal submission, proposal acceptance/approval, and order won or enquiry dropped.

1.3.19 Customer satisfaction and repeat order

  • The Sales head and BU Head of the project ensure customer satisfaction
  • Customer complaints are logged into Customer Feedback Log (FRPMC04) span and tracked to closure by the Sales team and BU Head.
  • Business Unit Head and Sales team member work with the customer for repeat order and referrals based on the projects executed and business relationship.

1.3.20 MLD and MLR

The Sales team maintains MLD (FRPDM01) and MLR (FRPDM02) for the function.

1.3.21 Metric Collection and Analysis

The Metrics pertaining to the function are collected by sales team and presented on quarterly basis to all stakeholders through MIS dashboard.

Please refer to Metric Plan for all the metric related details.

The goals for the metrics will be reviewed every six months by the Sales Head and an RCA is done wherever applicable. The goals are revised if need be based on the outcome of the RCA

1.3.22 Retention and Disposition of Records

  • The sales team member maintains the records in a pre-defined folder tree structure defined in FRSAL5. The tree structure is refined across periods of time depending on the need.
  • Each sales team member maintains the folder tree structure for their data and the common repository of all combined is maintained with the sales head in a secondary backup device.
  • The sales team member takes the monthly back up of all the necessary sales data and retain it with the sales head as well as the sys admin team for future reference.
  • The documents are maintained in backup media for a period of three years or as required by the contract.
