Talent Management
ISO Clause: 6.1 & 6.2 FP05 Version: 3.80 Date: 1st August, 2016

5.1 Introdution

5.1.1 Purpose
Talent Management department (TM) is a common facility for IMSPL and IMSSPL. TM will undertake or coordinate training programs based on the training requirements raised by concerned managers, BU heads, employees or HR executives. All Business Units and functions can avail services of Integra’s TM Department.

Talent Management Department's vision is to "Create value by enhancing competencies of the employees in two areas namely, knowledge and skills."

5.1.2 Policy
The organization is committed to:

  1. Maintain physical infrastructure, hardware/software, and adequate qualified staff for internal training.
  2. Establish and implement training plans and schedules for the organization.
  3. Measure and improve the effectiveness of the trainings.
  4. Arrange 16 hours of training for the employees in a year.

5.1.3 Scope

Talent Management function is wholly owned by both IMSPL & IMSSPL organization. It as an integral part shall follow this process to assist the training needs of an employee of these organizations, facilitate his/her needs in the domain that shall lead to the accomplishment of individual department goals & achieving of organizational goals.TMF shall strictly adhere to the purpose of this process. TMF shall co-ordinate Induction Training & Fresher’s Training but, planning, & effectiveness monitoring is not in scope of this team.

5.2 Stakeholders

5.2.1 Roles & Responsibilities

In TM department, there are three roles: TM Head, TM Manager and TM Coordinator

5.2.2 TM Head

Talent Management Department will function under the Talent Management Head appointed to the post by Integra Management. TM Head will be responsible for talent management planning and operations.

5.2.3 TM Manager

The TM Manager will oversee the talent management operations and will report to the TM Head on a quarterly basis.

Responsibilities of TM Manager include reviewing and approving the monthly training plan, analyse effectiveness of training, derive and execute action points for improvement, address concerns raised by managers / BU Heads / HR executive and the training coordinator.

5.2.4 TM Coordinator

Training coordinator will be responsible for day to day activities of the talent management group. The TM coordinator will report to the TM manager on a monthly basis using FROTR22 Based on inputs from BU Heads, managers, HR and TM Head, the TM coordinator will prepare the monthly training calendar, which is further reviewed by TM Manager and approved by TM Head. The TM coordinator will facilitate the training by interacting with the requester, reporting heads, trainees and trainer. The training attendance is taken using template FROTR24 .

5.1.2 The Ecosystem

Talent Management group interacts with BU Heads, Managers, Employees and HR for getting the training requirement and facilitating the same.

BU Head and Managers can raise training requests, using FROTR01 , for team based on any project pre-requisite or team ramp up.

Employees can raise training requests for self ramp up using:

HR can raise training request for QMS training when a new employee is recruited. The induction training is conducted by the HR department. Performance appraisal system (PAS) allows the employee to request for training which will in turn benefit the organization. Both these requests are consolidated and sent by the HR every quarter using FROTR08 .

All the above mentioned requests are reviewed for validity:

  1. Check with HR whether the requestor is still in Integra
  2. Check with the requestor’s reporting head whether the request is still applicable.

All the valid requests will be consolidated by the TM coordinator into a monthly training plan using FROTR06 In case the training needs to be conducted immediately, the TM group initiates the same over and above the planned trainings for the current quarter.

The monthly training plan is reviewed and approved by the TM Manager.

Process mentioned in section 4 is then followed for execution of each of the training session.

5.1.3 The Process

5.2.1 Training Initiation

Training process will be initialized either on approval of monthly training plan or on receipt of immediate training request from BU Head / Manager (Refer section 3).

Trainings are planned only if minimum number of trainees is 5. However in case of urgency for training, this clause is bypassed.

5.2.2 Faculty Finalization

  1. Internal Trainer

    TM group maintains a list of trainers within the organization and the topics that can be taken by each of them. Using this list, the TM coordinator will check availability of trainer for the requested topic. This list is verified with BU Heads and updated every quarter.

  2. External Trainer

    In case no internal trainer is found for the requested topic, the training coordinator explores and identifies an external trainer. The external trainer will then be evaluated by internal stakeholders with respect to technical and communication skills. In case the external trainer is recommended by the management / TM Head, no evaluation is required.

    Once the trainer is finalized, TMG gets the quotation from the trainer / institution. Quotation will be approved by Talent Management Head and top management. Once the quote is approved, the training coordinator communicates with the trainer and confirms on the date and venue for the same.

5.2.3 Training Pre-requisites

TM coordinator makes end to end arrangements for the training such as venue hall booking, attendance sheet, training material, training detailed schedule, refreshments, feedback forms and other training specific infrastructure requirement such as PCs, laptops, software, etc.

Training materials are stored in the Talent Management repository.

5.2.4 Training

Training is conducted by the trainer on the planned date and venue. Attendance is marked by the TM coordinator for all the participants.

5.2.5 Training Feedback Management

Once training session is completed, feedback form (FROTR04) is distributed to all the participants. The same is duly filled by the participants and submitted back to TM coordinator.

5.2.6 Training Effectiveness Measurement

Feedback on effectiveness of technical training is collected for the following trainings:

  1. On job trainings, the skill data will be updated in the TM portal by the reporting head within the quarter. Any employee whose skill rating is below 3 (on a scale of 5) should be sent for classroom training.
  2. For classroom trainings, training effectiveness form (FROTR23) is sent to managers / reporting heads of all the participants. The same is duly filled by the managers / reporting heads and submitted back to TM coordinator.

The effectiveness of the training is further analysed by the TM Manager and action points are derived for improvement of the same.

4.3.9 Status Reporting

The Head-Recruitment will report the monthly status as per Recruitment Monthly Status Report for all the HR requests.,

The Head-Recruitment will report weekly status on RAB requirement. Also, the open and closed reports on RAB recruitments will be available in EMS Portal. The access for reports will be available to COO, BU head and Sales Head.l

5.2.7 Retention and Disposition of Records

  • TM group will maintain all the training records for a period of one year.
  • In January every year, records older than a year will be packed and suitably labelled and handed over to the General Admin In-Charge for storage.
  • In January every year, the General Admin In-Charge will identify all records, which are more than three years old and will destroy them as applicable.
  • The General Admin In-Charge will maintain the list of the stored item using Archival Register FRARC01 .

5.2.8 Course Content

5.2.9 Content Management

The course material including the presentation and any other material must be stored in the talent management repository.

5.2.10 Content Review & Updates

If training is planned for a repeated topic, TMG will get the existing course content reviewed by the trainer. In case of any changes / updates, the updated content is stored in the repository.
