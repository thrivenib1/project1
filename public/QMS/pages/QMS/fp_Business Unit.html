19.1. Purpose
The purpose of this document is to define the processes and procedures for the Business Units, functioning at Integra Micro Software Services Pvt. Ltd.

19.2. Policy

The Primary function of the Business Unit is to provide software engineering services to its customers and generate revenue for the company. As part of the above key functions, the Business Unit (BU) will support in pre-sale for providing the technical proposal, and own post-sale support, software services and support during warranty and annual maintenance period. BU will also be responsible for Project Management, Resource Management, Existing Customer Interactions and Relationship, Employee Engagements and Partner Management.

19.4. Nature of Business

IMSSPL Business Units are engaged in two diverse types of services namely:

  • Resource Augmentation Business (RAB): In this Business Area services are offered by IMSSPL�s resources under the overall guidance, control and monitoring of customers. The work allocation is also done by customers and resources follow customer calendar and processes. In RAB, most of the times the resources workout of customer�s premises, however, based on the need the resources also work out of IMSSPL�s premises.
  • Projects: The end to end software design, development and delivery responsibility of the services is undertaken by the Business Unit. The project is executed either from IMSSPL�s premises or customer�s premises or both based on the need as per agreed terms and conditions, including ODC/OTC

IMSSPL offers customers three engagement models:

Model Name Description
RAB - T & M

Project - (T&M) In this engagement, IMSSPL executes the project either from IMSSPL�s premises or customer�s premises or both based on the need and agreed terms. 19.5. Business Units Operations

The key objective of the Business Unit is to provide software engineering services and generate revenue. The performance is measured based on the revenue target set in the beginning of the financial year versus revenue achieved during the review period. Business Unit operations are mainly divided into:

  • Planning
  • Pre-Sales and Post-Sales Support for RAB and Projects
  • Delivery of Software Services
  • Customer Engagement
  • Employee Engagement
  • Resource Management & Deployable Pool management

At the beginning of the Financial Year the Business Units do the planning for BU in discussion with all the Senior Managers within the BU, Interact with Sales and prepare the projections. The projections will be presented to the COO for review and approval. The BU plan includes Revenue target, resource requirements, new initiatives, strategy to achieve the set targets, resource management, and Training needs. The qualification criteria for the RAB enquiry is based on the business unit focus areas, skill sets, and the experience level. In case, the enquiry does not fall under this criterion, it is treated as disqualified and dropped. The Qualification guidelines are given below:

For any strategic business enquiries, the COO will discuss with the BUs and accordingly, it is taken forward as a special case or dropped. The BU�s also must keep in mind about the Head Count, before deciding on any enquiry. The enquiries are tracked and reviewed during the weekly review meetings.

19.5.4. Qualification Criteria for Project Enquiries

The project enquiries are referred to BD, BU and COO by the sales team. The team further studies and qualifies the enquiry based on specific Requirements Specification guidelines. (Refer Requirements Guidelines from QMS - GLREQ01).The guidelines are based on technical aspects, feasibility, risk involved, past experience on similar projects and focus area.

19.6. Enquiry Allocation Criteria

The enquiries are allocated to the business unit based on the following criteria:

  • New customer new enquiry: In case of a new customer and new enquiry for projects, the opportunity is given to the business unit based on the focused area. However, decision in this regard is taken by the COO.
    • Ex1: Telecom, VAS, MFS, Mobile application development etc. is given to telecom BU
    • Ex2: Enterprises solutions, BPM, K2, Portal etc is given to Enterprise BU
  • New customer enquiry for RAB is also decided by the COO.
  • Existing customer new enquiry generally goes to the existing BU owner; there can be situation where the enquiry is passed to the other BU. The decision in this regard is taken by the COO.

19.6.1. Business Unit Resource Augmentation Process

Sales department is primarily responsible for generating enquires from the existing and prospective customers who may need Resource Augmentation (RAB) services, pro-actively to increase business, exploring all possible contacts. The BUs also refer prospects from their network.

  • The RAB sales team qualifies the enquiry as per the qualification criteria and guidelines, defined during the beginning of the Financial Year
  • The enquiry is forwarded by the RAB sales team to respective BU head and BU coordinator from the BU for suitable profiles, based on the enquiry allocation criteria.
  • The BU head further studies and qualifies the enquiry based on specific requirements and looks for the suitable resources in the pool.
  • BU head explores all possible options of referring in-house resources or taking engineers from external world (direct recruitment and through partners) if a particular requirement is not serviceable with the available pool of engineers.
  • Once the engineers are selected in interview, the deputation letter is issued by BU coordinator of the BU to the engineer and the engineer is sent to client location.
  • BU coordinator collects the periodic status report from the resources, reviews and gives the feedback, if any to BU. [SK1]
  • Sales team coordinates with the customer and deployed resources to gather leave and timesheet details and gets it approved by the customer for billing.
  • The billing calculation is done by sales team
  • BU Coordinator will raise the invoice based on the customer approvals received from the sales team and tax related inputs from finance.
  • Yearly salary/performance appraisal feedback is collected from the reporting client manager by the sales team and forwarded to the BU coordinator.

19.6.2. Dropping of Enquiries

The following steps will be followed to drop an enquiry and communicated to the sales team through an e-mail.

  • The respective business unit head decides on dropping enquiries based on focus areas, bench status, and other business-related reasons and the same is intimated to the sales team during weekly review meetings.
  • In case, any enquiry which is for more than 5 Sr. resources or 10 Junior resources or wherever the monthly billing is of 10 lakhs and above, the BU takes approval from the COO to drop the enquiry with justification.
  • Sales team can drop enquiries based on legal and commercial aspects.
  • COO can drop enquiries based on the terms and conditions and other priorities.

19.6.3. Approvals

The following criteria is followed for special approvals needed to service an enquiry which is not from regular focus area COO approval is needed in the following cases:

  • If the value of order is high (More than 5 engineers)
  • If there is a strategic initiative by either sales or BU
  • If the billing rates are very low or the focus area is out of chosen domains or the management wants to start business in a particular area

19.7.1. Deputation Letter

  • The BUC counsels the selected software engineers on the procedures, standards and code of conduct to be followed at the customer�s site after deployment.
  • A deputation letter and a brief mail regarding deployment with the customer is issued to the selected engineer by the BUC and RAST respectively, before deployment.
  • The printed copy duly signed by the concerned engineer is handed over to the HR by BUC and the same is maintained in the engineer�s personnel file for future reference.
  • All necessary customer specific supporting documents (like background checks, NDA) are completed with the help of HR wherever applicable.
  • After reporting to the concerned customer contact, the engineers are expected to update the RAST about their contact details at the customer�s premises including their reporting manager�s name, contact details and basic project/domain related information and the same is reflected in the master file.

19.7.2. Interaction Post Deployment

  • Deployed engineers work under the overall supervision and guidance of managers at customer premises, if onsite else reporting head at IMSSPL.
  • Engineers are also expected to keep the BU Heads updated about their work in brief without compromising on the confidentiality issues of the customer, through periodic status report and/or whenever asked for.
  • They are also expected to keep the RAST and BUC informed about any other relevant information at the customer site like change in project, manager, division, or any business-related information like any division being taken over, any regrouping etc.
  • The BU Head interacts on need basis for resolving issues with client project manager, project leader, and test lead etc with whom the engineers are deployed.
  • The RASH and/or RAST meets the vendor manager of customer regularly/ on need basis, to discuss overall status and also for growing business.
  • The BU head meets the concerned engineering manager of customer regularly/ on need basis, to discuss the progress and issues, if any to facilitate timely action for early resolution.
  • The outcome of the meeting and action items generated is discussed/ shared with the management at IMSSPL to resolve issues, if any timely and plan for future requirements, if any.
  • The BU head interacts with the software consulting engineers in getting feedback on issues related to technical help and training needs, if any and the same is collected through the Monthly Status Report FRRAB2A along with other required information.

19.7.3. Service Closure

The following is ensured at the time of closure of a service by an engineer.

  • The software engineer is expected to handover all the work-related documents and customer property to the concerned authorities at the customer's place.
  • Any hardware, software, firmware, etc., if any provided by IMSSPL is also collected back from the deployed engineer by the BU Head with the help of BUC.
  • The RAST obtains the no dues certificate from the reporting manager/ HR of concerned customer, through service closure template FRRAB6 through an email. In few cases, it is carried out as part of the exit process of the customer itself and the confirmation in this regard is obtained by the RAST.

19.7.4. Performance Appraisal

  • The RASM or RASC interacts with the customer to collect feedback on the performance of the deployed software engineers as per the appraisal cycle defined by the HR at IMSSPL.
  • The collected feedback is shared with the engineer by BU so that they are aware of the performance ratings received from their manager at customer�s site and an acknowledgement is taken from the engineer for the same. An appraisal meeting or telecon is conducted only if the concerned engineer has any concern regarding the feedback shared. This is then discussed and tracked to closure by the BU head.
  • In case of a feedback below desired level, the detailed discussion is held with the engineer by BU head to understand the causes and also to impress upon taking corrective action to overcome known issues and weaknesses. This is further tracked by the BU head by taking fortnightly feedback for one month.
  • BU head adds his/her comments wherever there is a contradiction in qualitative and quantitative ratings/feedback.
  • The feedback received is also entered into IMSSPL�s online appraisal system by BU head so as to enable HR to process as per the policy of the company.

19.8. Business Unit process for Projects

The BU team works with Business Development and Sales team on every new enquiry which is received. The enquiry is qualified based on the focus areas, customer expectation, and availability of the senior and junior resources to deliver the project.

  • The BU team works on the technical proposal which contains in scope, assumptions, proposed solution, development methodology, receivables, deliverables, schedules along with effort estimate and proposed acceptance criteria and submits it to Business Development team.
  • The sales team will provide the risks involved in the project from the sales and customer relations point of view. The sales team may also provide the mitigation plan and contingency for the same if needed.
  • The Business development team works on overall proposal in coordination with BU and Sales and sends it for review.
  • The BU team will work on the technical part of the project proposal which includes, In-scope, out-scope, technical assumptions, dependencies, proposed solution, receivable, deliverables, Team composition, project schedule, efforts estimate, UAT, warranty and Post warranty.� The BU team reviews mainly on the specific terms and conditions.
  • Once the pricing is worked out, the Sales team discusses with BU team for confirmation and upon agreement the proposal is submitted to the customer/prospect. Before proposal submission the Sales team verifies the content of the proposal with proposal release checklist CHIPRC01 from QMS
  • Project commences as per the terms mentioned in the proposal after receipt of purchase order/ contract is signed with the customer OR an approval from the management and followed by the project kick off. Based on the submission purchase order/ contract, the BU/Delivery Head, Project Manager, Architect, Sales team, Software Quality Assurance (SQA) and other key personals are identified.
  • Delivery Head is responsible for providing adequate resources (includes human and other resources), work environment for the execution of project.
  • Project Manager is responsible for planning, controlling and delivering the project besides motivating the team
  • Sales team solicits and obtain initial requirements from the customer with necessary approvals and sign offs, wherever required from customer.
  • The SQA ensures that the organizations QA process is adhered to for testing the developed solution as per agreed scope.

The SSR /DOU are reviewed by the DH and decision is taken to use one of the project execution models from the QMS (Quality Management System) suiting the requirements of the project. Once the Sales team receives the purchase order, account manager from Sale team will call for Project Initiation meeting Project Initiation Note (FRPPL01) after accepting the order followed by Kick-off meeting (TPPPL06) by the project manager

The organization is committed to follow the best practices of Project management for monitoring and controlling projects to meet Customer requirements by establishing project plan (e.g. PMP, CMP, QMP, and RMP) with Milestones.

To review the project health, there are monthly PPRs (Project Performance Reviews) presented to the Sr. Management along with the Project Key member (PM, Sr. Architect) and BU Head.

The PM informs Business Unit Head on completion of project. Sales team member works towards raising final and other pending invoices and collecting payments.The PM prepares Project Closure Report (FRPRC01). The minutes of meeting are recorded using FRMMR03. PM archives documents and source code as per the PR10 Backup, Restore and Archival Maintenance .

  • Enquiries are of various types from multiple sources and they are in turn processed by the Sales, Business Development and Business Unit teams within the company. Enquiries may also be received directly by others e.g. BU /BD /Others. However all enquiries will necessarily need to be routed to the sales team.� All information available at the time of receipt of an enquiry is passed on to the sales team.
  • �A specific POC (Point of Contact or Sales Owner) from the sales team is attached to every enquiry by the Head of Sales. In case of resource augmentation, the enquiry is routed to the RAB team in Sales, the corresponding BU head and BU coordinator. Specific enquiries may need the participation of multiple BUs, however a lead BU will be identified and associated. Based on the nature, type and size of the enquiry, a proposal team (PT) may be formed by the BD for further processing and creation of the proposal. This team comprises of identified members (Architects/Specialists) from the BU, BD and the Sales team.
  • Specific components of the proposal are processed as follows:
    1. BU members work on the technical documentation - architecture, implementation approach, project methodology, alternative solutions, implementation schedules, effort, team composition and milestones etc.
    2. The BD will identify/prepare organizational collaterals (case studies, company profile balance sheet, quality certificate etc.) to suit and augment the
    3. proposal.
    4. The BU coordinator must modify and update resumes of relevant employees to be attached to the proposal if required.
    5. Preparation of executive summary + covering letters are carried out by BD
    6. A proposal cost sheet is prepared including hardware cost, complexity of the project, risk factors, cost of finance etc. by BD.
    7. All technical aspects/deliverables are primarily owned by a BU and all Financial /Legal /Administrative � issues/aspects are dealt with by the sales team in consultation with COO.
  • [T N2] BU can decide, in consultation with technical teams, to drop a project proposal based on finding on technical viability and profitability. BD and Sales team can drop enquiries based on legal and commercial aspects. COO can drop enquiries based on the terms and conditions and other priorities.

19.9.1. Change Request (CR) Handling

All the new requests from the customer which are not part of the initially agreed Scope of Work or contract are treated as Change request from the customer. The project manager follows the CR Management process defined in the project management process. Once the technical feasibility and effort analysis are done, the change request information is routed through the sales team or the account manager. The account manager or the sales team interacts with customer in closing the same and getting a Purchase Order for the same.

Resource Planning

Business Unit provides resource plan to meet the target. Business Unit also requests for resources on need basis. The overall head count average is tracked and kept below the head count approved by the management for a Financial year by the BU and COO.

Resource Training

Business unit conducts following types of trainings for the resources.

  • Class room training (Technical Training): The BU recommends the resources for specific skills to the talent management department which organizes the training with internal or external faculty.
  • On Job training (Technical Training): The BU inducts the resources into the existing projects for a period of 1-3 months based on the experience of the resources and the need of the training and trains the resource on the Job.
  • Non-Technical Training: The BU nominates the engineers and managers for various conferences to get the awareness on Marketing�s skills, UI/UX, Project Management and Leadership skills.
  • In-house training: The BU nominates the engineers and managers for soft skill training like e-mail writing, communications skills, Leadership training, Process audit training etc.,

19.10.Business Unit Bench Management / Deployable Pool

BU requests HR department to recruit candidates with specialized skills or fresh graduates. Resources who have completed their client assignments report to respective BUs and become part of the bench. Resources on bench are also trained on new skills or programming languages to broaden their knowledge base to reduce their deployable time. The HR maintains skill database for all employees. Talent Management efforts also facilitate HR to capture the required data in an efficient manner. The manager associated with the bench resources from the BU side identifies the skills and coordinates with Talent Management team to provide the necessary training. At the end of each skill development session, TM department evaluates the resource and provides feedback to HR and the concerned BU. In discussion with the BU, the TM department then decides whether to re-train the resource or release the resource for RAB or projects. Also in some cases, TM may decide to discuss with HR other alternatives available for non-performing resources. The manager also undertakes internal projects to enhance the resource skills. Bench is also utilized for developing proof of concepts in specific areas.
