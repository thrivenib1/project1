Project Planning
ISO Clause: 7.1 PR02 Version: 3.40 Date: 14th February 2014

2.0 Introdution

2.1.1 Purpose
The purpose of Project Planning is to establish and maintain project plans that define project activities.

2.1.2 Policy
Integra is committed to using best practices of Project Planning to achieve the organizationâ€™s strategic goal. The Project Manager defines project planning parameters, develops the project plan for the project and obtains internal and external commitments to the project plan. Project planning processes includes planning project activities to set cost, schedule and quality goals for the project.

2.1.3 Scope
The Project Planning procedure is applicable for all the projects executed in Integra except projects of duration less than 2 weeks. Such projects of small duration can follow customized project planning procedures as decided by the project managers and approved by the delivery heads.

2.1.4 Entry criteria
Project planning commences as soon as the contract is signed with the Customer.

2.3.2 Tasks
The steps of the PP Process are described below:

Establish WBS

2.3.2.1 Estimate Scope of the Project

  • After the project kick-off, PM, Architect and PL will prepare the high level WBS for the project. The technical proposal is used as input to prepare the WBS.
  • A peer PM and Architect will review the prepared high level WBS and submit an IRR.
  • The high level WBS is updated based on the review comments and submitted to the Delivery Head for approval.

2.3.2.2 Establish Estimates of Work Product and Task Attributes

  • The PM and Architect will prepare the estimation after creating the high level WBS.
  • A peer PM/Architect will review the estimation sheet and prepare an IRR.
  • The estimation sheet is updated with the review comments and submitted to the Delivery Head for approval.

2.3.2.3 Manage Requirements Changes:

  • The PM will select the appropriate project life cycle model from the PAL based on the needs of the project.
  • Tailor the life-cycle model to meet a specific project needs. (Optional).
  • Conduct EPG meeting so that the tailoring and deviations to the organization standard process is approved. Record the selected life cycle model in the PMP.

2.3.2.4 Determine Estimates of Effort and Cost

  • The Architect and PM will prepare effort and cost estimates during the proposal preparation stage.
  • The PM, Architect and PL will prepare the detailed WBS to re-estimate the effort, cost and schedule for the project at two important internal milestones:
    1. Approval of the Software Requirement Specification (SRS) document
    2. Approval of the High Level Design Document (HLD).
  • The PM, Architect and PL will update the detailed WBS and re-estimate effort, cost and schedule whenever there is a change request raised by the customer or by the project team.
  • The PMP is updated to capture changes in the effort, cost and schedule.

2.3.2.5 Reconcile Work and Resource Levels

  • Using the detailed WBS, the PM will reconcile the project plan to reflect available and estimated resources.
  • The PM will identify differences in the estimated requirements and actual project allocation.
  • Reconcile the estimates versus the budget. Renegotiate specific project parameters with management and customer.
  • After reconciling, update the detailed WBS and PMP to record the changes.

Establish PMP

2.3.2.6 Establish the Budget and Schedule

  • The PM will prepare the budget and schedule for the project using the detailed WBS.
  • The PM will record the project schedule and budget in the PMP.

2.3.2.7 Identify Project Risks

  • The PM along with the project team will identify, analyze and prioritize risks in the project. The risk repository is one of the inputs to identify the project risks.
  • The PM updates the risk management plan.

2.3.2.8 Plan for Data or Configuration Management

  • PM will record the requirements to ensure privacy and security of the configuration items and archival mechanism in the configuration management plan. The contract and proposal are important input items to plan data management.
  • PM will identify the project data to be collected and distributed.

2.3.2.9 Plan for Quality Management

  • The PM along with the project team will plan the standards, benchmarks, optimizing processes, planning quality activities that will prevent non-conformance to requirements, setting quality goals and identifying project metrics against which team performance can be measured.
  • The PAL is one of the inputs in preparing the quality plan. Other inputs are the contract and technical proposal documents.
  • The PM prepares the quality assurance plan.

2.3.2.10 Plan Project Resources

  • PM will identify staffing requirements and other infrastructure requirements for the project. The PM uses the WBS to plan the project resources.
  • The resource requirements for the project are recorded in the PMP.

2.3.2.11 Identify Knowledge and Skills

  • PM and Architecture will identify and plan for the knowledge and skills needed to perform the project. The SRS, WBS and skills database are few of the input documents used to plan the knowledge and skills required.
  • The knowledge and skills required for the project are recorded in the PMP.

2.3.2.12 Plan Stakeholder Involvement and Communication Management Plan

  • PM will identify the stakeholders and their involvement in the project. Project Charter and PIN are two of the inputs used to identify stakeholder and determine their involvement in the project.
  • The PM will prepare the communication management plan after analysing stakeholder involvement in the project.

2.3.2.13 Establish the Project Plan

  • The PM will establish and maintain the overall project plan.
  • The PMP includes the auxiliary plans of RMP, QAP and CMP.
  • The established PMP and auxiliary plans are submitted to the Delivery Head for review and approval.

2.3.2.14 Review Plans that Affect the Project

  • The Delivery Head will review all the plans (PMP, RMP, QAP, CMP) and record review comments using IRR.
  • The PM will update the PMP according to the review comments received.
  • The updated PMP is reviewed and approved by the Delivery Head.
