Control of Documents

ISO Clause: 4.2.3 MP01 Version: 3.00 Date: 17th May 2010

Introduction

1.1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this procedure is to ensure that only relevant copies of a document are available for use and the obsolete copies are withdrawn from points of use.

1.1.2 Policy

All projects will maintain the project document as stated in this process document for ease of operation and clarity.

1.1.3 Scope

This procedure will be applicable for creation and distribution of all project documents.

1.1.4 Entry criteria

The entry criterion for this process is project kick-off.

1.3.1.2 Version numbering system

1.3.1.2.1 Assigning version number

· The author is responsible for assigning the correct version number for the work item.

· The version number is assigned based on the nature of the revision.

· The PM is responsible for deciding the nature of the revision.

· The format of the version number is XX. Yy where XX is the release number, Y is the major revision number and y is the minor revision number.

1.3.1.2.2 Major change

If the change is a major one, the minor version number is initialized to zero and the major portion of the version number is incremented by one, i.e. the version number shall be incremented by 00.10.

1.3.1.2.3 Minor change

· If the change is a minor one, only the minor portion of the version number is incremented by 00.01.

· If the minor number reaches 0.09, then the change is reflected as a major change. The major version number would be incremented by one and the minor version number would be initialized to zero.

1.3.1.2.4 Draft version number

The version number of the first draft version of the work item will be 00.01. Every time there is a revision/change, the version number would be incremented in steps of 00.01.

1.3.1.2.5 Changing version number

· Every time the work item undergoes a revision, a new version number must be assigned to the work item.

· The new version number depends upon the nature of the change - minor change or major change

1.3.1.2.6 Internal approval

The first internally approved version of the work item would be numbered as version 00.50. i.e. the first internal baseline.

1.3.1.2.7 Release to the client

The version number of the work item will be changed to 01.00 before releasing to the customer. The work item has to be internally approved and baselined before releasing it to the client.

1.3.1.2.8 Tracking changes during review cycles

Whenever an earlier baseline work item is revised, the following guidelines shall be followed:

i) Add the suffix "0RCn" to the version number, indicating that this is a "Review Cycle". The number "n" shall be 0 for the first cycle of review. For every cycle of review this shall be incremented by 1.

ii) If the changed document is rejected, or if the change is abandoned, the original baseline document is retained with the earlier version number.

iii) Once the revised work item is accepted after 'n' cycles of review or testing, the new version number shall be incremented by 00.10 or 00.01, depending on the type of change, i.e. Major or Minor and "RCn" is dropped.

1.3.2 Maintain Master List of Documents

· The PM, with the help of CTL will prepare and maintain the MLD (FRPDM01) in PCC.

· The MLD will have all the approved documents listed with their latest version number, release date and number of copies made.

· Whenever a new version of the document is released, the new list will replace the old.

· The MLD will list both the "Internal" and "External" documents such as customer supplied documents and user manuals of the tools used for project execution.

1.3.3 Approve Documents

· The documents will be reviewed and approved as per the Verification procedure, PR07 Verification.

· The approval authorities for documents will be as described in the SPMP for the project.

Hard copy

The "Master" copy of an approved document will have the signature of the approving authority.

Electronic Media

In case of soft copy, the "Master Copy" will be available in the "Controlled" library. Refer to Configuration Management procedure (PR04).

1.3.4 Document Storage and Archival

The PM will maintain the master copy of project documents. Project documents will be archived as per the Backup, Restore and Archival Maintenance (PR10) procedure.

1.3.5 Issue Control

The PM will issue/control copies of the project documents.

Electronic Media

· Controlled copies will be deployed on designated file servers and appropriate access permissions will be provided to the project team members.

· The PM could issue the document through E-mail.

· The soft copies distributed through mail are declared as "Uncontrolled copies".

Soft Copy Print Out

The soft copy print outs are declared as "Uncontrolled" unless stamped as “Master Copy” and signed by the approving authority.

1.3.6 Recall/dispose Obsolete Documents

Hard Copy

When a new version of the document is released and issued, earlier "Controlled" copies are recalled and marked as “Superseded”.

Electronic Media

Project documents are controlled through SVN as per Configuration Management Procedure (Configuration Management PR04 ).

1.3.7 Modify Document

· Changes to approved documents will be initiated based on the Change Request Form (FRCON01).

· The changes to the document will be reviewed as per the Verification (PR07) procedure.

· The Revision History section in the document will be updated for such changes.

1.3.8 Control of Customer Supplied Document(s)

Customer supplied documents will be controlled as per Control of Customer Supplied Material (PR12).
