Guidelines for Selection Criteria for Internal Auditors

GLOPF01

Version: 3.10

Date: 23rd Sept 2011

 

Internal Auditors must have the following skills.

1.     Generic Knowledge and Skills.

2.     Quality Specific Knowledge and Skills.

3.     Personal Attributes.

4.     Education, Work Experience, Auditor training and Audit Experience.

Generic Knowledge and Skills

1.     Apply audit principles, procedures and skills.

2.     Plan and organize the work effectively.

3.     Conduct the audit in a timely manner.

4.     Prioritize and focus on matters of significance.

5.     Collect information through effective interviewing, listening, observing and reviewing.

6.     Verify the accuracy of collected information.

7.     Confirm the sufficiency and appropriateness of audit evidence to support audit findings and conclusions.

8.     Assess those factors that can affect the reliability of the audit findings and conclusions.

9.     Understand the appropriateness and consequences of using sampling techniques.

10.  Record audit activities through work documents.

11.  Prepare audit reports that are clear and concise.

12.  Hold information confidential.

13.  Communicate effectively, either through personal linguistic skills or through the support of a competent interpreter.

14.  Application of management systems to different organizations.

15.  Application of the reference documents to different audits.

16.  Contracts and agreements.

2. Quality Specific Knowledge and Skills

1.     Quality terminology.

2.     Quality management principles and their application.

3.     Quality management tools and their application (for example statistical process control).

4.     Sector specific terminology and process and practices.

3. Personal Attributes

1.     Ethical-fair, truthful, sincere, honest and discreet.

2.     Open minded- willing to consider alternative ideas or points of view.

3.     Diplomatic � tactful in dealing with people.

4.     Observant � constantly and actively aware of physical surroundings and activities.

5.     Perceptive � instinctively aware of or able to understand and adapt to situations.

4. Education, Work Experience, Auditor Training and Audit Experience
