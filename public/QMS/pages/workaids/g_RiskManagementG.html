Risk Management Guidelines

1.    Introduction

Risks are those events or conditions that may occur and whose occurrence has an adverse effect on the project. Risk management is a discipline for living with the possibility that future events may cause adverse effects. Project Risk Management is the systematic process of identifying, analyzing and responding to project risks. This guideline is developed to help manage risks in a project, if and when they occur.

1.1      Purpose

The purpose of this guideline is to:

·        Identify the potential risks in the project

·        Plan to mitigate the risks

·        Monitor and mitigate the risks during the execution

1.2      Scope

This guideline is applicable to all projects. The risk management activities are divided into:

·        Risk assessment

·        Identification

·        Analysis

·        Prioritization

·        Risk management planning

·        Risk monitoring

2.    Project risk management

Risk in a project is a probabilistic event. It may occur, or it may not occur. Risk management in a project involves risk assessment and risk control.

2.1      Risk assessment

Risk assessment consists of three components; risk identification, risk analysis and risk prioritization.

2.1.1. Risk identification

Identifying risk for the project involves in listing down what might go wrong in the project, which would create a risk situation. The project risk identification should be carried out before starting the project. The PM should present and discuss the risks identified for the project during the project kick-off meeting. Risks are categorized as follows:

·        Technical risks

·        Schedule risks

·        Effort risks

·        Resources risks

·        Cost risks

·        Others

Methods for identification of risks:

·        Use of checklist

·        Brainstorming,

·        Meetings and

·        Interviews

A risk repository of commonly occurring risks is available in the PCC. The PM can use this repository to identify and mitigate the most commonly occurring risks for the project.

2.1.2. Risk analysis

The identified risks for the project gives the possible events that can hinder the project meeting its defined goals. However, the consequence of different risks could be different. Therefore, all the risks have to be analyzed to understand the impact of the risk.

2.1.3. Risk prioritization

Risks prioritization involves analyzing the possible effects of the risk event. The impact could be direct loss or loss due to business opportunity, employee morale and so on. Risk prioritization is done based on the risk exposure factor.

Risk exposure, RE, is defined as:

RE (risk exposure) = Probability ® * Impact ®  (1)

Probability ® is the probability of occurrence of a risk R and Impact® is the loss due to the risk R.

The values of probability and loss factors are found from the risk repository for a risk R. Once the risks are prioritized we can decide what mitigation action must be taken for each of them.

As risks are probabilistic events, they do not occur frequently hence historical data available for quantitative risk analysis is low. Hence, the risk prioritization will be based more on experience rather than on hard data from the past.

The probability of risk occurring is categorized into three categories: low, medium and high. The probability range for these three categories is given below:

Probability

Range

Low

0% – 30%

Medium

31% – 70%

High

71% – 100%

For ranking the effects of a risk on the project, a unit of impact is introduced. The consequences can be measured in monetary terms. However, to keep risk management simple, the risk impact is measured on a scale of 1 to 10. The risk effects are rated as low, medium and high. The following table gives the impact ranges:

Impact

Range

Low

1 – 3

Medium

4 – 7

High

 8 - 10

With these ranges and ratings a simple method for risk prioritization can be defined as follows:

1.      For each risk, assign a probability rating.

2.      For each risk, assess the impact on a scale from 1 – 10.

3.      Compute the RE (risk exposure) for each risk and rank them in descending order.

4.      Select top few risks for mitigation and tracking.

Use risk repository data for risk probability and mitigation.

3.    Risk Monitoring and Control

Once the risks have been identified and prioritized, we can decide what risks to be controlled and tracked based on the risk exposure factor. Risk control activities involve:

·        Risk mitigation plan

·        Risk monitoring and tracking

3.1      Risk Management Planning

For managing the risk proper planning is essential. The Project Manager will identify the mitigation strategy for the identified risks. Use the risk repository database for the mitigation plan and discuss the plan in the project kick-off meeting. Mention the risks identified for the project and the mitigation strategy for each risk in the SPMP of the project.

3.2      Risk Monitoring and Tracking

Risk monitoring and control is the process of keeping track of identified risks, monitoring the residual risks, identifying new risks and ensuring the execution of the risk mitigation plan. Risk prioritization and the consequent planning are based on the risk perception at the time of project initiation. Since, risk is a probabilistic event and also depends on factors external to the project, the risk factor could change from time to time during the project life cycle. Hence, risks need to be re-evaluated during the execution and appropriate mitigation strategy should be work out. The risks of the project should be evaluated at the end of every phase of the life cycle.

The purpose of the risk monitoring is to determine if:

·        Risk responses have been implemented as planned.

·        Risk responses have been effective, or new responses have to be developed

·        Project assumptions are still valid

·        Risk exposure has changed from its prior state, with trend analysis

·        Risks have occurred that were not previously identified.
