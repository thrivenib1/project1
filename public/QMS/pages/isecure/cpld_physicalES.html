Physical and environmental security policy
ISMS Clause / Control: A 11 ICPo17 Version: 1.00 Date: 10th April, 2017

17.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

17.4.1. General

17.4.2. Physical and Environmental Security Policy

17.4.2.1. The physical perimeter of the site locations, storage locations and equipment housing locations should be secured.

17.4.2.2. There should be no gaps in the perimeter or areas where a break-in could easily occur.

17.4.2.3. The external walls should be of solid construction and all external doors should be protected against unauthorized access using appropriate control mechanisms such as locks, etc.

17.4.2.4. A physical manned security should be available 24X7 in all exit and entry points

17.4.2.5. All prominent locations and areas where critical assets / equipment is sited should be under 24 X 7 CCTV surveillance day and night.

17.4.2.6. General buildings, offices, rooms and facilities should be protected by ensuring that all doors and windows remain closed and locked while unoccupied.

17.4.2.7. Manned security and reception desks should be used to restrict access to offices of supporting and servicing units containing confidential information.

17.4.2.8. Critical equipment and information should be placed in secure areas (such as filing cabinet rooms, printing rooms, computer rooms, and data centers).

17.4.2.9. Access to secure areas should be restricted to authorized personnel only.

17.4.2.10. Personnel should only be aware of the existence of, or activities within, a secure area on a need-to know basis.

17.4.2.11. The exterior roof, walls and flooring of the site should be of solid construction and all external doors should be suitably protected against unauthorized access with control mechanisms.

17.4.2.12. Personnel of contracted third party service providers should be given restricted access to secured rooms and this should always be under supervision unless CCTV camera equipment monitoring the room.

17.4.2.13. Entrance of secure areas must be controlled by physical recording assigned to onsite personnel or authorized staff only

17.4.2.14. Visitor log shall be used to maintain a physical audit trail of visitors' activities. Retain this log for a minimum of three months. Onsite personnel or authorized staff should verify the identify and documents the following of visitors:

  • Name
  • Organization or company represented
  • Time in and out
  • Purpose of visiting
  • Signature of visitor
  • Assets bought by the visitor
  • Permission approval for Assets
  • Government ID proof reference
  • Phone Number

17.4.2.15. Appropriate security measures should be applied to equipment placed in shared areas and area off-site equipment, taking into account the various risks of working.

17.4.2.16. Appropriate power protection (e.g. Uninterruptable Power Supplies, Redundant Power Feeds), adequate fire protection, proper heating and cooling must be installed to prevent interruption of service or availability.

17.4.2.17. Equipment's sitting should prevent any risk to the equipment due environmental factors such as rain, heavy wind, etc.

17.4.2.18. Equipment sitting must be as per manufacturer's indications if available.

17.4.2.19. Suitable intruder detection systems should be installed to national, regional or international standards and regularly tested to cover all external doors and accessible windows; unoccupied areas should be alarmed always; cover should also be provided for other areas, e.g. computer room or communications rooms;

17.4.2.20. Preferably, the information processing facilities managed by the organization should be physically separated from those managed by external parties.

17.4.2.21. Specialist advice should be obtained on how to avoid damage from fire, flood, earthquake, explosion, civil unrest and other forms of natural or man-made disaster.

17.4.2.22. Controls should be adopted to minimize the risk of potential physical and environmental threats, e.g. theft, fire, explosives, smoke, water (or water supply failure), dust, vibration, chemical effects, electrical supply interference, communications interference, electromagnetic radiation and vandalism environmental conditions, such as temperature and humidity, should be monitored for conditions which could adversely affect the operation of information processing facilities;

17.4.2.23. Lightning protection should be applied to all buildings and lightning protection filters should be fitted to all incoming power and communications lines;

17.4.2.24. Equipment processing confidential information should be protected to minimize the risk of information leakage due to electromagnetic emanation.
