Access Control Policy
ISMS Clause / Control: A 9.1.1 ICPo5 Version: 1.00 Date: 10th April, 2017

5.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

5.4. Procedure

5.4.1. General

The policy for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

5.4.2. Access Control Policy

5.4.2.1. An exception to this policy is allowed, where there is a business need to be exempted from this policy (since it is too costly, too complex, adversely impacting other business requirements) a risk assessment must be conducted being authorized by ISMS MR.

5.4.3. Logical Access Control

5.4.3.1. All Access to the company's resources residing on the organization's network and organization's information processing assets must be controlled strictly via user credential login's only as stated in Password Management Policy.

5.4.3.2. Such user credential login must be provided only on the principle of

  • Need to know basis.
  • Segregation of duties;
  • Level of access required.

    • 5.4.3.3. User credential login must be allocated by the System Admin upon induction of the new person in the organization.

    5.4.3.4. User credentials must be managed as per organization's password management policy.

    5.4.3.5. Logical access permissions must be established and maintained by the IT department, on user to user basis.

    5.4.3.6. Such permissions must be reviewed on annual basis or on change of user role, whichever is earlier, and review record must be maintained by the IT department.

    5.4.3.7. Network diagrams must always be up-to-date, that also indicates the logical access controlled enabled.

    5.4.3.8. Information systems network shall be divided into logical segments or domains based on:

    1. Access requirements,
    2. Value and classification of information stored or processed in the network.
    3. User activities logs must be tracked and stored by the IT department.

    5.4.4. Physical Access Control

    5.4.4.1. All Physical access control must be controlled by the General Admin team.

    5.4.4.2. General Admin team must maintain the matrix of the access card numbers issued to user and the permissions granted for physical access.

    5.4.4.3. All critical physical entry and exit points must be monitored via CCTV camera's 24*7 day and night.

    5.4.4.4. The General Admin team should grant physical access to restricted area to a user based on role requirement only, and upon termination of role the same must be revoked.

    5.4.4.5. All entries to the offices, working spaces, information processing facilities and its housing must be controlled for physical movement via, access card entries.

    5.4.4.6. All user movements logs must be maintained.

    5.4.4.7. All Emergency exits must be accessible by all personnel in the organization. However, it shall be used only during an emergency.

    5.4.4.8. Stock record of the access cards and its status of use must be maintained by the General Admin on real-time basis.

    5.4.4.9. Unused access cards must be de activated and stored in a secured access controlled storage point.

    5.4.4.10. All physical file storages / storage racks for storage of equipment and devices are controlled for physical access via lock and key.
