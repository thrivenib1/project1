6.4.1. General

The policy for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

6.4.2. Management of secret authentication information of users

6.4.2.1. Any Exemptions to this policy is allowed, where there is a business need to be exempted from this policy (since it is too costly, too complex, adversely impacting other business requirements) a risk assessment must be conducted being authorized by ISMS MR.

6.4.2.2. Allocation of secret authentication information must be controlled through a formal management process (user account creation).

6.4.2.3. Users are required to maintain their own secret authentication information, they are provided initially with secure temporary secret authentication information`, which they are forced to change on first use.

6.4.2.4. Temporary secret authentication information must be unique to an individual and must not be guessable.

6.4.2.5. Default vendor secret authentication information must be altered following installation of systems or software.

6.4.2.6. Accountability of such information must be solely with the user.

6.4.2.7. Secret authentication information must be adhered to the password management system established by the organization.

6.4.2.8. The Secret authentication information must not be shared with anyone, unless exempted by a formal risk assessment, and due to the need of business requirements.
