20.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

20.4.1. General

The policy for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

20.4.2. Communication Security policy

20.4.2.1. Email account should be used primarily for business related purposes; personal communication is permitted on a limited basis, but non-organization related commercial uses are prohibited.

20.4.2.2. The organization's communication networks and systems should never be used for personal use.

20.4.2.3. The Communication channels of the organization should not be shared with any strange third party personnel unless the business relationship for the organization demands for its purpose.

20.4.2.4. Unauthorized transmission of company trade secrets, confidential information or privileged communications shall be strictly prohibited.

20.4.2.5. All e-mail activities should be monitored.

20.4.2.6. Email system shall not to be used for the creation or distribution of any disruptive or offensive messages, including offensive comments about race, gender, hair, color, disabilities, age, sexual orientation, pornography, religious beliefs and practice, political beliefs, or national origin.

20.4.2.7. Employees who receive any emails with this content from any source should report the matter to their IT team immediately.

20.4.2.8. Users are prohibited from automatically forwarding organization email to a third-party email system (public domain email).

20.4.2.9. Individual messages which are forwarded by the user to a third-party email system must not contain organizations confidential or above information, unless the third-party email is having a business relationship with the company.

20.4.2.10. Using a reasonable amount of organization resources for personal emails is acceptable, but non-work related email shall be saved in a separate folder from work related email.

20.4.2.11. Sending chain letters or joke emails from the organization's email account is prohibited.

20.4.2.12. Employees shall have no expectation of privacy in anything they store, send or receive on the company's email system

20.4.2.13. All e-mails shall carry the ''confidentiality disclaimer''.

20.4.2.14. Telephony services of organization must not be used for personnel purposes or for any other purpose other than business activities.

20.4.2.15. All wireless infrastructure devices that connect to an organization network should be controlled by wireless monitoring system enabled in the organization.

20.4.2.16. As a rule, the following applies to requests for fax and analog lines:

  • Fax lines are to be approved for departmental use only.
  • No fax lines will be installed for personal use.
  • The fax machine must be placed in a centralized administrative area designated for departmental use, and away from other computer equipment.
  • A computer which can make a fax connection is not to be allowed to use an analog line for this purpose.

20.4.2.17. Use of an analog/ISDN fax line is conditional upon the requester's full compliance with the requirements listed below. These requirements are the responsibility of the authorized user to enforce always:

  • The fax line is used solely as specified in the request.
  • Only persons authorized to use the line have access to it.
  • The line will be used solely for organization's business, and not for personal reasons.
  • All downloaded material, prior to being introduced into organization's systems and networks, must have been scanned by an approved anti-virus utility (e.g., Virus Scan) which has been kept current through regular updates.

20.4.2.18. Requesting an Analog/ISDN Line

  • Once approved by a HOD, the individual requesting an analog/ISDN line must provide the following information to General Admin Head.
  • a clearly detailed business case / explanation of why other secure connections available at cannot be used should be mailed.
  • the business purpose for which the analog line is to be used,
  • the software and hardware to be connected to the line and used across the line,
  • what external connections the requester is seeking access.

20.4.2.19. Cabling security must be ensured and verified for all communication cables, record of such verifications should be maintained.
