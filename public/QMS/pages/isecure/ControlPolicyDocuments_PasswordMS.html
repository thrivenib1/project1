Password Management Policy
ISMS Clause / Control: A 9.4.3 ICPo8 Version: 1.00 Date: 10th April, 2017

8.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

8.4.2. Password Management Policy

8.4.2.1. An exception to this policy is allowed, where there is a business need to be exempted from this policy (since it is too costly, too complex, adversely impacting other business requirements) a risk assessment must be conducted being authorized by ISMS MR.

8.4.2.2. Password management systems must be interactive and must ensure quality passwords.

8.4.2.3. Passwords must be changed regularly, for every 90days, to avoid unauthorized access to information and information systems.

8.4.2.4. All system-level passwords (e.g., root, enable, Windows Administrator, application administration accounts, etc.) must be changed on at least a quarterly basis.

8.4.2.5. All user-level passwords (e.g., email, web, desktop computer, etc.) must be changed at a frequency automatically popped by the domain control, or as when there could be a risk for authorized access sought by the user.

8.4.2.6. All users must select strong passwords.

8.4.2.7. Strong passwords have the following characteristics:

  1. Contain at least three of the five following character classes:
    • Lower case characters
    • Upper case characters
    • Numbers
    • Punctuation
    • ''Special'' characters (e.g. @#$%^&*() _+|~-=\` {}[]:";' <>/ etc)
    • Never repeat the same password in six changes.

    8.4.2.8. Do not share account passwords with anyone, including administrative assistants or secretaries. All passwords are to be treated as sensitive, confidential organization information.

    8.4.2.9. Passwords must never be written down or stored on-line without encryption.

  2. Do not reveal a password in email, chat, or other electronic communication.
  3. Do not speak about a password in front of others.
  4. Do not hint at the format of a password (e.g., "my family name")
  5. Do not reveal a password on questionnaires or security forms

8.4.2.10. If someone demands a password, refer them to this document and direct them to the ISMS core team.

8.4.2.11. Age of password: in automated system (domain) it is set as ''90 calendar days'' & manual system employees are request to change every ''90 calendar days''.

8.4.2.12. Privilege passwords can be created based on specific request from the PM. The PM can log the request through PCC. The system administrator will create the privilege password for the user on the advice of the PM.
