ISMS Clause / Control: A 12.1.2 ICPr6 Version: 1.00 Date: 10th April, 2017

6.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

6.4.1. General

The procedure for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

6.4.2. Change Management

6.4.2.1. Only changes to critical IT assets or system configuration polices that can disrupt the business activities of the organization, follows this process of change management.

6.4.2.2. Regular changes which are required for system updating are exempted from this process

6.4.2.3. Documentation changes should be as per Procedure for the control on documents established in the organization

6.4.2.4. For compliance purposes, all communications need to be in writing, i.e. by email, meetings need to have minutes taken etc.

6.4.2.5. This communication will be retained by the IT Team and filed with the Change Documentation relating to the change. For this reason, verbal requests and authorization are not acceptable.

6.4.2.6. Complete a Change Request Form and submit to the system administrator of the IT team in the organization

6.4.2.7. Enter as much detail as possible in the Request Details section. If this change will affect other departments please enter the names of the Department.

6.4.2.8. The Change Request form will be reviewed by the system administrator who will gather additional information, add Department Managers deemed to be affected and arrange meetings.

6.4.2.9. Then the system administrator creates a Specification detailing exactly what is being changed, which is sent to all Stakeholders. The Specification should incorporate all the requirements.

6.4.2.10. The Change Stakeholders carefully review the Specification to ensure that all the requirements and their interests are covered.

6.4.2.11. The Change Stakeholders will need to approve the specification by email.

6.4.2.12. The system administrator will conduct a risk assessment based on the agreed specification. They will check all the systems and processes affected by the proposed change and list any risk areas. The Risk Assessment is used to create a change Recommendation to ensure that any risk to the business has been identified and mitigated. The Recommendation will include items such as specific training and testing requirements. A copy of the Risk Assessment, including the recommendation, will be sent to the Stakeholders.

6.4.2.13. The Implementation Plan details all the stages that are required in order to successfully manage the change and include a Test Plan and Roll Back Strategy. In more complicated changes this may also include a project schedule and timeline.

6.4.2.14. The change implementation plan will be approved by the IT Head

6.4.2.15. Once the Implementation Plan has been approved it is vital that the staffs in each department are made aware of what needs to happen, when and by whom.

6.4.2.16. The IT Manager:

  • Notifies affected Staff of the change and assigns actions and makes them aware of the Roll Back Strategy.
  • Ensures that Staff who have been allocated Test Actions have copies of the Test Plan and are aware that all test documentation is to be retained.
  • Liaises with other Stakeholders and the system administrator to ensure that all aspects of the change are progressing as planned.

6.4.2.17. To minimize unnecessary disruption, ensure that the plan is followed as closely as possible and any issues are highlighted to the system administrator as soon as possible. The system administrator wills co-ordinate communications between all the Stakeholders.

6.4.2.18. Ensure all staff follows the Implementation Plan.

6.4.2.19. Once a change has been implemented it is important that the situation is reviewed to identify any problems that could be prevented in future or improvements that could be made.

6.4.2.20. The Stakeholders will carry out a Post Implementation Review one month after the change has been promoted to Live (unless problems or issues present themselves more immediately).

6.4.2.21. The ISMS core team will review Change Documentation and follow up material quarterly. The minutes and action points of these reviews are held on file with the Change Documentation.

6.4.2.22. The Internal and External Auditors will examine the Change Management Documentation on a half yearly and End of Year basis and their comments and recommendations will be acted upon.

6.4.2.23. Acceptance testing programs and related criteria are established for new information systems, upgrades and new versions, through the process of testing by the IT Team, after any change being carried out to the system, and the same is updated in the change history register.

6.4.2.24. Automated updates should not be used on critical systems as some updates can cause critical applications to fail.

6.4.2.25. A software update management process should be implemented to ensure the most up-to-date approved patches and application updates are installed for all authorized software.
