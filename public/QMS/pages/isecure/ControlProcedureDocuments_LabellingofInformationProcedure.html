1.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

1.4. Procedure

1.4.1. General

The procedure for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

1.4.2. Labelling of Information

1.4.2.1. This procedure needs to be adhered across all functions and departments of the organization. All documents available in the organization are appropriately identified and labelled based on its sensitivity, , and it is applicable to all documents either originated or stored.

1.4.2.2. Each Process user should follow the procedure for control of documents (FP14 QMS Document Management), established in the organization for creation, updating / revision of the documents established for the process carried out in the organization.

1.4.2.3. If a document is received from any external party (customer or supplier), then such documents, based on the nature of information contained in it, should be labelled as described in this procedure.

1.4.2.4. Each document, should be labelled as follows:

  • The Footer of each document shall describe the ''Document labelling correlating to the Information Classification criteria in the organization''.
  • If Footer provision not possible, since it's a received document, then the ''PDF'' document that is received the first and last page of the document, the ''TEXTMARK'' correlating to the Information Classification criteria in the organization should be placed.
  • If documents that are received cannot be edited for labelling purpose, then the file of the document should be selected and the properties of such document should be visited and the information labelling correlating to the Information Classification criteria in the organization should be written.
  • If the document is a hard copy then correlating to the Information Classification criteria in the organization, appropriately STAMP on each page of document must be done or on the FILE holding the documents STAMP must be placed.
  • If the documents are stored in a removable media (USB Pen drive, CD, etc.), then correlating to the Information Classification criteria in the organization, appropriately the media needs to be labelled, it is recommended that encryption to be followed in all such media where possible. <

1.4.2.5. Only after labelling of documents, a document can be approved in the organization.

1.4.2.6. Any document not labelled is publicly available information, which does not require the need of any labelling.

1.4.2.7. When needed, the sharing restrictions of the document also can be indicated during the labelling of document / information.

1.4.2.8. The Labelling of Assets, are in the form of Asset Tag allocated to each asset.
