16.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

16.4. Procedure

16.4.1. General

The policy for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

16.4.2. IS Policy for Supplier Relationship

16.4.2.1. Terms and Conditions surrounding risk mitigation of a supplier's access to organization's assets / information must be documented, reviewed, and agreed upon by the organization and the specific supplier in supplier agreement / work order / purchase order released to the supplier.

16.4.2.2. All suppliers that access, process, store, or provide various IT components / information of organization must agree with organization's security requirements around suppliers' relationship with the assets / organization information.

16.4.2.3. Types of information access must be defined that diverse types of suppliers will be allowed, as well as monitoring and controlling the access.

16.4.2.4. Types of obligations applicable to suppliers must be defined to protect the organization's information.

16.4.2.5. Security requirements agreements for suppliers must also include details on addressing risks surrounding handling, processing, and communicating of assets or services.

16.4.2.6. The risks to the organization's information and information processing facilities from business processes involving external parties must be identified and appropriate controls implemented before granting access.

16.4.2.7. Legal and regulatory requirements, including data protection, intellectual property rights, and copyright, etc. must be clearly identified and addressed in supplier agreements.

16.4.2.8. All suppliers including service providers in the organization must enter into the NDA / Confidentiality agreement with the organization prior to rendering service.

16.4.2.9. Professional ethics must be maintained by suppliers and their personnel when carrying out the business relationship with the organization.

16.4.2.10. A process must be developed to monitor and assess the service delivery of a supplier to ensure it is meeting appropriate business and security requirements, as well as meeting any contract or SLA requirements.

16.4.2.11. A change management process must be in place to address any alterations to an existing policy, including documentation of said change and ensuring it is in alignment with business processes and follows appropriate re-assessment of risk involved, if necessary.
