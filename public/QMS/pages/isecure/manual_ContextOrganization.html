4.0 Context of the Organization

4.1 Understanding the organization and its context

The organization has determined external and internal issues that are relevant to its purpose and that affect its ability to achieve the intended outcome(s) of its information security management system.

The intended outcome(s) of the organisation information security management system is the ''Preservation of the Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability'', of the Information, Information processing facilities and support utilities that assist in achieving its intended outcome.

The External Context refers to the external environment in which the organization seeks to achieve its objectives, such as:

  1. the cultural, social, political, legal, regulatory, financial, technological, economic, natural and competitive environment, whether international, national, regional or local;
    • Such as acts / laws of countries: STPI compliance, Environmental Protection Act, IT act, Copy right act, etc.
  2. key drivers and trends having impact on the objectives of the organization; and
  3. relationships with, and perceptions and values of external stakeholders.

    4. The Internal Context refers to the internal environment in which the organization seeks to achieve its objectives, such as:

  4. governance, organizational structure, roles and accountabilities;
  5. policies, objectives, and the strategies that are in place to achieve them;
  6. the capabilities, understood in terms of resources and knowledge (e.g. capital, time, people, processes, systems and technologies);
    • ISMS awareness, Use of the advanced technologies and applications.
  7. information systems, information flows and decision-making processes (both formal and informal);
  8. relationships with, and perceptions and values of, internal stakeholders;
  9. the organization's culture;
  10. standards, guidelines and models adopted by the organization; and
  11. form and extent of contractual relationships

The Organisation's intended outcome(s) of its information security management system is to ensure the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of Information in a secured environment.

4.2 Understanding the needs and expectations of interested parties

The organization has determined:

  1. The interested parties that are relevant to the information security management system; and
  2. The requirements of these interested parties relevant to information security. INTEGRA, considers various interested parties, such as Client(s), Statutory Bodies, Suppliers, Stakeholders etc, in terms of their need, expectations, and have documented this information

Reference:

  • PR05 Requirements Development,
  • PR06 Technical Solution,
  • Records of CHREQ01 Requirement Specification Checklist,
  • Records of FRMMR03 Minutes of Meeting.

4.3 Determining the scope of the information security management system

The organization has determined the boundaries and applicability of the information security management system to establish its scope.

When determining this scope, the organization has considered:

  • the external and internal issues referred to in 4.1;
  • the requirements referred to in 4.2; and
  • Interfaces and dependencies between activities performed by the organization, and those that are performed by other organizations.

Information security management system related to the scope is available as documented information in IS_01_Scope.

4.4 Information security management system

The organization has established, implemented, maintains and continually improves an information security management system, in accordance with the requirements of the ISO 27001 International Standard.

The organisation has taken care into consideration of the following to ensure that the ISMS are established:

  1. By establishing all policies and procedures required by this international standard
  2. By nominating an ISMS Core team to closely monitor the ISMS in the organisation.
  3. By adopting the risk assessments, subjecting all critical assets risks of the organisation and addressing them appropriately.
  4. By establishing and implementing IT security policies and corresponding resources for its implementation
  5. By ensuring the resources are available in the organisation to establish and monitor its ISMS, such as:
