10.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

10.4. Procedure

10.4.1. General

The procedure for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

10.4.2.1. All installation to any device must be disabled to users and enabled only to IT department through system administration systems

10.4.2.2. The important IPR covered in this procedure is the use and installation of software's in the organization.

10.4.2.3. This installation includes for:

  • Office desktop computers.
  • Company laptop computers.
  • Telecommuter home computers (provided by the company).

10.4.2.4. Most of the software titles on organization's current software list are not freeware; therefore, the cost of software is a consideration for most titles and their deployment.

10.4.2.5. It is the goal of the IT department to keep licensing accurate and up to date. To address this, the IT department is responsible for purchasing software licenses for the following software categories:

  • Desktop operating system software
  • Productivity tools package
  • Internet software
  • Accessories
  • The other software categories (workgroup-specific titles) are the purchasing responsibility of the workgroup in which they serve. However, the application(s) are still installed and supported by the IT department.

10.4.2.6. To control costs, licensing costs are a factor in the decision-making processes that go into client software planning and request approval.

10.4.2.7. If a user is to request software for their computer, the proper method will be to send a request to the IT manager.

10.4.2.8. A response is guaranteed within one business day via e-mail. If the Urgent option is selected or an in-person appearance occurs, a solution may be delivered at the first possible time. All in-person or ''walk-in'' requests are logged by a manual entry into the support request system to track licensing needs and costs.

10.4.2.9. When operating platforms are changed, business critical applications are reviewed and tested to ensure there is no adverse impact on organizational operations or security.

10.4.2.10. Modifications to software packages are discouraged, limited to necessary changes and all changes shall be strictly controlled, by only IT Team in the organization, through permission by ISMS MR.
