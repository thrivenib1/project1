7.0 Support

7.1 Resources

The organization has determined and provided the resources needed for the establishment, implementation, maintenance and continual improvement of the information security management system.

7.2 Competence

The organization has:

  1. Determined the necessary competence of person(s) doing work under its control that affects its information security performance;
  2. The minimum competence of all permanent employees in organisation shall be
  3. Awareness of Security Threats and Vulnerabilities
  4. IT Security Control Policies and Procedures.
  5. The minimum competence of all any third-party personnel working in organisation shall be
  6. Do's and Don'ts related to information security requirements
  7. Ensure that these persons are competent based on appropriate education, training, or experience;
  8. Where applicable, take actions to acquire the necessary competence, and evaluate the effectiveness of the actions taken; and
  9. Retain appropriate documented information as evidence of competence.

The required competence in the organisation is mapped in the Skill Assessments carried out in the organisation and such information is available in the Job Descriptions of the Roles in Organisation.

Reference: Role Details - Responsibility and Authority description, Candidate Evaluation, Skill Map Assessment, Resumes of Employees.

7.3 Awareness

Persons doing work under the organization's control are aware of:

  1. The information security policy;
  2. Their contribution to the effectiveness of the information security management system, including the benefits of improved information security performance; and
  3. The implications of not conforming to the information security management system requirements.
  4. All such awareness is provided during the ISMS trainings provided in the organisations at planned intervals or during inductions provided to the Employee's.

Reference: Training Calendar, Training Need requisitions, Training and Feedback Record, Training effectiveness record.

7.4 Communication

The organization has determined the need for internal and external communications relevant to the information security management system including:

  1. on what to communicate;
  2. when to communicate;
  3. with whom to communicate;
  4. who shall communicate; and
  5. the processes by which communication shall be effected.

Reference: ISMS Communication Matrix INTEGRA/ISMS/D03

7.5 Documented information

7.5.1 General

The organization's information security management system includes:

  1. documented information required by this International Standard; and
  2. documented information determined by the organization as being necessary for the effectiveness of the information security management system.

Documented Information Available in the Organisation Include:

  • Scope of the ISMS
  • Information security policy and objectives
  • Risk assessment and risk treatment methodology
  • Statement of Applicability
  • Risk treatment plan
  • Risk assessment report
  • Definition of security roles and responsibilities
  • Records of training, skills, experience and qualifications
  • Monitoring and measurement results
  • Internal audit program
  • Results of internal audits
  • Results of the management review
  • Results of corrective actions
  • Logs of user activities, exceptions, and security events

7.5.2 Creating and updating

When creating, and updating documented information the organization ensure appropriate:

  1. identification and description (e.g. a title, date, author, or reference number);
  2. format (e.g. language, software version, graphics) and media (e.g. paper, electronic); and
  3. review and approval for suitability and adequacy.

Reference: Procedure for ISMS MR activities INTEGRA/ISMS/SOP02.

7.5.3 Control of documented information

Documented information required by the information security management system and by this International Standard is controlled to ensure:

  1. it is available and suitable for use, where and when it is needed; and
  2. it is adequately protected
  3. For the control of documented information, the organization address the following activities, as applicable:
  4. distribution, access, retrieval and use is controlled through SVN - collaboration and document management platform from SVN;
  5. storage and preservation, including the preservation of legibility, is identified and documented in the Master list of Records.
  6. control of changes (e.g. version control); is exercised by providing the change control only to MR, and such information is also documented in the Master list of Documents.
  7. retention and disposition, is identified and documented in the Master list of Records.

The Following is achieved through the system of SVN and Login Credentials which are access controlled to ensure from loss of confidentiality, improper use, or loss of integrity is taken into consideration on the documented information.

Documented information of external origin, determined by the organization to be necessary for the planning and operation of the information security management system, is identified as appropriate, and controlled.

Reference: Procedure for ISMS MR activities INTEGRA/ISMS/SOP02.
