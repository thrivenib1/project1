8.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. 8.4.1. General

The procedure for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

8.4.2. Changes to Supplier

8.4.2.1. Changes to supplier can be due to supplier SLA evaluation or supplier performance evaluation or need for new services from supplier changes to supplier includes:

  • enhancements to the current services offered;
  • development of any new applications and systems;
  • modifications or updates of the organization's policies and procedures;
  • new or changed controls to resolve information security incidents and to improve security
  • changes and enhancement to networks;
  • use of new technologies;
  • adoption of new products or newer versions/releases;
  • new development tools and environments;
  • changes to physical location of service facilities;
  • change of suppliers;
  • sub-contracting to another supplier.

8.4.2.2. The ISMS Core team member must consult with the process owner and ISMS MR and review the new agreement Terms and Conditions.

8.4.2.3. The acceptable use policy of the organization must be communicated to the supplier, considering the changes made to the services of supplier.

8.4.2.4. If necessary, risk assessment must be carried out to ensure that the changes caused to supplier services, does not lead business risks and compromise of the information security management system.

8.4.2.5. The responsibilities or terms of conditions of employment should be reinstated as per the change carried out.

8.4.2.6. The supplier agreement should be reviewed with necessary risk owners and then finalized with the supplier.

8.4.2.7. Records of such changes should be maintained
