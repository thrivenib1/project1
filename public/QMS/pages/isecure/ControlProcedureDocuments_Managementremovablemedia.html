Management of Removable Media Procedure
ISMS Clause / Control: A 8.3.1 ICPr3 Version: 1.00 Date: 10th April, 2017

3.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

3.4. Procedure

3.4.1. General

The procedure for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

3.4.2. Management of removable media

3.4.2.1. Removable media device includes:

  • CDs.
  • DVDs.
  • Optical Disks.
  • External Hard Drives.
  • USB Memory Sticks
  • any other storage media

3.4.2.2. Prominent identification and labelling of the media needs to be done, in correspondence with the organization's ISMS policy.

3.4.2.3. Organization should prohibit the use of all removable media devices, but if needed for business requirements, then should comply with this procedure.

3.4.2.4. All removable media devices and any associated equipment and software must only be purchased and installed by system admin department of the organization, in accordance with the organization purchase procedure/policy.

3.4.2.5. To minimize physical risk, loss, theft or electrical corruption, all storage media must be stored in an appropriately secure and safe environment.

3.4.2.6. Each user is responsible for the appropriate use and security of data and if required for not allowing removable media devices, and the information stored on these devices, to be compromised in any way whist in their care or under their control.

3.4.2.7. All data stored on removable media devices must, where possible, is encrypted. The contents of any re-usable media that are to be removed from the organization should be made unrecoverable.

3.4.2.8. Media should be stored in their containers when not in use, and should not be left in drives unnecessarily, as this can cause both heat and mechanical damage.

3.4.2.9. Media should not be allowed to encounter liquids, dust, or smoke, and should not be exposed to either extreme heat or direct sunlight.

3.4.2.10. Drives should be maintained and cleaned on a regular basis, to prevent damage to media.

3.4.2.11. Magnetic media should be kept away from potential sources of magnetic fields, including electrical equipment.

3.4.2.12. It is important to regularly check all stored media (ideally on a six-monthly basis). Media should be visually checked for signs of damage, and a random sample checked for readability.

3.4.2.13. Damaged or faulty removable media devices must not be used.

3.4.2.14. Virus and malware checking software must be operational on both the machine from which the data is taken and the machine on to which the data is to be loaded, through removable media.

3.4.2.15. Disposing of Removable Media must be complying with the procedure for Disposal of Media.

3.4.2.16. If no longer required, the contents of any re-usable media that are to be removed from the organization should be made unrecoverable.

3.4.2.17. Where necessary and practical, authorization should be required for media removed from the organization and a record of such removals should be kept to maintain an audit trail;

3.4.2.18. Where removable media is sent external parties for data recovery, it must be ensured that NDA / Confidentiality agreement is entered between external parties and risk assessment of the same is carried out.
