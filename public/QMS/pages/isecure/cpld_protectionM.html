18.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

18.4.1. General

The policy for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

18.4.2. Protection form malware policy

18.4.2.1. Anti-malware application must be installed in all systems, and information processing facilities that offers real-time scanning protection to files and applications running on the target system.

18.4.2.2. If the target system is a mail server, it MUST have either an external or internal anti-virus scanning application that scans all mail destined to and from the mail server.

18.4.2.3. Anti-Malware software's consist of both "detection" and "containment" features, this is must be utilized always (process users must not disable) for ensure the safety and security of information system resources.

18.4.2.4. The system utilized in the organization should ensure that comprehensive logs of anti-malware are maintained.

18.4.2.5. The Antimalware software must be patched on all systems and updated on real-time basis, such patches should be controlled by the organization.

18.4.2.6. Unless exempted by a risk assessment, and with the written permission from ISMS MR, no user / system can have option for disability (temporary and permanent) of the malware system.

18.4.2.7. Logs of the performance of the anti-malware system must be maintained by the IT department.
