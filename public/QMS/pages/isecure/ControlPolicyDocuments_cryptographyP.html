9.4.1. General

The policy for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

9.4.2. Policy on Cryptography use

9.4.2.1. All removable media, including memory sticks, must be encrypted.

9.4.2.2. All Laptop hard drives must be encrypted.

9.4.2.3. Portable electronic devices such as Blackberry's, Android Tablets / iPads and Android phones/ iPhones, etc must be protected by passwords/PIN numbers.

9.4.2.4. Wi-Fi Protected Access encryption is mandatory for all wireless networks.

9.4.2.5. E-mails (including attachments) must be encrypted whenever sensitive or critical data is contained or attached that contains customer information or organization's classified data.

9.4.2.6. Statute information being communicated electronically must be authenticated by use of digital signatures;

9.4.2.7. All Terminals and Terminal connections used by the organization must be encrypted with strong keys as required by customer / order specific conditions.

9.4.3. Policy on Cryptography keys

9.4.3.1. Cryptographic algorithms, key lengths and usage practices should comply to the national laws of India.

Encryption Act link & Section:

https://www.dsci.in/taxonomypage/602 (Section 84A - IT act)

9.4.3.2. All cryptographic keys should be protected against modification and loss.

9.4.3.3. Secret and private keys need protection against unauthorized use as well as disclosure.

9.4.3.4. Issuing and obtaining public key certificates must be approved by national laws of India and only by the Corporate.

9.4.3.5. The systems established in the organization should control distributing keys to intended entities, including how keys should be activated when received and also storing keys, including how authorized users obtain access to keys.

9.4.3.6. Only the corporate of the organization is allowed for destroying keys.

9.4.3.7. ISMS MR must maintain the log of all cryptographic keys used in the organization, indicating its nature of encryption.

9.4.3.8. Cryptographic keys must be generated and stored in a secure manner that prevents loss, theft, or compromise.
