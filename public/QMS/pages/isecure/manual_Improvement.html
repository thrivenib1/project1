10.0 Improvement

10.1 Non-conformity and Corrective Action

When nonconformity occurs, the organization:

  1. reacts to the nonconformity, and as applicable:
    1. acts to control and correct it; and
    2. deals with the consequences;
  2. evaluates the need for action to eliminate the causes of nonconformity, in order that it does not recur or occur elsewhere, by:
    1. reviewing the nonconformity;
    2. determining the causes of the nonconformity; and
    3. determining if similar nonconformities exist, or could potentially occur;
  3. implement any action needed;
  4. review the effectiveness of any corrective action taken; and
  5. make changes to the information security management system, if necessary.

Corrective actions taken are appropriate to the effects of the nonconformities encountered.

The organization retains documented information as evidence of:

  1. the nature of the nonconformities and any subsequent actions taken, and
  2. the results of any corrective action.

Reference: Procedure for ISMS MR activities INTEGRA/ISMS/SOP02.

10.2 Continual improvement

The organization continually improves the suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the information security management system, by taking necessary corrective actions on all its Non-conformities, regularly reviewing its Risk Assessments and taking necessary risk treatments, Internal Audits and Management reviews of the Information Security Management System.
