ISMS Management Representative Procedural

ISMS Clause: 6.1.2; 6.1.3

SOP2

Version: 1.00

Date: 10th April, 2017

 

2.0    Purpose

i.     To define controls for creating and updating documented information on all types of documents used in the organization.

ii.    To defined controls for the identification, storage, protection, retrieval, retention and disposition of documented information.

iii.   To define a process for controlling the non-conformance situation and Initiate Corrective Action to eliminate causes of existing nonconformity to prevent recurrence and to eliminate potential causes of nonconformity to prevent occurrence.

2.1. Scope

i.     To provide system for planning, scheduling, conducting internal audits and follow-up actions to verify that ISMS related activities and results meet the planned arrangements and that they are effectively implemented.

ii.    Be followed for planning, conducting the management review meeting and to determine/ monitor the effectiveness of ISMS.

iii.   This is applied to all processes, documents, data and records concerned with the ISMS and including documents of external origin to ensure the availability of relevant documents with concerned personnel at the place of work.

2.2. Terms and Definition

i.     NIL

2.3. Abbreviations

i.     MR: Management Representative

2.4. Responsibilities

2.4.1.         Primary Responsibility: Management Representative.

2.4.2.         Contributory Responsibility: ISMS Core Team, Process Heads.

2.5. Process

2.5.1.         Creation and Updating of Documents

2.5.1.1.    The Management Representative controls the documents being used in the Organization as required by the ISMS.

2.5.1.2.    The Documentation structure, Nomenclature, Preparation - review and approval system of the documents used the Organization is as indicated in Annexure 1.

2.5.1.3.    All Documents, based on the hierarchy identified in Annexure 1 - are signed off using Digital signatures / rather than physical sign of the role in the document.

2.5.1.4.    The documents are identified by adopting unique document numbering system as defined Document Template.

2.5.1.5.    All documents are given a suitable provision, which indicate Version status of the documents.

2.5.1.6.    Versions of all documents begin with ''1.0''

2.5.1.7.    All the documents will have a version number specified in the following format:

2.5.1.8.    Version x.yy where "x" represents the issue status number and "yy" represents the revision number.

2.5.1.9.    The MR ensures that relevant versions of applicable documents are made available at points of use.

2.5.1.10. The Management Representative maintains the 'Master Copy' of all ISMS Documents is stored in Server and access is provided to MR only. The PDF Version of ISMS documents are uploaded on the Server and accessibility is provided to all employees of the Organization- for reference purpose only.

2.5.1.11. The MR will issue control copies of the ISMS documents in PCC.

2.5.1.12. Electronic Media

2.5.1.13. All Documents in electronic media will be controlled as follows:

i.     The version control will be ensured using SVN

ii.    Write access to the Controlled version only for the MR.

iii.  The Controlled copies will be deployed on Intranet. Read access will be provided to all.

2.5.1.14. In case of external documents like standards, legal rules & regulations, etc. issued to departmental heads/ concerned personnel, the control is with the MR of the organization, as per the necessary control on external documents.

2.5.1.15. Any change in the documents is brought to the attention of the Management Representative.

2.5.1.16. Management Representative reviews the required change and make the necessary change and revising the revision number of the document and gets approved as per Annexure 1.

2.5.1.17. The Old copies of the document are retrieved back from the concerned department and identified as ''Obsolete Copy'' to prevent its unintended use and new copies are issued to them.

2.5.1.18. The Management Representative maintains the Master List of Documents, which summarizes the number of documents maintained with the current Version status.

2.5.1.19. The Management Representative ensures that all the documents of external origin are under control as discussed above. MR updates External Origin Documents in, which summarizes the number of external documents inside the organization. He will check the revision status of external origin documents with the source once in a year.

2.5.1.20. The Documents are made available on the Server (Common Location) as a PDF File and issued to all Employees for reference only. Master Copy of ISMS documents are maintained by MR and MR has been provided with full access (Read, Write and Delete mode). Users will view the PDF File only.

2.5.1.21. Documents are controlled and distributed by <Document distribution Software>.

2.5.1.22. Document will be in one of the following states:

i.     Draft: An unapproved document will be in "Draft" state.

ii.    Controlled: An approved document will be in "Controlled" state.

iii.  Un-Controlled: An approved document that is issued to an external entity will not be controlled, the printed copies are, by default, "Uncontrolled" unless they are stamped as "Master" or "Controlled copy

iv.  Superseded: Whenever a new version of the document is approved and released, the old version will be in "Superseded" state.

2.5.1.23. If the documents are retained beyond retention period, the same will be identified as ''REFERENCE COPY'' and written by pen on the top the documents, A Watermark of ''REFERENCE COPY'', shall be placed on the softcopy of the document.

2.5.2.         Control on documented information

2.5.2.1.    Documented Information, are those that evidence the occurrence of certain activities / events - and are special type of documents, which are established and maintained to provide evidence of conformity to requirements and the effective operation of the ISMS.

2.5.2.2.    The documented information is arranged accordingly as date-wise or project-wise or customer wise or number wise, Machine number wise, documented information are also identified by a title and number as assigned by Organization.

2.5.2.3.    Documented information of certain processes is identified only with Title Description and no Record number is assigned to it.

2.5.2.4.    ISMS documented information related to legal or regulatory requirements and contractual obligations are maintained as classified documents and retained by MD or his nominee only.

2.5.2.5.    All the documented information are maintained against any damages and ensured the information are not lost.

2.5.2.6.    The concerned HOD and MR ensure documented information remain legible, readily identifiable and retrievable.

2.5.2.7.    The documents related to Projects and ISMS Documents are taken back up on the Server / External Hard Disk. Also, backup is taken on Server / External Hard Disk or DVD's and stored in Offsite Location.

2.5.2.8.    All the documented information is stored at appropriate places so that the retrieval is very fast.

2.5.2.9.    All the documented information is retained as per the defined retention period and disposed of appropriately after the retention period. If retained for any purpose, the same shall be identified as ''REFERENCE COPY''.

2.5.2.10. The retention period of the ISMS documented information are mentioned in the documented information List.

2.5.2.11. Documented information kept for the performance of the process and of all occurrences of significant security.

2.5.2.12. All the documented information will be destroyed after the retention period by the means of ordinary disposal method such as tearing/ shredding, and archiving - in case of softcopy.

2.5.3.         Conducting Internal Audit

2.5.3.1.    The Organization circulates the entire ISMS documents as PDF Version on the Server and accessibility to the documents are provided by MR.

2.5.3.2.    Internal audit will be conducted as per Annual Audit plan - once every 6months, to comprehensively cover the Annual Audit Plan the following is considered.

i.     Status and importance of the process to be audited, based on risk assessments

ii.    Areas to be audited

iii.  Previous Audit results

iv.  Performance of the Organization with respect to the implementation of ISMS.

2.5.3.3.    Internal Audit schedule will be released based on the following: -

i.     Importance of the area

ii.    Repetition of nonconformance's/ Security incidents

iii.  Occurrence for more number of nonconformity

iv.  Annual audit plan

2.5.3.4.    Trained auditors will be assigned who are independent of the work being performed in the audit area.

2.5.3.5.    In case of urgent need or non-availability of Trained Internal Auditors, External support like Consultants will be used for conducting the Internal Audit.

2.5.3.6.    The identified Personnel will undergo a detailed Training program for conducting Internal Audit from External Training Agency and the trained personnel will conduct one cycle of Internal Audit to get qualified as effective Internal Auditor. Auditors will not audit their own work.

2.5.3.7.    Audit observations are recorded in the Audit Observation sheet and handed over to respective Auditee/MR.

2.5.3.8.    The findings are classified as Compliance (C), Noncompliance (NC) and Opportunity for Improvement (OI).

2.5.3.9.    Audit non-conformances will be recorded in the Corrective action report and brought to the attention of the Auditee.

2.5.3.10. Auditee decides the Correction, Corrective Action to be taken as planned such that the non-conformances does not occur or repeat again.

2.5.3.11. Follow up audit to verify implementation & effectiveness of Corrective actions, will be organized on or after the target date by the MR.

2.5.3.12. The closed audit reports shall be given to MR.

2.5.3.13. Documented information of the Audits and their results will be presented to the Management Review and maintained by MR.

2.5.4.         Management Review

2.5.4.1.    The Management Review is conducted to review the continuing suitability, adequacy and effectiveness of the established ISMS.

2.5.4.2.    MR arranges for the Management Review once in 6 months.

2.5.4.3.    The members for the Management Review are identified, in the agenda of Management review meeting circulated by the MR, one week prior to the MRM.

2.5.4.4.    The MR communicates to the concerned persons in advance for preparing and submitting their performance in the review meeting.

2.5.4.5.    The Management Representative prepares the agenda for the review, which includes:

i.     the status of actions from previous management reviews;

ii.    changes in external and internal issues that are relevant to the information security management system;

iii.  feedback on the information security performance, including trends in;

iv.  nonconformities and corrective actions;

v.   monitoring and measurement results;

o  Number of IT tickets/complaints raised Vs closed (x axis - months)

o  Number of incidents reported Vs closed

o  Number of ISMS event reported Vs closed

o  Number of Virus/malware/spyware

o  Updates of patches received Vs Completed (classification vice: Microsoft, firmware, server, network gears, etc.)

o  Capacity Plan

o  Change Configurations

o  Disciplinary Process Data

o  Vulnerability Performance data

o  BCP performance data

o  SLA performance of Suppliers (supplier evaluation)

o  Number of registered users

vi.  audit results;

vii. fulfillment of information security objectives;

viii.          feedback from interested parties;

ix.  results of risk assessment and status of risk treatment plan; and

x.   opportunities for continual improvement.

2.5.4.6.    The conclusions from the Management Review shall be focused on decisions related to continual improvement opportunities and any needs for changes to the information security management system.

2.5.4.7.    Minutes of meeting cum action plan shall be circulated to all the people attended the meeting.

2.5.5.         Control of Non-Conformity and taking corrective action

2.5.5.1.    Any Security Breach, or event that can Impact on compromising the Confidentiality, Availability and Integrity of the Information from the defined system, is considered as a Non-Conformity.

2.5.5.2.    Non-Conformity can be reported by Internal Employee's, Interested Party, External Personnel visiting organization and Customer.

2.5.5.3.    For the non-conformance including complaints reported by the customer, necessary actions will be initiated and informed accordingly to the Customer.

2.5.5.4.    The MR and respective Departmental Heads analyses the nature of the error/problem and decides the disposition action (correction).

2.5.5.5.    A root cause analysis, shall be done based on the Incident occurred, that lead to Non-Conformity of the ISMS, and Respective Departments shall maintain documented information of such corrective actions.

2.5.5.6.    Corrective actions are taken to prevent reoccurrence of problems related to ISMS requirements.

2.5.5.7.    The concerned personnel responsible to take corrective action will analyze the problem to identify the root cause or potential causes. The findings of the investigation will be recorded in the corrective action form. The solution to the root cause is identified and recorded.

2.5.5.8.    The initiator of the CA will track the corrective action implementation, recording results of action taken through verification process, and review the effectiveness of corrective action taken.

2.5.5.9.    The function head / MR / internal auditors verify the implemented corrective action for effectiveness of the status of the corrective action and are reviewed in the management review meeting.
