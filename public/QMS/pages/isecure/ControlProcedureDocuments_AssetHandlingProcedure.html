Asset Handling Procedure
ISMS Clause / Control: A 8.2.3 ICPr2 Version: 1.00 Date: 10th April, 2017

2.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

2.4. Procedure

2.4.1. General

The procedure for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

2.4.2. Asset Handling

2.4.2.1. All Assets associated with information and information processing facilities must be identified.

2.4.2.2. An inventory of these assets must be drawn up and maintained.

2.4.2.3. The Asset Inventory is managed by

  • General Admin for Asset ownership owned by General Admin (NON-IT assets)
  • System Admin Team for All IT assets managed by the system admin team
  • Project team for assets managed by the Project Team

2.4.2.4. Inventory details must at least include:

  • identification System
  • type of asset, including specification of make/model/format, creation/manufacture date, and any other information necessary to specify type;
  • quantity
  • assigned owner;
  • location (logical or physical location, range of physical locations if portable);
  • backup information (if applicable)
  • license information (if applicable)
  • protection
  • access Level
  • maintenance / review system
  • any additional data necessary to allow recovery from a disaster or otherwise assure continuity of operations.

2.4.2.5. Asset types subject to this control may include, depending on organizational requirements:

  • information in databases or data files, systems documentation, contracts or agreements, research information, user manuals, training materials, operational or support procedures;
  • software assets, including application and system software, development tools and utilities;
  • physical assets, including computer and communications equipment, fixed location and removable storage media;
  • services, including general utilities like HVAC, lighting and power supply;
  • IPR, Patent Rights, Program Source codes, Hardware integrated products, Data, Software Products;
  • Hard disk, Pen Drives, Terminals, etc
  • Employee, Employee Information, etc

2.4.2.6. The asset inventory should be accurate, up to date, and consistent and aligned with other inventories.

2.4.2.7. For each of the identified assets, ownership of the asset should be assigned and the classification should be identified, correlating to the Information valuation system established in the organization.

2.4.2.8. Individuals as well as other entities having approved management responsibility for the asset lifecycle qualify to be assigned as asset owners.

2.4.2.9. The asset owner should:

  • ensure that assets are inventoried;
  • ensure that assets are appropriately classified and protected;
  • define and periodically review access restrictions and classifications to important assets, taking into account applicable access control policies;
  • ensure proper handling when the asset is deleted or destroyed.

2.4.2.10. Ownership should be assigned when assets are created or when assets are transferred to the organization.

2.4.2.11. The asset owner should be responsible for the proper management of an asset over the whole asset lifecycle.

2.4.2.12. The identified owner does not necessarily have any property rights to the asset.

2.4.2.13. Rules for the acceptable use of information and of assets associated with information and information processing facilities should be identified, documented and implemented.

2.4.2.14. Employees and external party users using or having access to the organization's assets should be made aware of the information security requirements of the organization's assets associated with information and information processing facilities and resources.

2.4.2.15. All employees and external party users should return all of the organizational assets in their possession upon termination of their employment, contract or agreement.

2.4.2.16. The termination process should be formalized to include the return of all previously issued physical and electronic assets owned by or entrusted to the organization

2.4.2.17. During the notice period of termination, the organization should control unauthorized copying of relevant information (e.g. intellectual property) with the terminated employees and contractors.

2.4.2.18. Handling of all such assets are based on:

  • access restrictions supporting the protection requirements for each level of classification;
  • maintenance of a formal record of the authorized recipients of assets;
  • protection of temporary or permanent copies of information to a level consistent with the protection of the original information;
  • storage of IT assets in accordance with manufacturers' specifications;
  • clear marking of all copies of media for the attention of the authorized recipient;

2.4.2.19. The Media Handling procedure should be established in the organization.

2.4.2.20. All physical assets of the organization should have asset tagging, that can include information of:

  • Asset Tag
  • Name of the Asset
  • Location
  • Year or procurement / Installation
  • Asset ID
  • Asset Owner
  • Risk Owner
  • Asset Classification, based on Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability evaluations
  • Year of Procurement of Asset

2.4.2.21. All Assets are procured through the Purchase team of the organization as per the purchase policy established in the organization system.

2.4.2.22. When IT assets are procured, then system admin approval is required for all such systems procurement.

2.4.2.23. Once a physical asset is procured, it is verified and accepted as per the organization's quality management system practices and Asset is entered in the respective asset register corresponding with the managed department.

2.4.2.24. After entry in the asset register, by relevant asset accountable persons, the asset is issued to process user.

2.4.2.25. Any Maintenance on the asset is recorded in History card of the corresponding asset.

2.4.2.26. All assets lost, stolen or damaged must be reported to the ISMS core team for proper channelization of information .

2.4.2.27. Based on the risk assessment, if necessary, insurance on the assets are taken to mitigate the associated risks.

2.4.2.28. Disposal of all assets, are possible only when the Disposal of asset note is signed by any of the MR.

2.4.2.29. All disposals of assets are to be done in compliance to the organization's process.
