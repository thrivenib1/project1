Risk Assessment and Treatment Procedure

ISMS Clause: 6.1.2; 6.1.3

SOP1

Version: 1.00

Date: 10th April, 2017

 

1.0   Purpose

This procedure is to ensure the risks to information, information processing systems and critical assets maintained by the organization are identified and controlled or mitigated in the Organization.

1.1. Scope

This process is applicable to all departments where Information which is printed, written, stored electronically, transmitted by post, email and also where assets used in the organization for its processes requires the preservation of confidentiality, integrity and availability.

1.2. Terms and Definition

IS01_03_T&D

1.3. Abbreviations

RARTP - Risk Assessment and Risk Treatment Plan

1.4. Responsibilities

1.4.1.    Primary Responsibilities: ISMS MR, ISMS Core team

1.4.2.    Contributory Responsibilities: All Users

1.5. Process

1.5.1.    Asset or Information identification

1.5.1.1.  Each department wise the process owners or the process users, should identify the asset or the information that is available at the department.

1.5.1.2.  If the asset is subjected to any legal requirements, where risk to the asset could lead to legal issues, should be identified.

1.5.1.3.  If the asset is subjected to any contractual requirements, where risk to the asset could lead to contractual obligations fulfillment, should be identified.

1.5.1.4.  If applicable, the location where the asset is residing should be identified.

1.5.1.5.  Based on the above factors, he asset owner must be identified for each asset.

1.5.2.    Risk Identification

1.5.2.1.  For each asset identified, by the persons carrying out the risk assessment, all the existing and potential threats subjected to those assets must be identified.

1.5.2.2.  For the identified threats, the associated vulnerabilities in the organization system, processes or practices must be identified.

1.5.2.3.  The risk owner for threats / vulnerabilities associated the asset must be identified.

1.5.3.    Business Impact Assessment

1.5.3.1.  The business impact that can be the consequence of the result due to the threat on the asset must be identified.

1.5.3.2.  Business impact severity classification must be done, and identified for each business impact

1.5.3.3.  The following principles are followed for the business impact assessment

i.             3 - When the risk in the business impact leads to Government hassles, or Halt to business,

ii.           2 - When the risk in the business impact leads to Reputation lost, associated with external factor but can be corrected over a period,

iii.          1 - When the risk in the business impact leads to Internal issues only, can be resolved Company Internal.

1.5.4.    Asset Valuation and risk identification and analysis

1.5.4.1.  Based on the confidentiality, Integrity and availability of the asset, the assets must be evaluated in the company and the asset valuation must be determined.

1.5.4.2.  The Organization follows the following description for its asset valuation, based on the principle of the risk subjected to the asset.

iv.          Confidentiality Valuation

o   3 - Entity disclosed to very limited personnel with high level of access control (Individual / Department internal).

o   2 - Entity set disclosed / shared with more number of people with access control (Company Internal).

o   1 - Entity set disclosed / shared with people without access control (publicly available).

v.           Integrity Valuation

o   3 - Accuracy and precision of data very high and if tampered cannot be corrected without impact.

o   2 - Accuracy and precision of data high and if tampered can be corrected with / without impact.

o   1 - Accuracy and precision of data required and if tampered can be corrected with impact / impact has no issue.

vi.          Availability Valuation

o   3 - Very High Impact to business if unauthorized access / lost and non-compensated/ non-recoverable over a period.

o   2 - Moderate Impact to business if unauthorized access / lost and can be compensated / recoverable over a period.

o   1 - No Impact business if unauthorized access / lost and need not be compensated / recovered over a period.

1.5.4.3.  Threat description must be identified as applicable to the subject under assessment.

1.5.4.4.  Likelihood Occurrence Rating must be identified as applicable to the identified subject.

vii.         1 - Low - Once in qtr. / more than a qtr.

viii.       2 - Medium - Atleast Once in a month's.

ix.          3 - High - Atleast Once in fortnight.

1.5.4.5.  Vulnerability Description must be identified as applicable to the subject under assessment.

1.5.4.6.  Existing Control (if any) to effectively address the vulnerabilities identified applicable to the subject under assessment must be defined.

1.5.4.7.  Ability to Occur Rating must be identified as applicable to the identified subject, considering the existing controls, on the basis of:

x.           1 - A: Difficult / requires technical competence / Extreme planning.

xi.          2 - B: Willful / intentional,

xii.         3 - C: Easy.

1.5.4.8.  Severity of outcome rating must be identified as applicable to the identified subject, based on:

xiii.       3 - Govt hassles, or Halt to business.

xiv.       2 - Reputation lost, associated with external factor but can be corrected over a period.

xv.        1 - Internal issues only, can be resolved Company Internal.

1.5.4.9.  Risk Level Calculation is a product of the ratings of Confidentiality* Integrity * Availability* Likelihood of occurrence * Ability to occur* Severity of outcome.

1.5.5.    Risk evaluation and acceptance

1.5.5.1.  The organization is defined the following criteria for risk evaluation

xvi.       Any Risk, that has a risk level below 32, specific control need not be applied, as it is retained risk

xvii.      Any Risk, above 32, the risk must be controlled and communicated to the risk owner, and the risk is acceptable but risk must be monitored as residual risk.

1.5.5.2.  All Risk above 32, must be communicated to risk owner and obtain the risk owner's approval for the same.

1.5.5.3.  Assess the Risk Post application of control

1.5.5.4.  Asses the new or modified Impact on Business, because of control being applied on the risk.

1.5.5.5.  Risk assessments are made to reevaluate after one month of initial risk identification to verify the effectiveness of control and measurement of residual risks.

1.5.5.6.  All residual risks must be communicated to the risk owner, and obtain their approval.

1.5.5.7.  Email, approval of the residual risk from the risk owner is acceptable in the organization.

1.5.6.    Risk Treatment

1.5.6.1.  For all risks, based on the risk criteria established in the organization risk treatment must be carried out.

1.5.6.2.  Annexure ''A'' of ISO 27001:2013, can be used as a basis for selection of control required for the treatment of risk.

1.5.6.3.  The options of treating the risk followed in the organization are:

xviii.     Terminate: re modify the process / system in the organization to terminate the possibility of the threat exploiting the vulnerability of the asset.

xix.       Transfer: Share the exposure, either totally or in part, with a partner or agency or contractor, or through insurance to transfer risk consequence due to the possibility of the threat exploiting the vulnerability of the asset.

xx.        Treat: introduce preventative actions to reduce the probability or impact if the risk occurs due to the possibility of the threat exploiting the vulnerability of the asset.

xxi.       Tolerate: The ability of an effective action against some risks may be limited or the cost of taking such action may be disproportionate to the potential benefits gained, hence tolerate the risk due to the possibility of the threat exploiting the vulnerability of the asset, but with an application of the most possible control over it.

1.5.7.    Review of risk assessments

1.5.7.1.  Upon occurrence of any information security risk assessment, the security incident needs to be addressed as required, and the risk assessment studies must be reviewed for risk evaluation.

1.5.7.2.  Irrespective of occurrence of any incident the ISMS Core team of the organization, must carry out or review the risk assessment studies once every 6 months once.

1.5.7.3.  Upon process modification or change to existing systems of the organization, risk assessment must be carried out and then the process modification or changes to the process to be carried out.
