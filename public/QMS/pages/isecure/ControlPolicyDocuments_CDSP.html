Clear Desk and Screen Policy
ISMS Clause / Control: A 11.2.10 ICPo10 Version: 1.00 Date: 10th April, 2017

10.0 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this documented information is to provide an overview of the various policies. The required policies to be adhered in all processes preserve the Confidentiality, Integrity and Availability of the information system of the organization. Applicable to all activities, personnel performing the activities, personnel responsible for the activity to be performed in the defined manner covering the scope of activities at organization.

10.4.1. General

The policy for information security defined below has been approved by management for publication and communication to employees along with relevant external parties.

10.4.2. Clear Desk and Screen Policy

10.4.2.1. An exemption to this policy is allowed, where there is a business need to be exempted from this policy (since it is too costly, too complex, adversely impacting other business requirements) a risk assessment must be conducted being authorized by ISMS MR.

10.4.3. Clear Desk

10.4.3.1. Where practically possible, paper and computer media must be stored in suitable locked safes, cabinets or other forms of secured furniture when not in use, especially outside working hours.

10.4.3.2. Where lockable safes, filing cabinets, drawers, cupboards etc are not available, office / room doors must be locked if left unattended.

10.4.3.3. At the end of each session all sensitive information must be removed from the work place and stored in a locked area.

10.4.3.4. Confidential sensitive or classified information, when printed, must be cleared from printers immediately.

10.4.3.5. Lock all office areas when they are not in use.

10.4.3.6. All writing boards in desk's and conference halls must be wiped before leaving the work station or area.

10.4.3.7. No company confidential, project confidential or personal confidential information is allowed to be left on desk / whiteboards.

10.4.4. Clear Screen

10.4.4.1. No information files must be left on desktop screen of any system / machines.

10.4.4.2. No information files must be stored in C-drive / System configuration drive of any machine.

10.4.4.3. Computers / computer terminals must not be left logged on when unattended and must be password protected.

10.4.4.4. Computer screens must be angled away from the view of unauthorized persons

10.4.4.5. The Windows Security Lock must be set to activate when there is no activity for a short pre-determined period of time.

10.4.4.6. The Windows Security Lock must be password protected for reactivation

10.4.4.7. Users must log off or lock their machines when they leave the room.
